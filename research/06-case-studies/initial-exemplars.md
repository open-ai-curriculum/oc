# Initial Exemplars and Comparative Notes

## Purpose

This document captures an initial set of curriculum initiatives relevant to this repository's goals.

It does not claim that all examples are equivalent. The point is to identify useful patterns across:

- fully open OER models
- free/open-access models
- nonprofit distribution models with varying reuse permissions

## Exemplar 1: Open Up Resources

### What is verified

- Open Up Resources identifies itself as a nonprofit curriculum publisher serving PK-12 with openly accessible curricula, professional learning, and partnerships.
- The organization reports serving more than 3.6 million students.
- Open Up Resources released openly licensed curriculum, including Illustrative Mathematics 6-8 Math under CC BY.

### Why it matters

This is a strong example of an open curriculum effort that pairs:

- openly available materials
- implementation support
- professional learning
- scale-oriented distribution

### Relevant lesson for this repository

Open content alone is not the full operating model. Professional learning, implementation support, and partnerships are part of how curriculum succeeds in practice.

## Exemplar 2: Core Knowledge Foundation

### What is verified

- Core Knowledge provides open access to content-rich curriculum materials for Preschool through Grade 8.
- Many materials are available for free download.
- The site states materials may be used, adapted, and shared with attribution, while restricting sale of the original or adapted program.

### Why it matters

This is a useful example of broad free access and practical dissemination at scale, even though its terms are more constrained than a public-domain approach.

### Relevant lesson for this repository

There is a meaningful distinction between:

- free access
- adaptable open licensing
- no-restrictions public-domain release

The repository should stay precise about which openness model it is pursuing.

## Exemplar 3: CK-12

### What is verified

- CK-12 describes a mission centered on open educational resources for K-12.
- Its mission materials emphasize content that can be mixed, modified, and printed to suit teacher needs while aligning to curriculum standards.
- The model emphasizes customization and standards alignment.

### Why it matters

CK-12 is a strong exemplar of modularity, customization, and multi-format resource strategy.

### Relevant lesson for this repository

Adaptability and printability are not secondary conveniences. They are central to real-world reuse.

## Comparative Takeaways

Across these exemplars, several consistent patterns appear:

1. Successful open or open-adjacent curriculum initiatives do more than publish files.
2. Standards alignment, teacher usability, and implementation support matter as much as availability.
3. Licensing clarity matters because "free," "open," and "public domain" are not interchangeable.
4. Scaled adoption depends on distribution and operational support, not only content quality.

## Limits

It does not yet include:

- deeper implementation-outcome analysis
