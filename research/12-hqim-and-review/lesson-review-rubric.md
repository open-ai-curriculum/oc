# Lesson Review Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric is used to review an individual lesson before release or inclusion in a larger unit.

It is narrower than the unit rubric and focuses on whether the lesson itself is teachable, aligned, accessible, and instructionally sound.

## Scoring Scale

For each dimension, assign one rating:

- `meets expectations`
- `partially meets expectations`
- `does not meet expectations`

## Release Rule

A lesson should not be approved if it:

- does not meet expectations in objective and alignment
- does not meet expectations in instructional coherence
- does not meet expectations in accessibility and inclusion
- does not meet expectations in knowledge integrity

If the lesson includes assessment, it also must not:

- does not meet expectations in assessment quality

## Rubric Dimensions

### 1. Objective and Alignment

Review questions:

- Is the lesson objective clear?
- Is the objective aligned to the stated standard or unit goal?
- Do the activities actually support that objective?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- objective is explicit
- the lesson focuses on the intended learning
- alignment is visible rather than implied

### 2. Instructional Coherence

Review questions:

- Does the lesson have a clear beginning, middle, and end?
- Do tasks build toward the intended learning?
- Is the pacing plausible?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- the sequence makes sense
- transitions and task flow are clear
- students are not asked to do disconnected work

### 3. Rigor and Student Thinking

Review questions:

- Does the lesson ask students to think at the intended level?
- Are students doing meaningful cognitive work?
- Are supports helpful without replacing the core thinking?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- the lesson demands more than completion or recall when higher rigor is intended
- scaffolds support access without collapsing complexity

### 4. Assessment and Evidence of Learning

Review questions:

- Is there a clear way to tell whether students met the lesson objective?
- Are checks for understanding aligned to the objective?
- If graded, is the evidence meaningful?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- checks for understanding are purposeful
- exit tasks, prompts, or products reveal actual learning

### 5. Teacher Usability

Review questions:

- Can a teacher enact the lesson without major guesswork?
- Are instructions, materials, and timing clear?
- Are discussion prompts or facilitation notes usable?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- lesson steps are understandable
- required materials are visible
- the teacher can see how to run the lesson

### 6. Accessibility and Inclusion

Review questions:

- Does the lesson reduce avoidable access barriers?
- Are instructions and formats usable for diverse learners?
- Are examples, names, contexts, and framing inclusive?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- essential content is accessible
- learners are not unnecessarily excluded by format or framing

### 7. Knowledge Integrity

Review questions:

- Are the core explanations and examples accurate?
- Are there any invented claims or misleading simplifications?
- Is the lesson safe and responsible for the intended learners?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- no material inaccuracies
- no fabricated evidence or references
- no irresponsible framing for sensitive material

## Reviewer Summary

Record:

- lesson title
- reviewer
- date
- rating for each dimension
- release decision
- required revisions

## Decision Outcomes

- `approved`
- `approved with revisions`
- `return to draft`

## Confidence

Medium-high confidence.
