# Unit Review Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric is used to review a curriculum unit before release or wider reuse.

It should be used with:

- `hqim-review-framework.md`
- accessibility review checklist
- AI human review policy when AI assistance was used

## Scoring Scale

For each dimension, assign one rating:

- `meets expectations`
- `partially meets expectations`
- `does not meet expectations`

## Release Rule

A unit should not be approved if it:

- does not meet expectations in standards alignment
- does not meet expectations in coherence
- does not meet expectations in accessibility and inclusion
- does not meet expectations in knowledge integrity

If the unit includes major assessment components, it also must not:

- does not meet expectations in assessment quality

## Rubric Dimensions

### 1. Standards Alignment

Review questions:

- Are target standards identified clearly?
- Do the major learning experiences align to those standards?
- Is the level of rigor appropriate?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- standards are explicit
- unit goals align with standards
- tasks and outcomes reflect the intended rigor

### 2. Unit Coherence

Review questions:

- Does the unit build toward clear understandings or competencies?
- Do lessons connect logically?
- Is there a visible progression of knowledge and skill?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- lessons form a purposeful sequence
- activities are not disconnected
- the unit builds toward a clear culminating understanding or performance

### 3. Rigor and Grade-Level Fit

Review questions:

- Is the intellectual demand appropriate for the grade band?
- Are supports helpful without watering down the goal?
- Does the unit avoid busywork or superficial coverage?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- grade-level work remains central
- scaffolds support access without replacing rigor
- tasks require real thinking or application

### 4. Assessment Quality

Review questions:

- Does the unit include evidence of learning that matches the goals?
- Are success criteria clear?
- Can the assessment distinguish between shallow completion and understanding?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- assessment is aligned to goals
- performance expectations are visible
- tasks elicit meaningful evidence of learning

### 5. Teacher Usability

Review questions:

- Can a teacher understand how to enact the unit?
- Are materials, pacing, and facilitation expectations clear?
- Are likely misconceptions or support needs anticipated?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- teaching moves are clear
- required materials and preparation are visible
- teacher guidance reduces guesswork

### 6. Accessibility and Inclusion

Review questions:

- Does the unit reduce avoidable barriers?
- Are materials accessible across common modes of use?
- Are examples and contexts inclusive and respectful?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- accessibility considerations are built in
- essential content is not locked behind one modality
- examples avoid obvious exclusionary framing

### 7. Knowledge Integrity

Review questions:

- Is the content accurate?
- Are examples, references, and claims trustworthy?
- Are sensitive topics handled responsibly?

Evidence of meeting expectations:

- no clear factual errors
- no fabricated evidence
- representations are responsible and appropriate

## Reviewer Summary

Record:

- unit title
- reviewer
- date
- rating for each dimension
- release decision
- required revisions

## Decision Outcomes

- `approved`
- `approved with revisions`
- `return to draft`

## Confidence

Medium-high confidence.
