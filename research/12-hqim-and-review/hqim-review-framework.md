# HQIM Review Framework for Open Curriculum

## Purpose

This document defines a practical framework for evaluating whether curriculum materials in this repository meet the standard of high-quality instructional materials.

The goal is to prevent the project from scaling content generation before it has a clear definition of quality.

## Core Position

A curriculum resource is not high quality simply because it is:

- standards-tagged
- well formatted
- engaging on the surface
- AI-generated quickly

High-quality instructional materials should demonstrate coherence, rigor, usability, accessibility, and instructional integrity.

## Review Dimensions

### 1. Standards Alignment

Questions:

- Are the learning goals clear?
- Does the material actually address the claimed standards?
- Is the level of rigor appropriate to the standards?

Minimum expectation:

- standards alignment is explicit and plausible, not decorative tagging

### 2. Coherence

Questions:

- Do lessons build logically within the unit?
- Do tasks support the intended understanding?
- Are knowledge and skill demands sequenced meaningfully?

Minimum expectation:

- activities, examples, and assessment form a coherent learning arc

### 3. Rigor and Grade-Level Appropriateness

Questions:

- Are students asked to do work at the intended level of complexity?
- Are supports present without diluting the core demand?
- Does the material avoid both oversimplification and unnecessary complexity?

Minimum expectation:

- the resource reflects grade-level expectations while providing usable scaffolds

### 4. Assessment Quality

Questions:

- Do assessments measure the intended learning?
- Are success criteria clear?
- Can the material distinguish between surface completion and genuine understanding?

Minimum expectation:

- assessment is aligned, usable, and not trivially gameable

### 5. Teacher Usability

Questions:

- Can a teacher understand what to do without guesswork?
- Are objectives, materials, timing, and instructions clear?
- Are likely misconceptions or facilitation needs addressed?

Minimum expectation:

- the material is not only educationally sound, but also teachable

### 6. Accessibility and Inclusion

Questions:

- Does the material reduce avoidable barriers?
- Are examples and framing inclusive?
- Is accessibility built into the material, not bolted on afterward?

Minimum expectation:

- the material can be used by diverse learners without obvious exclusionary barriers

### 7. Knowledge Integrity

Questions:

- Are facts, examples, and representations accurate?
- Are sensitive topics handled responsibly?
- Are citations or evidence claims real when included?

Minimum expectation:

- the material is trustworthy enough to enter classroom use

## Review Process

Each substantial curriculum artifact should pass three layers of review:

### Layer 1: Threshold review

Confirm that the artifact is complete enough to evaluate.

Reject early if:

- objectives are missing
- standards claims are unclear
- content is obviously incomplete
- citations are fabricated

### Layer 2: Quality review

Evaluate the artifact across all review dimensions in this framework.

### Layer 3: Release decision

Decide:

- approved
- approved with revisions
- return to draft

## Recommended Scoring Model

Use a simple three-level scoring model for each dimension:

- `meets expectations`
- `partially meets expectations`
- `does not meet expectations`

A material should not be approved if it:

- does not meet expectations in standards alignment
- does not meet expectations in assessment quality when assessment is included
- does not meet expectations in accessibility and inclusion
- does not meet expectations in knowledge integrity

## Immediate Review Heuristics

Before the repository has full rubrics, reviewers should ask:

1. Would a teacher know what students are supposed to learn?
2. Would the tasks plausibly lead to that learning?
3. Would the assessment reveal whether students actually learned it?
4. Would diverse learners face avoidable barriers here?
5. Is the content accurate and fit for classroom use?

## Relationship to Existing Field Practice

This framework is informed by the broader HQIM movement and materials-review practice that emphasize:

- standards alignment
- coherence
- teacher support
- rigorous task design
- transparent review criteria

The repository should continue to refine this framework using more content-specific rubrics over time.
