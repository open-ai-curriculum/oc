# Mathematics Foundation

## Purpose

This document identifies core design considerations for mathematics curriculum development in the repository.

## Key Principles

- maintain grade-level rigor
- build conceptual understanding, procedural fluency, and application together
- sequence content coherently over time
- use representations intentionally
- align assessment to actual mathematical reasoning, not only answer production

## Relevant Standards and Source Families

- state math standards
- Common Core mathematics where applicable
- What Works Clearinghouse math practice guides
- HQIM-oriented math review guidance

## Design Implications for This Repository

- math lessons should expose reasoning, not only final answers
- worked examples must be carefully checked for correctness
- AI-generated math content requires especially strong accuracy review
- assessments should reduce the chance of shallow answer-only completion

## Common Risks

- incorrect examples or solutions
- standards-tagging without real rigor alignment
- over-scaffolded tasks that erase grade-level demand
- assessments that reward answer retrieval rather than mathematical thinking

## Confidence

Medium-high confidence.
