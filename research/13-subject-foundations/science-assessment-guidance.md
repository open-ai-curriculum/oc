# Science Assessment Guidance

## Purpose

This document defines science-specific assessment expectations for curriculum developed in the repository.

## Core Position

Science assessment should gather evidence of scientific understanding and reasoning, not only terminology recognition.

## Assessment Priorities

### 1. Align to the science claim

Each task should make clear whether it is assessing:

- conceptual understanding
- explanation of phenomena
- evidence use
- modeling
- argumentation

### 2. Use evidence-rich prompts

Science assessment is stronger when students are asked to:

- interpret data
- explain observations
- justify a claim with evidence
- analyze or revise a model

### 3. Avoid vocabulary-only proxies

Knowing terms does not by itself demonstrate scientific understanding.

### 4. Review visuals and procedural accessibility

Science assessments often depend on:

- diagrams
- tables
- graphs
- lab or investigation descriptions

These need accessibility review in addition to content review.

## Review Questions

Before release, reviewers should ask:

1. What scientific understanding is the task meant to reveal?
2. Does the task require explanation, interpretation, or evidence use where appropriate?
3. Are visuals, graphs, or procedures accessible and accurate?
4. Could students succeed through memorized language without real understanding?

## Common Risks

- assessments that overreward vocabulary recall
- inaccessible diagrams or data displays
- AI-generated scientific distractors or explanations that are inaccurate
- tasks that appear inquiry-based but do not actually reveal reasoning
