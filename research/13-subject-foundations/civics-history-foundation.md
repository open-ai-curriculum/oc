# Civics and History Foundation

## Purpose

This document identifies core design considerations for civics and history curriculum development in the repository.

## Key Principles

- prioritize inquiry, evidence, context, and interpretation
- handle contested or sensitive material with explicit review discipline
- support multiple perspectives without collapsing into false equivalence
- connect civic knowledge with participation, reasoning, and historical understanding

## Relevant Standards and Source Families

- state social studies standards
- Educating for American Democracy
- civics and history inquiry frameworks

## Design Implications for This Repository

- source selection and framing require careful review
- AI-generated civics/history content needs heightened bias and sensitivity review
- lessons should distinguish fact, interpretation, and argument
- assessments should privilege evidence use, sourcing, and reasoning

## Common Risks

- oversimplified historical narratives
- biased or exclusionary framing
- invented details or quotations
- false balance on well-established facts

## Confidence

Medium-high confidence.
