# Science Foundation

## Purpose

This document identifies core design considerations for science curriculum development in the repository.

## Key Principles

- integrate disciplinary core ideas, science and engineering practices, and crosscutting concepts where relevant
- prefer inquiry and explanation over fact listing alone
- use models, evidence, and argumentation carefully
- connect science learning to investigation and real-world phenomena

## Relevant Standards and Source Families

- NGSS and related state standards
- science education research and field frameworks
- HQIM-oriented science review guidance where available

## Design Implications for This Repository

- science units should be organized around meaningful phenomena or problems where possible
- accuracy review is critical for scientific explanations and examples
- assessments should measure explanation, modeling, and evidence use, not only vocabulary recall
- accessibility needs are especially important for diagrams, labs, and visual representations

## Common Risks

- oversimplified or incorrect explanations
- vocabulary-heavy tasks with weak conceptual depth
- inaccessible diagrams or investigation instructions
- assessments that miss scientific reasoning

## Confidence

Medium confidence.
