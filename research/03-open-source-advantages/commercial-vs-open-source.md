# Curriculum Openness, Reuse, and Platform Dependence

## Purpose

This document analyzes the practical advantages and tradeoffs of open, reusable curriculum approaches compared with more platform-dependent or proprietary approaches.

It is intentionally narrower and more disciplined than a blanket claim that one model is always superior.

## Core Position

The strongest research-supported advantages of more open and reusable curriculum models are:

- adaptability
- transparency
- reduced lock-in
- easier localization and redistribution

The current repository does not yet have enough empirical evidence to claim universal superiority across all cost, quality, or adoption outcomes.

## Practical Advantages of More Open Curriculum Models

### 1. Adaptability

Materials that are easier to edit and redistribute are generally better suited to:

- local standards variation
- educator modification
- localization and translation
- accessibility adaptation

### 2. Transparency

Reusable materials with visible structure and review processes support:

- clearer quality review
- more legible standards alignment
- easier peer feedback and improvement

### 3. Reduced Platform Dependence

When curriculum is not trapped inside one proprietary delivery system, institutions are better positioned to:

- move across tools
- preserve their work
- avoid rebuilding content after system changes

### 4. Community Improvement Potential

Where governance and contribution systems are strong, reusable curriculum ecosystems can benefit from:

- shared refinement
- educator feedback loops
- lower duplication of effort

## Important Limits

More open or reusable models do not automatically guarantee:

- lower total cost
- stronger implementation quality
- better student outcomes
- easier adoption

Those results depend on:

- teacher usability
- implementation supports
- governance
- professional learning
- technical delivery choices

## Implications for This Repository

For this project, the most defensible takeaway is:

- build curriculum that is portable, reviewable, and adaptable
- avoid unnecessary platform dependence
- avoid overstating outcome claims without stronger case evidence
