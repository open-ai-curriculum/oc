# K-12 Curriculum Development Research

This directory contains research and documentation on K-12 curriculum development, focusing on standards-based design, open education, implementation practice, assessment, and the operating requirements for an open-source curriculum project.

## Research Focus Areas

1. **Industry-Leading Frameworks**: Understanding by Design (UbD), Bloom's Taxonomy, UDL, and adjacent design frameworks
2. **Standards-Based Approaches**: Alignment with state and national standards
3. **Licensing and Openness Questions**: Repository licensing, governance, and reuse tradeoffs
4. **Implementation Strategies**: Practical guidance for curriculum development teams
5. **Assessment Methods**: Evidence-based evaluation and continuous improvement
6. **Real-World Applications**: Case studies and best practices from successful implementations

## Key Findings Summary

Our research reveals significant advantages of open-source curriculum development over commercial educational software, particularly in terms of:
- **Cost transparency and sustainability**
- **Freedom from vendor lock-in**
- **Customization and adaptability**
- **Community-driven innovation**
- **Alignment with educational goals over profit motives**

## Research Methodology

This documentation is being revised to follow a shared methodology and source discipline. The working approach includes:

- official standards and public guidance where available
- academic and consensus educational frameworks
- comparative analysis of open vs. proprietary approaches
- implementation-oriented field guidance
- case evidence where real examples are available
