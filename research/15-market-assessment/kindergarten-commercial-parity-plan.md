# Kindergarten Commercial Parity Plan

## Objective

Move the repository's Kindergarten offering from a serious commercialization prototype to a product system that can credibly meet or exceed current commercial elementary offerings.

## Planning Standard

This plan does not treat the current Kindergarten omnibus books as the primary commercial target.

The plan assumes the repository will meet or exceed commercial offerings only if it:

1. builds strong canonical subject programs first
2. reaches full-year lesson and page density in the core subjects
3. provides teacher-facing and student-facing assets at lesson scale
4. embeds multilingual, accessibility, and family supports as core product features
5. assembles omnibus books only from stronger subject-program source products

## What "Meet Or Exceed Commercial Offerings" Means

For this repository, meeting or exceeding commercial offerings means the Kindergarten system can credibly compete on:

- annual instructional completeness
- teacher usability
- student-page quality
- reproducible and classroom-material completeness
- assessment depth and calibration
- multilingual and accessibility supports
- family support
- implementation clarity
- subject-program coherence

Exceeding commercial offerings does not mean matching every publisher feature. It means outperforming the commercial baseline in the areas most aligned to this repository's constitution:

- stronger transparency of standards, review, and provenance
- better portability and modifiability of source assets
- stronger explicit accessibility and multilingual design
- tighter cross-artifact traceability from scope to lesson to assessment to support material

## Current Gap Summary

The comparative analysis at [k-1-commercial-comparative-analysis.md](/Volumes/data/development/oc/research/15-market-assessment/k-1-commercial-comparative-analysis.md) and the earlier Kindergarten gap work show that Kindergarten is ahead of Grade 1 but still below commercial parity because:

- only ELA has begun a real subject-program commercialization build
- math is still missing as a commercial-grade source program
- non-ELA subjects are still mostly pilot- or omnibus-summary-level
- student books and workbooks remain compressed and under-designed
- teacher-edition content is still lighter than commercial teacher editions
- reproducibles and assessments exist, but not yet at full-year production depth
- final text approval, rights handling, and human QA are still incomplete

## Strategic Position

The repository should pursue a two-tier Kindergarten commercialization strategy:

### Tier 1. Beat the market on source-product discipline

Build better source systems than commercial programs typically expose:

- cleaner lesson traceability
- explicit review and provenance records
- explicit multilingual and accessibility layers
- reusable spec-driven artifact architecture

### Tier 2. Reach market parity on classroom usability

Match or exceed commercial programs on:

- full-year lesson volume
- page counts
- teacher-edition depth
- reproducibles and assessments
- implementation and pacing support

The repository will only be truly competitive when both tiers are achieved.

## Parity And Exceedance Criteria

The repository should not claim Kindergarten commercial parity until all of the following are true.

### A. Core annual completeness

- ELA reaches at least 160 authored lessons
- Math reaches at least 150 authored lessons
- each content and enrichment subject has a defensible annual plan with enough authored volume to constitute real coverage
- all Kindergarten weeks are covered in a year map without major dead zones

### B. Teacher-use completeness

- core subjects have lesson-level teacher narratives throughout the year
- teachers do not need to invent missing core lesson materials
- preparation notes, pacing notes, misconceptions, intervention, and extension paths are present across the full year
- multilingual and accessibility supports are embedded at point of use

### C. Student-use completeness

- student-facing pages are actual instructional pages, not prose descriptions
- workbook and lesson-page systems have repeated, stable page architectures
- response space, visual support, and navigation are suitable for Kindergarten classroom use

### D. Assessment completeness

- ELA and Math each include three benchmark windows
- each module or unit includes formative and end-of-module evidence opportunities
- scoring guidance and exemplars exist at the level teachers need for reliable use

### E. Production completeness

- reproducibles exist as designed production files, not just text masters
- print-ready assets are page-stable
- editorial, accessibility, and subject QA have been executed on the actual production files

### F. Exceedance features

To credibly claim the repository exceeds commercial offerings, Kindergarten should also provide:

- stronger artifact traceability than typical publisher products
- explicit rights and provenance tracking for texts and assets
- reusable source formats rather than opaque packaged-only files
- clearly structured multilingual support notes at module and lesson levels
- transparent review and calibration documentation

## Major Workstreams

### Workstream 1. Finish Kindergarten ELA As The First True Commercial Subject Prototype

Purpose:

- turn the strongest current area into the first subject program that can stand on its own

Required outputs:

- complete remaining ELA modules
- complete full-year lesson inventory
- finalize text lists and rights handling
- convert teacher narratives and reproducibles into designed production files
- complete benchmark architecture
- execute specialist review

Exit criteria:

- Kindergarten ELA can stand alone as a full-year teachable subject program

### Workstream 2. Build Kindergarten Math To The Same Standard

Purpose:

- remove the single biggest commercial incompleteness in the grade-level offering

Required outputs:

- math product architecture
- 36-week scope and sequence
- 8-12 units
- 140-170 lessons
- student pages, manipulatives guidance, centers, teacher narratives, assessment system, and copy masters

Exit criteria:

- Kindergarten math can stand alone as a full-year teachable subject program

### Workstream 3. Right-Size The Content And Enrichment Subjects

Purpose:

- build enough annual depth that the grade is no longer a literacy-and-math prototype with token add-ons

Required outputs by subject family:

- science and social studies:
  - real annual module families with student pages, teacher lesson cards, and observation-based assessment
- arts, health, PE, and technology:
  - annual lesson-card systems, student prompt sheets, observation tools, and family extensions

Exit criteria:

- all subjects have justified package shapes and defensible annual coverage

### Workstream 4. Build The Kindergarten Design System

Purpose:

- close the production-fidelity gap with commercial student and teacher materials

Required outputs:

- stable page templates
- illustration and icon system
- reproducible layout rules
- teacher-edition layout conventions
- workbook page system
- print-production constraints and export guidance

Exit criteria:

- student and teacher assets can be rendered into consistent, classroom-usable designed files

### Workstream 5. Build The Full Assessment And Reporting Spine

Purpose:

- make the program usable for real classroom decision-making and reporting

Required outputs:

- benchmark windows
- module and unit checks
- observation protocols
- rubrics and scoring guides
- exemplar response libraries
- reporting and gradebook conventions

Exit criteria:

- teachers can assess and interpret performance across the Kindergarten year without inventing missing systems

### Workstream 6. Run Production QA And Adoption-Style Review

Purpose:

- ensure the repository is judged against real adoption expectations, not only internal completeness

Required outputs:

- editorial QA
- accessibility QA
- subject QA
- implementation/usability QA
- standards correlation review
- adoption-style completeness review

Exit criteria:

- the team can defend the completeness and usability of the program to a district or HQIM-style reviewer

## Sequenced Execution Plan

### Phase 1. Lock The Core Commercial Prototype

Priority:

- finish Kindergarten ELA commercialization
- start Kindergarten Math commercialization architecture immediately

Why first:

- commercial parity is impossible without both core subjects at real depth

### Phase 2. Build Annual Core-Subject Density

Priority:

- finish full-year ELA
- build full-year math through at least 75 percent of target volume
- establish benchmark windows and module/unit assessment cadence

Why second:

- this is the minimum threshold for commercial credibility in the elementary market

### Phase 3. Normalize Teacher, Student, And Reproducible Systems

Priority:

- standardize page types
- standardize teacher-edition components
- standardize reproducible and family-asset systems

Why third:

- a commercial program depends on repeatable systems, not isolated good lessons

### Phase 4. Fill The Content-Subject Depth Gap

Priority:

- science and social studies first
- arts, health, PE, and technology next

Why fourth:

- Kindergarten needs broad subject credibility, but commercial comparison is decided first by core-subject strength and overall completeness

### Phase 5. Assemble And Redesign Omnibus Products

Priority:

- rebuild omnibus books and teacher materials from stronger source products

Why fifth:

- omnibus books should reflect completed source programs, not substitute for them

### Phase 6. Execute QA And Market-Readiness Review

Priority:

- review page-stable files, not only markdown source files

Why sixth:

- parity claims must be defended against actual production assets

## Numeric Milestones

### Milestone 1. Commercial Prototype Threshold

- ELA:
  - at least 120 lessons authored
  - at least 4 modules with commercialization layers and designed production assets
- Math:
  - architecture, build targets, and first full unit family complete

### Milestone 2. Commercial Credibility Threshold

- ELA:
  - at least 75 percent of annual targets met
- Math:
  - at least 75 percent of annual targets met
- all other subjects:
  - at least 60 percent of annual targets met
- benchmark and reproducible systems working across the grade

### Milestone 3. Commercial Parity Threshold

- full-year ELA and Math complete
- all subjects represented as real annual programs
- designed student and teacher materials exist across the grade
- QA complete on page-stable production files

### Milestone 4. Commercial Exceedance Threshold

- traceability, provenance, multilingual supports, and accessibility systems are stronger and more transparent than common commercial baselines
- the repository can demonstrate advantages in flexibility, review transparency, and artifact portability without sacrificing classroom usability

## Immediate Next Moves

1. Finish the Kindergarten ELA commercialization path already underway.
2. Create the Kindergarten Math commercial product architecture and build-target package.
3. Define the Kindergarten production design system for student pages, teacher-edition pages, and reproducibles.
4. Establish a Kindergarten benchmark and reporting architecture before the grade gets larger.
5. After ELA and Math are both structurally strong, deepen science and social studies as the next most commercially visible content subjects.

## Final Judgment

Kindergarten can realistically meet or exceed commercial offerings, but not by polishing the current omnibus books in place.

It will only happen if the repository continues the current pivot toward subject-program source products, finishes ELA to full-year commercial depth, builds math to the same standard, and only then rebuilds the assembled grade-level books from those stronger sources.
