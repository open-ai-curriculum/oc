# New York Social Studies Standards

## Jurisdiction

- jurisdiction: `New York`
- subject: `Social Studies`
- source profile: `New York State K-12 Social Studies Framework and related inquiry-aligned guidance surfaces`
- repository codification status: `Drafted`

## Public Source Surface

New York publicly distributes a social studies framework surface that combines disciplinary content expectations with inquiry-oriented practice expectations and supporting guidance.

For repository purposes, the important public surfaces are:

- the K-12 social studies framework
- inquiry or practice dimensions associated with investigation and argument
- grade-band and secondary course organization cues

## Profile Summary

New York presents social studies through a framework structure that is strongly inquiry-forward. That makes it particularly relevant to the repository's shared `Q` inquiry spine and its downstream history, civics, geography, and economics branches.

For repository purposes, New York is best treated as an inquiry-centered, baseline-derived state profile.

## Relationship To Repository Social Studies Content Baseline

- relationship type: `inquiry-centered baseline-derived`
- core structure: `shared inquiry architecture layered onto disciplinary social studies content`
- notable state distinction: `New York foregrounds inquiry and concept-driven social studies framework organization more explicitly than the current repository baseline documents do`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

New York's public social studies framework is still primarily a content and inquiry organization surface. Repository performance bands for mastery, transfer, and public argument remain a separate governance layer unless a reviewed New York performance-descriptor surface is later codified.

## Grade-Band Structure

### Kindergarten Through Grade 4

Primary organizational pattern:

- grade-level social studies expectations
- community, identity, civic life, and place-focused themes
- inquiry and evidence expectations embedded in content work

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for early inquiry, community civics, chronology, geography, and evidence-talk nodes

### Grades 5 Through 8

Primary organizational pattern:

- upper-elementary and middle-grades social studies framework progression
- deeper treatment of history, geography, government, and economic systems
- stronger source evaluation and explanation expectations

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for mid-graph history, civics, geography, and economics branches
- useful for connecting `Q` inquiry nodes to disciplinary application nodes

### Grades 9 Through 12

Primary organizational pattern:

- course-band or course-specific high-school organization
- explicit inquiry, evidence, and argument demands
- stronger emphasis on public reasoning and disciplinary interpretation

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for advanced history interpretation, civic analysis, economic reasoning, and public-argument nodes
- requires course metadata when a repository node spans more than one New York high-school context

## Domain Or Strand Structure

New York's public social studies surface is most useful to the repository when modeled through these strands:

- inquiry, evidence, and argument
- history and change over time
- civics, institutions, and democratic participation
- geography, movement, and human-environment systems
- economics and decision-making

This makes New York an especially useful state profile for later standards-augmented graph work.

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `Social Studies Framework` is a meaningful organizational term and should be preserved
- inquiry is not just an adjunct skill layer; it is part of the state's structural grammar
- course-context and key-idea organization may not align one-to-one with repository node boundaries
