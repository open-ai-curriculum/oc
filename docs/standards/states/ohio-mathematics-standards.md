## Profile Summary

Ohio's public learning-standards surface defines what students should know and be able to do in pre-kindergarten through grade 12 and connects those standards to model curricula, assessments, and resources.

Ohio's mathematics public surface also prominently preserves the eight Standards for Mathematical Practice and associated progressions.

For repository purposes, Ohio is a baseline-derived profile.

## Relationship To Canonical Mathematics Content Baseline

- relationship type: `baseline-derived`
- core structure: `Ohio learning standards with strong compatibility to familiar mathematics domain progressions`
- notable state distinction: `Ohio publicly links content standards, model curricula, and standards-for-mathematical-practice progressions in a more integrated way than some states`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

Ohio explicitly states that school performance is measured in part by student progress in gaining the knowledge and skills within the learning standards. Even so, content standards alignment should remain separate from future codification of assessment-level proficiency descriptors.

## Grade-Band Structure

### Elementary And Middle Grades

Primary organizational pattern:

- grade-level learning standards
- linked mathematical practice expectations and model curricula

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for early number, operations, fractions, measurement, geometry, and data nodes
- mathematical-practice progressions may later provide useful secondary mapping metadata for reasoning and explanation-heavy nodes

### High School

Primary organizational pattern:

- high-school course standards and mathematical-practice progressions
- public standards surfaces support algebra, geometry, and advanced mathematics course references

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for algebra, functions, geometry, and statistics nodes
- some nodes may benefit from both content-standard and mathematical-practice linkage

## Domain Or Strand Structure

Ohio codification should preserve:

- grade or course
- content domain and standard identifier
- standards-for-mathematical-practice linkage where relevant
- optional model-curricula references as non-governing support metadata

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `Ohio's Learning Standards` is the governing state term
- `model curricula` are public support artifacts, not the standards themselves
- Ohio publishes `Standards for Mathematical Practice` and practice progressions as a prominent adjacent surface

## Mapping Readiness Notes

Ohio is a helpful early profile because it supports baseline-compatible content mapping while also pressuring the repository to decide how to handle mathematical-practice cross-links.

## Unresolved Review Questions
