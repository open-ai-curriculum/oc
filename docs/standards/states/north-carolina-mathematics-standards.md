## Profile Summary

North Carolina uses the Standard Course of Study (NCSCOS) as its public content-standards surface. The public mathematics surface currently exposes adopted 2017 K-8 mathematics standards and 2017-2019 high-school mathematics documents such as NC Math 1, NC Math 2, NC Math 3, NC Math 4, and additional course resources.

For repository purposes, North Carolina is a state-distinct profile. Although the underlying mathematics overlaps common multi-state content taxonomies, the state uses its own SCOS structure, high-school course model, and extensive unpacking and support resources.

## Relationship To Canonical Mathematics Content Baseline

- relationship type: `state-distinct`
- core structure: `grade-level and course-level Standard Course of Study documents with supporting unpacking and crosswalk resources`
- notable state distinction: `NC Math 1, NC Math 2, and NC Math 3 form a prominent secondary sequence that should be represented directly`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

North Carolina's EOG and related statewide testing surfaces are alignment-relevant, but the content standards profile should remain separate from any later performance-descriptor codification.

## Grade-Band Structure

### Kindergarten Through Grade 8

Primary organizational pattern:

- grade-level mathematics standards
- supporting resources such as unpacking documents and crosswalks

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for early number, operations, fractions, measurement, geometry, and data nodes
- unpacking documents may later help disambiguate fine-grained node alignment, but they should remain secondary to the governing standards surface

### High School

Primary organizational pattern:

- integrated-style course sequence centered on NC Math 1, NC Math 2, and NC Math 3
- additional courses and options charts for graduation pathways

Operational fit with repository node work:

- some high-school nodes may align better to integrated course references than to a traditional Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II sequence
- later mapping logic should support course-sensitive state variants

## Domain Or Strand Structure

North Carolina codification should preserve:

- grade or course
- standard or objective grouping
- anchor statement or identifier
- optional references to unpacking or crosswalk documents when they materially clarify interpretation

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `Standard Course of Study` and `SCOS` are governing state terms
- `NC Math 1`, `NC Math 2`, and `NC Math 3` are central high-school course references
- unpacking documents and options charts are substantial support materials but should not replace the governing standards records

## Mapping Readiness Notes

North Carolina is an important early profile because it forces the repository to support an integrated-course high-school structure rather than assuming every state uses a traditional secondary sequence.
