# Texas Social Studies Standards

## Jurisdiction

- jurisdiction: `Texas`
- subject: `Social Studies`
- source profile: `Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills for Social Studies and related TEKS organization surfaces`
- repository codification status: `Drafted`

## Public Source Surface

Texas publicly distributes social studies expectations through its TEKS structure.

For repository purposes, the important public surfaces are:

- TEKS-based grade-level and course-level social studies standards
- strand or reporting-group style organization across history, government, geography, economics, and citizenship
- state-specific course and graduation-structure cues

## Profile Summary

Texas uses a state-distinct organizational grammar relative to a generic social studies baseline because TEKS naming, strand grouping, and course structure are central to how the standards are interpreted.

For repository purposes, Texas is best treated as a state-distinct profile with strong elementary grade-level organization and explicit secondary course structure.

## Relationship To Repository Social Studies Content Baseline

- relationship type: `state-distinct`
- core structure: `TEKS-based organization with state-specific wording, grouping, and course expectations`
- notable state distinction: `Texas-specific organizational grammar should be preserved as first-class metadata rather than normalized away`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

Texas TEKS social studies standards are primarily a content and expectation taxonomy. Repository performance bands remain separate unless Texas performance-descriptor or assessment-level surfaces are later codified under explicit review.

## Grade-Band Structure

### Kindergarten Through Grade 8

Primary organizational pattern:

- grade-level TEKS social studies expectations
- integrated treatment of citizenship, history, geography, economics, and culture
- recurring use of skills expectations alongside content expectations

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for early civics, chronology, place, economics, and inquiry-support nodes
- requires care where Texas combines skills and content inside the same expectation grouping

### Grades 9 Through 12

Primary organizational pattern:

- explicit secondary course organization
- strong course distinctions across United States history, world history, government, economics, and related electives
- continued emphasis on skills, source use, and civic reasoning

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for advanced history, civics, economics, and public reasoning nodes
- requires course-specific metadata in future mapping work

## Domain Or Strand Structure

Texas's public social studies surface is most useful to the repository when modeled through these strands:

- citizenship and civic participation
- history and historical interpretation
- geography and human-environment relationships
- economics and decision-making
- culture and continuity/change
- social studies skills, source use, and communication

The repository should preserve the distinction between content strands and TEKS skills surfaces when later crosswalks are attempted.

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `TEKS` is a first-class organizational term and should remain explicit
- skills expectations are often embedded alongside content expectations
- Texas secondary organization is strongly course-specific and should not be flattened into a single generic high-school surface
