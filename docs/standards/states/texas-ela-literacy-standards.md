## Profile Summary

Texas uses the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills for English Language Arts and Reading rather than a CCSS-style standards family. The current public Chapter 110 surface describes the elementary, middle-school, and high-school ELAR standards as `Adopted 2017`, with public TEA pages noting implementation of the revised high-school ELAR TEKS in the 2020-2021 school year.

Texas also publicly exposes the English Language Proficiency Standards, with a newer ELPS edition adopted in September 2024 and scheduled for classroom implementation beginning in the 2026-2027 school year.

For repository purposes, Texas is a state-distinct literacy profile.

## State-Distinct Mapping Rules

Texas should not be flattened into a CCSS-style strand-only model.

Important repository rules:

1. preserve integrated strand logic where one Texas expectation spans reading, writing, inquiry, and discussion
2. preserve `academic oracy` as a first-class literacy dependency rather than treating it as optional classroom context
3. preserve `ELPS` adjacency where multilingual-language demands materially affect access to a literacy claim
4. keep course-based high-school mapping separate from generic grade-band assumptions

## Grade-Band Structure

### Kindergarten Through Grade 8

Primary organizational pattern:

- integrated ELAR strands
- student expectations by grade
- strong foundational skills and oracy emphasis in early grades

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for oral language, foundational literacy, reading response, vocabulary, and writing nodes
- ELPS adjacency is important for language-access interpretation and multilingual learner support

### High School

Primary organizational pattern:

- course-based ELAR standards
- integrated reading, writing, thinking, listening, and speaking strands

Operational fit with repository node work:

- strong fit for rhetorical analysis, literary interpretation, argument, and advanced composition nodes

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `ELAR` and `TEKS` are governing state terms
- Texas explicitly foregrounds `academic oracy`
- the seven integrated strands should be preserved rather than flattened into a CCSS-like category system
- `ELPS` is an adjacent but critical language-proficiency standards surface for literacy interpretation
