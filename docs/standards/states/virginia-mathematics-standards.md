## Profile Summary

Virginia uses the Standards of Learning for Mathematics and, as of the official VDOE standards page reviewed on April 14, 2026, the state public surface centers the 2023 Mathematics Standards of Learning approved on August 31, 2023, with full implementation during the 2024-2025 school year.

For repository purposes, Virginia is a state-distinct profile. The mathematics content overlaps common multi-state content taxonomies, but the SOL structure, course surfaces, and supporting state materials should be represented directly.

## Relationship To Canonical Mathematics Content Baseline

- relationship type: `state-distinct`
- core structure: `grade-level and course-level Standards of Learning with state-specific organization`
- notable state distinction: `Virginia publishes supporting materials such as Understanding the Standards and instructional guides alongside the standards surface`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

Virginia's statewide SOL assessment system is alignment-relevant, but the content standards family should remain distinct from any later performance-level codification. If Virginia performance descriptors or blueprint-linked proficiency materials are later added, they should be modeled as separate artifacts.

### High School

Primary organizational pattern:

- course-based mathematics standards
- public state surface includes Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, and additional courses such as Probability and Statistics and Data Science

Operational fit with repository node work:

- repository high-school nodes may need course-aware mapping

## Domain Or Strand Structure

## Notable Divergences Or State-Specific Terminology

- `Standards of Learning` and `SOL` are governing state terms
- `Understanding the Standards` and instructional guides are public support documents that may influence later interpretation but should not replace the standards themselves
- course offerings such as `Data Science` create mapping opportunities outside a narrow traditional algebra-geometry-algebra II sequence
