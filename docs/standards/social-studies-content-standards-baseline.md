# Social Studies Content Standards Baseline

## Purpose

This document codifies the initial academic content standards baseline for the social studies domain.

The current repository baseline uses the governed social studies graph itself as the canonical anchor-level content-alignment profile for later mapping work. This is a repository operational codification, not a reproduction of official state or national standards text and not a claim that external social studies standards are already fully crosswalked here.

## Why This Baseline Exists

The social studies graph already models capability, dependency, verification, failure, and intervention. That model remains primary.

This baseline adds a stable external-facing content taxonomy so later work can:

- map nodes to recognizable social studies content anchors
- check coverage and gaps by strand and grade band
- support future crosswalk work to state or national social studies standards families
- keep content alignment separate from mastery or proficiency judgments

## Baseline Structure

The repository treats social studies content alignment as a four-level structure:

1. grade band or category
2. disciplinary strand
3. cluster or grouping
4. anchor statement or later external code-level identifier

The current baseline is intentionally conservative. It preserves repository anchor statements first and leaves official identifier fields unresolved until external standards families are codified in the repository.

## Grade-Band Content Model

### Kindergarten To Grade 5

Primary emphasis:

- observation, description, questioning, and evidence use
- chronology, identity, and community memory
- community rules, roles, institutions, and fairness
- place, maps, regions, and human-environment interaction
- needs, wants, exchange, and incentives

Operational anchors:

- early inquiry and evidence discourse
- foundational history and source comparison
- civic participation and institutional understanding
- geographic place and movement reasoning
- economic choice and local market reasoning

### Grades 6 To 8

Primary emphasis:

- supported explanation, investigation, and source evaluation
- causation, context, and historical argument
- civic decision-making, policy reasoning, and comparative institutions
- scale, spatial patterning, migration, and sustainability
- incentives, trade, macroeconomic reasoning, and finance basics

Operational anchors:

- inquiry and evidence evaluation
- history and source reasoning
- civics and collective decision-making
- geography and human-environment systems
- economics and exchange systems

### Grades 9 To 12

Primary emphasis:

- synthesis, argument, and public communication
- historiography, public history, and capstone argument
- advanced civic argument, policy design, and participation
- geospatial analysis and policy application
- economic analysis, policy tradeoffs, and capstone modeling
- interdisciplinary synthesis and capstone public argument

Operational anchors:

- disciplinary capstones in each social studies strand
- cross-disciplinary synthesis and public-facing argument

## Content Domains To Preserve In Later Mapping

When node-level mappings are added, each social studies node should be eligible to map to one or more of these content domains:

- inquiry, evidence, and discourse
- history and source reasoning
- civics and collective decision-making
- geography and human-environment systems
- economics and exchange systems
- advanced secondary synthesis and public argument

## Mapping Unit For Later Work

The preferred future mapping record for a node should include:

- node id
- node name
- primary grade band
- primary content domain
- cluster or grouping
- anchor statement
- optional official external identifier
- coverage note
- review status

This document does not claim official state or national social studies standards completeness. It defines the repository's current anchor-level alignment surface.

## Claim Boundary

This baseline codifies an external-facing academic content taxonomy for social studies at the repository anchor level. It does not by itself prove instructional adequacy, standards compliance, assessment validity, or jurisdiction-specific alignment.
