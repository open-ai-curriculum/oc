# AP Comparative Government And Politics Framework Profile

## Framework

- framework family: `AP Comparative Government and Politics`
- subject: `Social Studies`
- source profile: `public AP Comparative Government and Politics course-framework surface`
- repository codification status: `Drafted`

## Public Source Surface

For repository purposes, the important public surfaces are:

- political systems and regime comparison
- participation, rights, and public life
- policy, economy, and development
- source analysis, argument, and comparison

## Profile Summary

This framework is a high-value external social studies surface because it aligns to the repository's comparative civics, policy, global, and argument branches, while also exposing where country-case specificity remains under-modeled.

For repository purposes, it is best treated as a comparative-civics-centered course-distinct secondary framework with strong policy-and-argument adjacency.

## Relationship To Repository Social Studies Content Baseline

- relationship type: `course-distinct baseline-adjacent`
- core structure: `regime comparison, participation, rights, development, political economy, and evidence-based comparative argument`
- notable framework distinction: `the public course organization expects country-case structures and comparative political-economy distinctions that the canonical graph does not yet model as first-class entities`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

The public AP framework informs source analysis, argument, and comparative reasoning expectations, but repository performance bands for mastery, transfer, and retention remain separate governance layers unless explicitly codified from reviewed performance materials.

## Organizational Structure

The framework is most useful to the repository when modeled through these strands:

- political systems and regime comparison
- participation, rights, and public life
- policy, economy, and development
- source analysis, argument, and comparison

## Repository-Relevant Landing Zones

The strongest current landing zones are:

- `C2` through `C6` for institutions, rights, comparative government, and civic argument
- `Q4` through `Q6` for evidence use, evaluation, and argument
- `E4` and `E5` for political-economy and development context
- `G5` and `G6` for global context and policy application
- `A1` and `A2` where synthesis and public communication are required

## Notable Divergences Or Framework-Specific Constraints

- the public AP course surface expects country-case structures that the repository does not yet model as first-class entities
- comparative political economy remains broader in the current graph than in the course framework
- public course framing and assessment format should not be confused with official repository blueprint verification
