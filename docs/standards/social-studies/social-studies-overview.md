# Social Studies Overview

## Purpose

This document codifies the repository's social studies standards family as a governed package with explicit structure, versioning, and claim boundaries.

## Scope

The current package scope includes:

- inquiry, evidence, and discourse in social studies
- history and source reasoning
- civics and collective decision-making
- geography and human-environment systems
- economics and exchange systems
- advanced interdisciplinary synthesis and public communication

## Relationship To Public Standards Families

The repository expects future social studies standards work to align to external public standards families where available, including state social studies standards and inquiry-oriented frameworks used across history, civics, geography, and economics.

The current repository package does not yet codify one external social studies standards family as the canonical identifier-level baseline. Until that corpus exists in the repository, the maintained social studies alignment surface remains repository-baseline first.

## Repository Interpretation Rule

The social studies standards package is an alignment and codification surface. It does not replace:

- the capability graph
- node-level dependency logic
- verification gates
- failure-mode handling
- intervention rules
- human review requirements

## Claim Boundary

This package codifies the repository social studies standards surface for later mapping work. It does not yet assert external identifier-level equivalence, classroom sufficiency, or reviewed learner-impacting approval.
