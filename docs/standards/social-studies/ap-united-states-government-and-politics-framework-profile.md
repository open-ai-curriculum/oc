# AP United States Government And Politics Framework Profile

## Framework

- framework family: `AP United States Government and Politics`
- subject: `Social Studies`
- source profile: `public AP United States Government and Politics course-framework surface`
- repository codification status: `Drafted`

## Public Source Surface

For repository purposes, the important public surfaces are:

- course-level conceptual organization around constitutionalism, institutions, participation, liberty, and policy
- disciplinary practices involving source analysis, evidence use, and argument
- assessment-facing free-response and evidence-analysis expectations as public course-structure cues rather than as standalone repository standards

## Profile Summary

This framework is a high-value external social studies surface because it aligns strongly to the repository's existing civics and public-argument branches.

For repository purposes, it is best treated as a civics-centered course-distinct secondary framework with strong inquiry-and-argument adjacency.

## Relationship To Repository Social Studies Content Baseline

- relationship type: `course-distinct baseline-adjacent`
- core structure: `constitutional principles, institutions, participation, civil liberties, and evidence-based public reasoning`
- notable framework distinction: `the public course organization is course-specific and assessment-adjacent rather than a full K-12 social studies grammar`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

The public AP framework informs argument, evidence, and explanation expectations, but repository performance bands for mastery, transfer, and retention remain separate governance layers unless explicitly codified from reviewed performance materials.

## Organizational Structure

The framework is most useful to the repository when modeled through these strands:

- constitutional foundations and governing principles
- interactions among institutions and branches
- civil liberties, rights, and public policy
- political participation, deliberation, and civic action
- evidence analysis, argumentation, and public communication

## Repository-Relevant Landing Zones

The strongest current landing zones are:

- `C2` through `C6` for institutional, constitutional, and civic-participation structure
- `Q4` through `Q6` for evidence, evaluation, and public argument
- `A1` and `A2` where capstone-style synthesis and public communication are required

## Notable Divergences Or Framework-Specific Constraints

- the public AP course surface is narrower and more course-specific than the repository's canonical social studies graph
- branch-specific institutional detail can exceed the current civics resolution even where the overall graph posture is strong
- public course framing and assessment format should not be confused with official repository blueprint verification
