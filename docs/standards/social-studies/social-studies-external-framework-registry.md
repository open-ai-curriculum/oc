# Social Studies External Framework Registry

## Purpose

This registry lists the major non-state external social studies frameworks and assessment-alignment surfaces that are in scope for later codification under the repository social studies standards package.

The registry records repository work posture only. It does not claim that a framework has been fully codified, officially verified, or approved for exam-preparation use.

## Expansion Rule

A framework family may move from `Planned` to `Overlay-backed profile only` when:

1. a maintained overlay exists or the repository has an explicit bounded alignment surface for that framework family
2. claim boundaries remain conservative about blueprint verification and course equivalence
3. unresolved framework-specific fidelity gaps are explicit

A framework family may move from `Overlay-backed profile only` to `Structurally codified` only when:

1. its public organizational grammar is explicitly modeled
2. its blueprint, framework, or public course structure is separated from repository inference
3. its major divergences from the canonical social studies graph are recorded

The current repository meets that threshold for:

- `AP United States History`
- `AP World History: Modern`
- `AP United States Government and Politics`
- `AP Comparative Government and Politics`
- `AP Human Geography`
- `AP Macroeconomics`
- `AP Microeconomics`

A framework family may move from `Structurally codified` to `Ready for mapping review` only when:

1. framework-specific landing zones are identifiable without hidden assumptions
2. unresolved review questions are explicit
3. the repository can distinguish structure coverage from stronger equivalence claims

The current repository meets that threshold for:

- `AP United States Government and Politics`
- `AP Human Geography`
- `AP United States History`
- `AP Macroeconomics`

## Claim Boundary

This registry defines repository work scope for external social studies frameworks. It does not by itself assert official blueprint verification, compliance, or complete equivalence to any public framework family.
