# AP United States History Framework Profile

## Framework

- framework family: `AP United States History`
- subject: `Social Studies`
- source profile: `public AP United States History course-framework surface`
- repository codification status: `Drafted`

## Public Source Surface

For repository purposes, the important public surfaces are:

- course-level historical thinking and source-analysis organization
- chronology, causation, continuity/change, and comparison demands
- public course structure around United States history themes, institutions, reform, migration, and economy

## Profile Summary

This framework is a high-value external social studies surface because it aligns strongly to the repository's history branch and its argument/synthesis work, even though the current graph remains compressed relative to course-specific chronology.

For repository purposes, it is best treated as a history-centered course-distinct secondary framework with strong sourcing-and-argument adjacency.

## Relationship To Repository Social Studies Content Baseline

- relationship type: `course-distinct baseline-adjacent`
- core structure: `historical thinking, chronology, causation, thematic interpretation, civic context, migration, reform, and economy`
- notable framework distinction: `the public course organization is explicitly United States-history specific and more chronologically resolved than the canonical repository history branch`

## Relationship To Repository Performance Baseline

The public AP framework informs source use, evidence analysis, and argument expectations, but repository performance bands for mastery, transfer, and retention remain separate governance layers unless explicitly codified from reviewed performance materials.

## Organizational Structure

The framework is most useful to the repository when modeled through these strands:

- historical thinking and sourcing
- periodization, causation, and continuity/change
- government, society, and public life
- economy, geography, migration, and reform
- evidence-based historical argument and synthesis

## Repository-Relevant Landing Zones

The strongest current landing zones are:

- `H1` through `H6` for chronology, source use, causation, periodization, and historiographic reasoning
- `Q4` through `Q6` for evidence use, evaluation, and argument
- `C2` through `C5` where government and public-life structures matter
- `G3` through `G5` and `E3` through `E4` where migration, geography, and economy intersect with historical interpretation
- `A1` and `A2` where synthesis and public communication are required

## Notable Divergences Or Framework-Specific Constraints

- the public AP course surface is more chronologically specific than the repository's canonical history branch
- course-specific United States institutional detail can exceed the current civics/history resolution
- public course framing and assessment format should not be confused with official repository blueprint verification

## Mapping Readiness Notes

This profile is structurally useful for future mapping because:

- the framework organization is clear enough to identify stable history landing zones
- the graph already models most of the underlying disciplinary territory explicitly
- the remaining gaps are concentrated in chronology specificity and branch detail, not in missing history coverage

Current posture:

- structurally useful for framework-to-node review
- not yet sufficient for blueprint-verified or identifier-level equivalence claims

## Unresolved Review Questions

- whether chronology should be decomposed further before stronger course-level claims are made
- which course-specific institutional distinctions should become first-class metadata rather than overlay notes
- how much assessment-format specificity should remain outside the standards package

## Claim Boundary

This document codifies the public AP United States History framework surface for repository alignment planning. It does not claim official blueprint verification, reviewed node-level equivalence, or exam-preparation approval.
