## Volume Metadata

- Volume title: Grade 1 Grading Key Draft
- Program layer: grading key
- Grade / grade band: Grade 1

## Purpose

Provide a real Grade 1 scoring and answer-key draft assembled from packet answer keys, scoring guidance, and assessment keys.

## How To Use This Volume

- Use `secure`, `developing`, and `not yet` as instructional performance bands.
- Pair this volume with the teacher edition and assessment pack.
- Record prompting level when it changes interpretation.

## Calibration and Access Notes

- Count oral explanation, drawing, manipulatives, shared writing, and performance evidence when those modes fit the lesson target.
- Separate correctness from independence; a correct response with heavy prompting should not be scored the same way as independent performance.
- Use [exemplar-student-responses.md](/Volumes/data/development/oc/curriculum/grade-1/program/exemplar-student-responses.md) to calibrate quality of explanation and evidence.

## Section 1: Narrative Reading and Key Details

### What Counts as Secure

- answers key-detail questions accurately
- retells beginning, middle, and end with relevant details

### What Counts as Developing

- answers or retells partially with prompting
- includes some relevant but incomplete detail

### What Counts as Not Yet

- responses are mostly guessed, off-text, or not sequenced

## Section 2: Informational Reading and Questions

### What Counts as Secure

- identifies the topic
- answers questions with relevant facts
- points to or names text support

### What Counts as Developing

- identifies part of the topic or gives a partial fact with support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet connect answers to the text

## Section 3: Phonics, Decoding, and Fluency

### What Counts as Secure

- decodes target words accurately
- rereads connected text more smoothly

### What Counts as Developing

- decodes with some errors or prompting
- rereads with limited improvement

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet apply blending reliably

## Section 4: Opinion Writing With Reasons

### What Counts as Secure

- states an opinion clearly
- gives a matching reason

### What Counts as Developing

- states an opinion or reason but not both clearly
- needs support to connect the reason

### What Counts as Not Yet

- writing does not yet communicate a clear opinion-and-reason relationship

## Section 5: Informative Writing From Shared Text

### What Counts as Secure

- names the topic
- includes one or more matching facts

### What Counts as Developing

- names the topic or gives a partial fact with support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- writing does not yet stay connected to the topic

## Section 6: Addition, Subtraction, and Number Stories

### What Counts as Secure

- represents the story accurately
- solves it reasonably
- explains the thinking
- may show the story with more than one valid representation

### What Counts as Developing

- solves or represents with partial accuracy or prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- representation and solution do not yet match the story

## Section 7: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

### What Counts as Secure

- identifies one plant need accurately
- records a real observation
- notices or describes a visible change or pattern when prompted appropriately

### What Counts as Developing

- gives a partial or prompted observation

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet connect the response to plant observation

## Section 8: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

### What Counts as Secure

- identifies places on a simple map
- names one community responsibility
- uses a symbol, key, or label accurately to support the answer

### What Counts as Developing

- partially reads the map or gives a partial civic explanation

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet connect map evidence and community role

## Section 9: Arts, Performance, and Creation

### What Counts as Secure

- creates in response to the prompt
- explains one artistic choice

### What Counts as Developing

- creates or explains, but not both clearly

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet connect the response to the prompt

## Section 10: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

### What Counts as Secure

- identifies a healthy or safe choice
- explains why it helps
- applies the idea to a familiar scenario rather than only naming a rule

### What Counts as Developing

- names a choice with limited explanation or support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet distinguish a helpful choice

## Section 11: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

### What Counts as Secure

- moves with control
- follows cues
- cooperates with a partner

### What Counts as Developing

- shows partial movement control or partner cooperation with prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet demonstrate safe control or cooperation

## Section 12: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

### What Counts as Secure

- orders and follows steps accurately
- names one safe digital-use norm
- can notice when the order is wrong and help fix it

### What Counts as Developing

- completes part of the sequence or names a rule with support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- does not yet demonstrate sequencing or safe-use understanding

## Grading Notes Across Grade 1

- Balance oral, written, visual, and performance evidence.
- Give more weight to reasoning and explanation than answer-only completion.
- Keep scoring aligned to grade-level expectations, not to neatness or stamina alone.
