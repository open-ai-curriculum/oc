Chapter 1: Narrative Reading and Key Details

Lesson Focus

Students answer questions about key story details and retell a narrative in order.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Read a short narrative aloud.
  • Pause for text-grounded questions.
  • Distinguish key details from extra details.
  • Close with beginning-middle-end retell.
Observation

Answers from the text, identifies a key detail, retells in a logical sequence.

Asset Pair

Key-detail strips and retell organizer.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 4

Chapter 2: Informational Reading and Questions

Lesson Focus

Students ask and answer questions about key facts and identify a text topic.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Preview the topic and text features.
  • Ask questions tied to key details.
  • Model how to use the text to answer.
Observation

Identifies the topic, answers with a relevant fact, uses text support.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 5

Chapter 3: Phonics, Decoding, and Fluency

Lesson Focus

Students decode short-vowel words and reread connected text more smoothly.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Model sound-by-sound blending.
  • Connect word reading to decodable text.
  • Reread for accuracy and phrasing.
Accessibility

Use sound boxes, letter cards, and oral rehearsal.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 6

Chapter 4: Opinion Writing With Reasons

Lesson Focus

Students state an opinion, add a matching reason, and reread for clarity.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Model an opinion statement.
  • Compare matching and non-matching reasons.
  • Support oral rehearsal before writing.
Family Connection

Invite one opinion and one reason in conversation at home.

Chapter 5: Informative Writing From Shared Text

Lesson Focus

Students name a topic and write or dictate one or more matching facts.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Revisit shared reading.
  • Model a topic-and-fact sentence.
  • Help students rehearse before writing.
Asset Pair

Informative fact organizer and exemplar topic-and-fact response.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 8

Chapter 6: Addition, Subtraction, and Number Stories

Lesson Focus

Students solve a number story with objects, drawings, or equations and explain their reasoning.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Present a short join or separate story.
  • Model representation with objects or drawings.
  • Compare strategies.
  • Invite more than one valid representation.
Observation

Accurate representation, reasonable strategy, explanation tied to the representation.

Asset Pair

Number-story representation mat and explain-my-strategy strip.

Chapter 7: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

Lesson Focus

Students observe plants, identify needs, and record evidence from observation.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Observe a real plant or image.
  • Discuss plant needs and visible evidence.
  • Revisit the same plant over time.
Observation

Records a real observation, names one plant need, notices one change or pattern.

Chapter 8: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

Lesson Focus

Students use a simple map and discuss responsible participation in shared spaces.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Study a simple community or school map.
  • Identify places and routes.
  • Teach how a symbol key works.
  • Connect map reading to community responsibilities.
Asset Pair

Map key and community response card.

Exemplar

Student circles the library and explains how to care for that place.

Chapter 9: Arts, Performance, and Creation

Lesson Focus

Students create an artistic response and explain one choice they made.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Introduce a clear prompt.
  • Allow multiple creative modes.
  • Support short sharing and reflection.
  • Invite one simple revision.
Observation

Creates in response to the prompt, explains one feature or choice, revises purposefully when prompted.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 12

Chapter 10: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

Lesson Focus

Students identify healthy or safe choices and explain why they help.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Sort simple examples.
  • Discuss safe and healthy choices.
  • Connect feelings to helpful responses.
  • Use daily-life scenarios and explanation language.
Accessibility

Use calm routines, concrete examples, and avoid shame-based framing.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 13

Chapter 11: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

Lesson Focus

Students move with control, respond to cues, and cooperate with a partner.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Model travel patterns.
  • Cue speed and direction changes.
  • Teach pathway awareness and safe spacing.
  • Rehearse partner routines before the movement task.
Family Connection

Suggest a simple partner or cue-based movement game at home.

Grade 1 Teacher Edition, page 14

Chapter 12: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

Lesson Focus

Students give and follow clear steps and discuss one safe digital-use norm.

Core Teaching Moves

  • Model a short sequence.
  • Order and test steps.
  • Use unplugged algorithm tasks before device tasks.
  • Include a simple debugging routine when steps fail.
Observation

Orders steps correctly, follows the sequence, names a safe-use norm, and notices when the order is wrong.
