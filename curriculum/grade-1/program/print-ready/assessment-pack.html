Assessment Access Notes

Accept oral, written, drawn, manipulative-based, and teacher-recorded evidence when the lesson target allows it. Keep assessment language simple unless reading it is itself part of the target.

Use reproducibles and exemplar student responses to support administration and calibration.

Assessments 1-4

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
1. Story Key Details and RetellRead or revisit a short narrative text, ask two key-detail questions, and ask the student to retell beginning, middle, and end.Answers key-detail questions accurately and retells in order with relevant details.
2. Informational Text QuestionsProvide a short informational text, ask a question about a key detail, and ask what the text is mostly about.Identifies the topic and gives a relevant fact-based answer.
3. Decoding and RereadingAsk the student to decode target words and reread a short connected line or passage.Sound-symbol accuracy, blending success, smoother rereading.
4. Opinion WritingGive a familiar topic and ask the student to state an opinion and one reason in speech or writing.Clear opinion and matching reason.
Grade 1 Assessment Pack, page 3

Assessments 5-8

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
5. Informative WritingRevisit a shared text or topic and ask the student to name the topic and give one fact.Topic named clearly; fact matches the topic.
6. Number StoryPresent a join or separate story; ask the student to show the math, solve it, and when appropriate show a second representation.Story represented accurately; solution and explanation match; compares or interprets a second valid representation when used.
7. Plant ObservationShow a plant or plant image; ask for one observation and one plant need; if possible ask what changed over time.Real observation; accurate plant need; notes one visible change or pattern when applicable.
8. Map and CitizenshipShow a simple map; ask the student to identify a place, use a key or symbol if present, and name one community responsibility.Map-reading accuracy; civic understanding; uses a key, symbol, or label accurately when applicable.

Assessments 9-12

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
9. Artistic ResponseGive a prompt; ask the student to create a quick artistic response and explain one choice; if appropriate ask for one revision.Prompt-connected creation; one explained artistic choice; purposeful revision when used.
10. Healthy ChoiceShow an everyday scenario and ask the student to identify one healthy or safe choice and explain why.Identifies a helpful choice and gives a sensible explanation.
11. Movement and CooperationAsk the student to complete a short movement task with cues and include one partner or cooperative element.Cue response; safe movement control; cooperative participation.
12. Sequencing and Safe UseGive a short set of steps to order; ask the student to follow them, state one safe digital-use norm, and fix the order if it is mixed up.Sequencing accuracy; safe-use understanding; notices and fixes incorrect order when debugging is used.
