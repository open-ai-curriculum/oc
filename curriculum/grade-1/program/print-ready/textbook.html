Unit 1: Reading Stories for Key Details

Page 1: Stories Have Important Details

Some details in a story are important because they help us understand what happened.

  • Who is in the story?
  • What happened?
  • Where did it happen?

Page 2: Retell the Story

Good readers retell the story in order: beginning, middle, end.

Talk About It
  • What is one important detail?
  • What happened in the beginning?
  • What happened in the end?
Grade 1 Textbook, page 3

Unit 2: Reading Informational Texts

Page 3: Texts Can Teach Us Facts

Informational texts teach about a topic. They may use headings, labels, pictures, and facts.

Page 4: Ask and Answer Questions

Readers ask questions to learn more and look back at the text to find answers.

Talk About It
  • What is the topic?
  • What is one fact you learned?
  • Which picture or sentence helped you?

Unit 3: Phonics and Fluency

Page 5: Read the Sounds in Order

Readers look at letters from left to right. They blend sounds together to read a word.

Page 6: Reread to Read Smoothly

Rereading can help readers fix mistakes, sound smoother, and understand better.

Talk About It
  • Which sound comes first?
  • What word did you blend?
  • Did rereading help?
Grade 1 Textbook, page 5

Unit 4: Opinion Writing

Page 7: Writers Share What They Think

An opinion tells what the writer thinks. Writers can say “I think...” or “My opinion is...”

Page 8: Reasons Match the Opinion

A reason should help explain the opinion and should match what the writer says.

Talk About It
  • What is your opinion?
  • What is one reason?
Grade 1 Textbook, page 6

Unit 5: Informative Writing

Page 9: Writers Teach About a Topic

Informative writing teaches the reader. Writers name the topic and tell facts.

Page 10: Facts Match What We Learned

Facts should come from reading, observation, or shared study. Writers reread to make sure their facts fit the topic.

Talk About It
  • What is the topic?
  • What fact did you learn?

Unit 6: Addition and Subtraction Stories

Page 11: Number Stories Show Math

Some number stories show joining. Some show separating. We can use objects, drawings, or equations to show the story.

Page 12: Explain How You Solved It

Mathematicians explain what happened, how they showed it, and how they found the answer.

Page 13: Different Strategies Can Work

Counters, drawings, and equations can all solve the same story if they match what happened.

Talk About It
  • Did the story show joining or separating?
  • How did you solve it?

Unit 7: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

Page 13: Plants Need Things to Live

Plants need water, light, air, and space to grow.

Page 14: Scientists Observe Carefully

Scientists notice patterns and record observations using words, drawings, and labels.

Page 15: Plants Change Over Time

Plants may grow new leaves, get taller, and change when they get enough water and light.

Talk About It
  • What do plants need?
  • What did you observe?

Unit 8: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

Page 15: Maps Show Places

A map can show where things are, including buildings, roads, paths, and important places.

Page 16: Communities Have Shared Responsibilities

People follow rules and take care of shared spaces.

Page 17: Symbols Help Us Read Maps

Maps use symbols and labels to show places such as a school, park, road, or library.

Talk About It
  • What place do you see on the map?
  • What is one community responsibility?

Unit 9: Arts, Performance, and Creation

Page 17: Art Can Show Ideas and Feelings

Artists create with lines, color, rhythm, and movement.

Page 18: Artists Share Their Choices

Artists can explain what they made, what they chose, and what they want others to notice.

Page 19: Artists Can Revise Their Work

Artists can add a detail or change a color, shape, sound, or movement to make the idea clearer.

Talk About It
  • What did you create?
  • What is one choice you made?

Unit 10: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

Page 20: Healthy and Safe Choices Matter

People make choices that affect health and safety. Some choices help our bodies and minds.

Page 21: Feelings Can Be Named and Managed

People can name feelings and choose helpful responses.

Page 22: Healthy Choices Help Us Every Day

Healthy choices include washing hands, eating healthy foods, resting, and wearing safety gear when needed.

Talk About It
  • What is one healthy choice?
  • What is one helpful response to a feeling?

Unit 11: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

Page 23: Bodies Move in Different Ways

People can walk, run, hop, and skip. They can also change speed and direction.

Page 24: Cooperation Helps Movement Work

Partners listen, share space, and follow cues.

Page 25: Cues Help Us Change Movement

Teachers or partners may give cues such as faster, slower, stop, and change direction.

Talk About It
  • How did you move?
  • How did you work with a partner?

Unit 12: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

Page 26: Steps Can Solve Problems

An algorithm is a set of steps in order.

Page 27: Digital Tools Should Be Used Safely

People should be safe and respectful with digital tools.

Page 28: Algorithms Need Clear Order

If the steps are out of order, the task may not work. People test steps, fix them, and try again.

Talk About It
  • What comes first?
  • What comes next?
  • What is one safe digital-use rule?
