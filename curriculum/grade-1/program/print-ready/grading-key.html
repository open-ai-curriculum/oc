Sections 1-4

SectionSecureDevelopingNot Yet
1. Narrative Reading and Key DetailsAnswers key-detail questions accurately and retells beginning, middle, and end with relevant details.Answers or retells partially with prompting.Responses are mostly guessed, off-text, or not sequenced.
2. Informational Reading and QuestionsIdentifies the topic, answers with relevant facts, and points to or names text support.Identifies part of the topic or gives a partial fact with support.Does not yet connect answers to the text.
3. Phonics, Decoding, and FluencyDecodes target words accurately and rereads connected text more smoothly.Decodes with some errors or prompting.Does not yet apply blending reliably.
4. Opinion Writing With ReasonsStates an opinion clearly and gives a matching reason.States an opinion or reason but not both clearly.Writing does not yet communicate a clear opinion-and-reason relationship.

Sections 5-8

SectionSecureDevelopingNot Yet
5. Informative Writing From Shared TextNames the topic and includes one or more matching facts.Names the topic or gives a partial fact with support.Writing does not yet stay connected to the topic.
6. Addition, Subtraction, and Number StoriesRepresents the story accurately, solves it reasonably, explains the thinking, and may show more than one valid representation.Solves or represents with partial accuracy or prompting.Representation and solution do not yet match the story.
7. Plants, Needs, and PatternsIdentifies one plant need accurately, records a real observation, and notices a visible change or pattern.Gives a partial or prompted observation.Does not yet connect the response to plant observation.
8. Community, Maps, and CitizenshipIdentifies places on a simple map, names one community responsibility, and uses a symbol, key, or label accurately.Partially reads the map or gives a partial civic explanation.Does not yet connect map evidence and community role.

SectionSecureDevelopingNot Yet
9. Arts, Performance, and CreationCreates in response to the prompt and explains one artistic choice.Creates or explains, but not both clearly.Does not yet connect the response to the prompt.
10. Nutrition, Safety, and EmotionsIdentifies a healthy or safe choice, explains why it helps, and applies the idea to a familiar scenario.Names a choice with limited explanation or support.Does not yet distinguish a helpful choice.
11. Locomotor Skills and CooperationMoves with control, follows cues, and cooperates with a partner.Shows partial movement control or partner cooperation with prompting.Does not yet demonstrate safe control or cooperation.
12. Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic ThinkingOrders and follows steps accurately, names one safe digital-use norm, and notices when the order is wrong and helps fix it.Completes part of the sequence or names a rule with support.Does not yet demonstrate sequencing or safe-use understanding.
