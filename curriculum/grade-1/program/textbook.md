## Unit 1: Reading Stories for Key Details

### Page 1: Stories Have Important Details

Some details in a story are important because they help us understand what happened.

We can ask:

- Who is in the story?
- What happened?
- Where did it happen?

### Page 2: Retell the Story

Good readers retell the story in order:

- beginning
- middle
- end

They use details that matter.

### Talk About It

- What is one important detail?
- What happened in the beginning?
- What happened in the end?

## Unit 2: Reading Informational Texts

### Page 3: Texts Can Teach Us Facts

Informational texts teach about a topic.

They may use:

- headings
- labels
- pictures
- facts

### Page 4: Ask and Answer Questions

Readers ask questions to learn more.

They look back at the text to find answers.

### Talk About It

- What is the topic?
- What is one fact you learned?
- Which picture or sentence helped you?

## Unit 3: Phonics and Fluency

### Page 5: Read the Sounds in Order

Readers look at letters from left to right.

They blend sounds together to read a word.

### Page 6: Reread to Read Smoothly

Rereading can help readers:

- fix mistakes
- sound smoother
- understand better

### Talk About It

- Which sound comes first?
- What word did you blend?
- Did rereading help?

## Unit 4: Opinion Writing

### Page 7: Writers Share What They Think

An opinion tells what the writer thinks.

Writers can say:

- "I think..."
- "My opinion is..."

### Page 8: Reasons Match the Opinion

A reason should help explain the opinion.

The reason should match what the writer says.

### Talk About It

- What is your opinion?
- What is one reason?

## Unit 5: Informative Writing

### Page 9: Writers Teach About a Topic

Informative writing teaches the reader.

Writers:

- name the topic
- tell facts

### Page 10: Facts Match What We Learned

Facts should come from reading, observation, or shared study.

Writers reread to make sure their facts fit the topic.

### Talk About It

- What is the topic?
- What fact did you learn?

## Unit 6: Addition and Subtraction Stories

### Page 11: Number Stories Show Math

Some number stories show joining.

Some show separating.

We can use objects, drawings, or equations to show the story.

### Page 12: Explain How You Solved It

Mathematicians explain their thinking.

They can say:

- what happened in the story
- how they showed it
- how they found the answer

### Page 13: Different Strategies Can Work

One student may use counters.

Another student may draw a picture.

Another student may write an equation.

Different strategies can solve the same story if they match what happened.

### Talk About It

- Did the story show joining or separating?
- How did you solve it?

## Unit 7: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

### Page 13: Plants Need Things to Live

Plants need:

- water
- light
- air
- space to grow

### Page 14: Scientists Observe Carefully

Scientists notice patterns and record observations.

They use:

- words
- drawings
- labels

### Page 15: Plants Change Over Time

Plants may grow new leaves.

They may get taller.

They may change when they get enough water and light.

Scientists compare what they see over time.

### Talk About It

- What do plants need?
- What did you observe?

## Unit 8: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

### Page 15: Maps Show Places

A map can show where things are.

It can show:

- buildings
- roads
- paths
- important places

### Page 16: Communities Have Shared Responsibilities

People help communities work well.

They follow rules and take care of shared spaces.

### Page 17: Symbols Help Us Read Maps

Maps use symbols and labels to show places.

A symbol can stand for:

- a school
- a park
- a road
- a library

Readers use the key to understand the map.

### Talk About It

- What place do you see on the map?
- What is one community responsibility?

## Unit 9: Arts, Performance, and Creation

### Page 17: Art Can Show Ideas and Feelings

Artists create with:

- lines
- color
- rhythm
- movement

### Page 18: Artists Share Their Choices

Artists can explain:

- what they made
- what they chose
- what they want others to notice

### Page 19: Artists Can Revise Their Work

Artists sometimes add a detail.

They may change a color, shape, sound, or movement.

Revision helps artists make their idea clearer.

### Talk About It

- What did you create?
- What is one choice you made?

## Unit 10: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

### Page 20: Healthy and Safe Choices Matter

People make choices that affect health and safety.

Some choices help our bodies and minds.

### Page 21: Feelings Can Be Named and Managed

People can name feelings and choose helpful responses.

Helpful responses include:

- asking for help
- taking a calm breath
- making a safe choice

### Page 22: Healthy Choices Help Us Every Day

Healthy choices include:

- washing hands
- eating healthy foods
- resting
- wearing safety gear when needed

These choices help keep our bodies and minds ready.

### Talk About It

- What is one healthy choice?
- What is one helpful response to a feeling?

## Unit 11: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

### Page 23: Bodies Move in Different Ways

People can:

- walk
- run
- hop
- skip

They can also change speed and direction.

### Page 24: Cooperation Helps Movement Work

Partners listen, share space, and follow cues.

Working together helps everyone move safely.

### Page 25: Cues Help Us Change Movement

Teachers or partners may give cues such as:

- faster
- slower
- stop
- change direction

Listening to cues helps people move with control.

### Talk About It

- How did you move?
- How did you work with a partner?

## Unit 12: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

### Page 26: Steps Can Solve Problems

An algorithm is a set of steps in order.

People can give and follow steps to complete a task.

### Page 27: Digital Tools Should Be Used Safely

People should be safe and respectful with digital tools.

They should:

- listen to directions
- use tools for the right purpose
- ask for help when needed

### Page 28: Algorithms Need Clear Order

If the steps are out of order, the task may not work.

People test steps, fix them, and try again.

That helps the algorithm make sense.

### Talk About It

- What comes first?
- What comes next?
- What is one safe digital-use rule?
