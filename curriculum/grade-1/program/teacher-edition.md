## Chapter 1: Narrative Reading and Key Details

### Lesson Focus

Students answer questions about key story details and retell a narrative in order.

### Core Teaching Moves

- read a short narrative aloud
- pause for text-grounded questions
- distinguish key details from extra details
- close with beginning-middle-end retell

### Teacher Language

- "Which detail helps us understand what happened?"
- "What happened first?"
- "What happened in the middle?"

### Observation Priorities

- answers from the text rather than guessing
- identifies a key detail
- retells in a logical sequence

### Accessibility Notes

- use repeated reading, visuals, and oral scaffolds
- allow drawing, pointing, and oral retell

### Family Connection

Invite families to ask for a simple retell of a familiar story.

## Chapter 2: Informational Reading and Questions

### Lesson Focus

Students ask and answer questions about key facts and identify a text topic.

### Core Teaching Moves

- preview the topic and text features
- ask questions tied to key details
- model how to use the text to answer

### Teacher Language

- "What is this text mostly about?"
- "Which detail helps answer the question?"

### Observation Priorities

- identifies the topic
- answers with a relevant fact
- uses a picture, label, or sentence as support

### Accessibility Notes

- use clear visuals and manageable text sections
- allow oral, gestural, and picture-supported responses

### Family Connection

Ask families to talk about one fact learned from a nonfiction text or topic.

## Chapter 3: Phonics, Decoding, and Fluency

### Lesson Focus

Students decode short-vowel words and reread connected text more smoothly.

### Core Teaching Moves

- model sound-by-sound blending
- connect word reading to decodable text
- reread for accuracy and phrasing

### Teacher Language

- "Say the sounds."
- "Blend the word."
- "Read it again smoothly."

### Observation Priorities

- accurate sound-symbol mapping
- successful blending
- improved rereading on the second attempt

### Accessibility Notes

- use sound boxes, letter cards, and oral rehearsal
- keep decodable text focused and readable

### Family Connection

Invite a short reread of a decodable line or word list at home.

## Chapter 4: Opinion Writing With Reasons

### Lesson Focus

Students state an opinion, add a matching reason, and reread for clarity.

### Core Teaching Moves

- model an opinion statement
- compare matching and non-matching reasons
- support oral rehearsal before writing

### Teacher Language

- "What is your opinion?"
- "Does that reason match?"
- "Read your sentence again."

### Observation Priorities

- clear opinion statement
- reason that actually supports the opinion
- basic rereading and revision

### Accessibility Notes

- allow dictation, drawing, and shared writing
- use sentence frames as needed

### Family Connection

Ask families to invite one opinion and one reason in conversation.

## Chapter 5: Informative Writing From Shared Text

### Lesson Focus

Students name a topic and write or dictate one or more matching facts.

### Core Teaching Moves

- revisit shared reading
- model a topic-and-fact sentence
- help students rehearse before writing

### Teacher Language

- "What is the topic?"
- "What fact did we learn?"
- "Does your fact match the topic?"

### Observation Priorities

- names the topic clearly
- includes a relevant fact
- rereads and clarifies the writing

### Accessibility Notes

- allow dictation and drawing-plus-writing
- use simple organizers and oral rehearsal

### Family Connection

Ask families to talk about one fact learned in class.

## Chapter 6: Addition, Subtraction, and Number Stories

### Lesson Focus

Students solve a number story with objects, drawings, or equations and explain their reasoning.

### Core Teaching Moves

- present a short join or separate story
- model representation with objects or drawings
- compare strategies
- invite more than one valid representation when time allows
- connect objects, drawings, and equations explicitly

### Teacher Language

- "What is happening in the story?"
- "How did you show it?"
- "How do you know the answer?"

### Observation Priorities

- accurate representation of the story
- reasonable strategy use
- explanation tied to the representation
- can compare or interpret more than one strategy for the same story

### Accessibility Notes

- use manipulatives and drawings
- reduce reading load not central to the math

### Family Connection

Invite families to make up and solve a simple number story at home.

## Chapter 7: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

### Lesson Focus

Students observe plants, identify needs, and record evidence from observation.

### Core Teaching Moves

- observe a real plant or image
- discuss plant needs and visible evidence
- record one observation
- revisit the same plant over time
- model the difference between an observation and a guess

### Teacher Language

- "What do you notice?"
- "What does a plant need to live?"

### Observation Priorities

- names one plant need
- records a real observation
- uses evidence rather than guessing
- notices one change or pattern across observations

### Accessibility Notes

- use labeled visuals, real objects, and oral discussion
- allow drawing and pointing

### Family Connection

Ask families to notice a plant and talk about what it needs.

## Chapter 8: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

### Lesson Focus

Students use a simple map and discuss responsible participation in shared spaces.

### Core Teaching Moves

- study a simple community or school map
- identify places and routes
- connect map reading to community responsibilities
- explicitly teach how a symbol key works
- connect citizenship to care for shared spaces and routines

### Teacher Language

- "What place is this?"
- "How do people use this space responsibly?"

### Observation Priorities

- reads a simple map feature
- identifies a place accurately
- names one civic responsibility
- uses a key or label to interpret a map symbol

### Accessibility Notes

- use clear icons, labels, and oral discussion
- allow pointing and drawing

### Family Connection

Invite families to talk about places they use in the community.

## Chapter 9: Arts, Performance, and Creation

### Lesson Focus

Students create an artistic response and explain one choice they made.

### Core Teaching Moves

- introduce a clear prompt
- allow multiple creative modes
- support short sharing and reflection
- invite one simple revision or added detail
- help students name one intentional choice in the work

### Teacher Language

- "What did you make?"
- "What is one choice you made?"

### Observation Priorities

- creates in response to the prompt
- explains one feature or choice
- revises or extends the work in a purposeful way when prompted

### Accessibility Notes

- allow drawing, sound, movement, and oral reflection
- avoid over-standardizing outcomes

### Family Connection

Encourage a simple creative response at home.

## Chapter 10: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

### Lesson Focus

Students identify healthy or safe choices and explain why they help.

### Core Teaching Moves

- sort simple examples
- discuss safe and healthy choices
- connect feelings to helpful responses
- use daily-life scenarios instead of decontextualized lists alone
- ask students to explain why a choice helps

### Teacher Language

- "Is this a healthy choice?"
- "Why does that help?"
- "What could you do if you feel upset?"

### Observation Priorities

- identifies a healthy or safe choice
- explains why it helps
- connects feeling and response appropriately
- applies a healthy or safe choice to a familiar scenario

### Accessibility Notes

- use calm routines and concrete examples
- avoid shaming or fear-based framing

### Family Connection

Ask families to talk about one healthy or safe everyday choice.

## Chapter 11: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

### Lesson Focus

Students move with control, respond to cues, and cooperate with a partner.

### Core Teaching Moves

- model travel patterns
- cue speed and direction changes
- structure a simple partner task
- teach pathway awareness and safe spacing explicitly
- rehearse partner routines before the full movement task

### Teacher Language

- "Change speed."
- "Freeze."
- "Work with your partner."

### Observation Priorities

- safe movement control
- response to cues
- partner cooperation
- awareness of shared space while moving

### Accessibility Notes

- offer movement choices and pacing supports
- keep safety expectations explicit

### Family Connection

Suggest a simple partner or cue-based movement game at home.

## Chapter 12: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

### Lesson Focus

Students give and follow clear steps and discuss one safe digital-use norm.

### Core Teaching Moves

- model a short sequence
- order and test steps
- connect the sequence task to one digital-safety expectation
- use unplugged algorithm tasks before device tasks
- include a simple debugging routine when steps fail

### Teacher Language

- "What comes first?"
- "What step comes next?"
- "What is one safe digital choice?"

### Observation Priorities

- orders steps correctly
- follows the sequence
- names a safe-use norm
- can notice when a step order fails and help correct it

### Accessibility Notes

- use picture cards and unplugged tasks
- do not assume home device access

### Family Connection

Invite families to talk through steps in a daily task and one safe tech rule.
