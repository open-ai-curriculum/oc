# Grade 1 Non-Literacy Program Guide

## Purpose

Deepen the assembled Grade 1 program across math, science, social studies, arts, health, physical education, and technology so those strands carry clearer instructional substance and teacher guidance.

## Status

Draft program companion. This guide expands the non-literacy program layer and should be used with the Grade 1 textbook, workbook, teacher edition, grading key, and assessment pack.

## Math: Addition, Subtraction, and Number Stories

### Student Experience

- solve join and separate situations using objects, drawings, and equations
- explain what happened in the number story
- compare different valid strategies

### Teacher Moves

- read stories aloud with clear quantities and actions
- model representation before introducing equations
- press for explanation with "How do you know?"

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- number-story mats
- draw-and-solve pages
- explain-your-strategy strips

### Assessment Evidence

- representation matches the story
- solution is reasonable
- explanation connects to the representation

## Science: Plants, Needs, and Patterns

### Student Experience

- observe plants over time
- identify basic needs and visible changes
- describe patterns using drawings, labels, and oral explanation

### Teacher Moves

- anchor observations in real plants or consistent images
- separate observation from guessing
- revisit the same plant over time

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- plant observation logs
- need-and-evidence match pages
- pattern-noticing sentence frames

### Assessment Evidence

- records a real observation
- names a plant need accurately
- identifies a simple pattern or change

## Social Studies: Community, Maps, and Citizenship

### Student Experience

- read simple maps and symbols
- locate familiar places
- explain one responsibility in a community

### Teacher Moves

- connect maps to real classroom and school spaces
- model how symbols stand for places
- discuss rules and responsibilities as shared care

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- map symbol cards
- school/community map pages
- citizenship reflection slips

### Assessment Evidence

- identifies place and symbol relationships
- uses a simple map to answer a question
- explains one responsibility clearly

## Arts: Performance and Creation

### Student Experience

- create from a prompt using visual art, rhythm, or movement
- revise or add a detail intentionally
- explain one artistic choice

### Teacher Moves

- model how artists make choices
- keep sharing routines brief and specific
- reinforce process, observation, and response vocabulary

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- prompt cards
- choice-reflection sheets
- peer-notice-and-name slips

### Assessment Evidence

- work responds to the prompt
- student explains one intentional choice
- participation is safe and sustained

## Health: Nutrition, Safety, and Emotions

### Student Experience

- identify healthy food, safety, and self-regulation choices
- explain why a choice is helpful
- connect feelings to responses and routines

### Teacher Moves

- use real scenarios rather than isolated vocabulary only
- connect health to daily routines
- model explanation language: "This helps because..."

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- scenario cards
- healthy-choice sorting pages
- emotions-and-response mini planners

### Assessment Evidence

- identifies a healthy or safe choice
- gives a sensible reason
- applies the idea to a familiar scenario

## Physical Education: Locomotor Skills and Cooperation

### Student Experience

- combine basic locomotor movements
- follow cues for speed, direction, and stopping
- cooperate with a partner or small group

### Teacher Moves

- model movement pathways clearly
- use cue words consistently
- teach partner routines, not just movement tasks

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- movement sequence cards
- cue-response charts
- cooperation reflection slips

### Assessment Evidence

- responds to cues
- moves with control
- participates cooperatively and safely

## Technology: Digital Citizenship and Algorithmic Thinking

### Student Experience

- order steps in a process
- follow and create simple algorithms
- identify one safe and respectful digital-use norm

### Teacher Moves

- connect algorithms to classroom procedures and games
- keep digital-citizenship language concrete
- use unplugged sequencing before device-based tasks

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- step cards
- algorithm path pages
- safe-choice response cards

### Assessment Evidence

- orders steps correctly
- follows or creates a simple sequence
- names and applies a safe-use expectation
