## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around simple maps, places, and community participation.
- It keeps the work concrete and inclusive.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare a clear simple map with recognizable icons.
- Use community examples that reflect varied lived experiences.
- Plan prompts that ask what places are for and how people use them.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may treat maps as pictures without meaning.
- Students may rely only on familiar places from their own lives.
- Some students may need help distinguishing place from role.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask students to point to a place and describe it.
- Connect map use to real movement or school routines.
- Keep citizenship language concrete and age-appropriate.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students identify a place on the map.
- Track whether students describe one community role or responsibility.
- Note who needs visual or oral language support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use clear icons, oral discussion, and pointing.
- Avoid requiring personal disclosure about home or neighborhood details.
- Keep examples inclusive and concrete.
