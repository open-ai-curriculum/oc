## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.1.3
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.1.3.B
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.1.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will decode short-vowel CVC words by blending sounds and reread a short decodable text with improved accuracy.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - letter cards or magnetic letters
  - sound boxes or dry-erase boards
  - a short decodable sentence strip or passage using target short-vowel patterns
  - teacher word cards for modeled blending
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose one or two short-vowel patterns for the lesson
  - prepare a short word list that progresses from easy to slightly more challenging
  - preview the decodable text for awkward phrasing or pattern overload

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Review that readers can say each sound, blend across the word, and then reread smoothly.
  - Orally segment and blend two or three sample words before showing print.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students build and read a short set of CVC words with teacher modeling.
  - The teacher models left-to-right decoding and blends sounds into whole words.
  - Students then read a short decodable text containing the same patterns.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The key content is accurate sound-symbol mapping and the transfer of that work into connected text reading.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students practice decoding a few new words with partners using sound boxes or letter cards.
  - The group rereads the decodable text a second time and notices where reading sounds smoother.
  - Students briefly explain one word they solved and what helped them read it.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student decodes at least one target word accurately.
  - Students reread the short text with improved accuracy or reduced prompting.
  - The teacher listens for whether students blend sounds rather than guessing from the first letter.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - Students name one decoding move they used.
  - The class reflects on why rereading helped the text become easier to read.
