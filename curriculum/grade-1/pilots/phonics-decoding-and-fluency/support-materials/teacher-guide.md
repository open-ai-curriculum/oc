## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around accurate decoding, short-vowel pattern recognition, and fluent rereading.
- It keeps foundational-skills work anchored in explicit instruction and connected-text reading rather than isolated drill alone.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the decoding target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Use a tight set of words that share the target pattern and avoid unnecessary exceptions.
- Preview the decodable text to make sure it is readable, coherent, and not overloaded with new patterns.
- Prepare manipulatives or writing tools so students can move between oral and printed practice smoothly.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may guess from the first letter rather than blend through the word.
- Students may confuse similar short-vowel sounds.
- Some students may decode a word correctly in isolation but lose accuracy in connected text.
- Some students may treat fluent reading as fast reading instead of accurate rereading.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Prompt students to say each sound and blend across the word before offering the answer.
- Use minimal pairs or near pairs carefully to highlight vowel differences.
- Keep fluency feedback focused on accuracy first, then smoother rereading.
- Ask students to explain what they noticed about one solved word.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students blend left to right or jump to guessing.
- Track which vowel patterns cause confusion.
- Note whether rereading improves accuracy, not just pace.
- Watch which students need oral rehearsal, manipulatives, or partner support to succeed.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use clear print, manipulatives, teacher modeling, and short practice rounds.
- Allow oral response, tile movement, tracked reading, and supported rereading.
- Distinguish language comprehension needs from decoding needs where possible.
- Keep examples and family-facing expectations inclusive and realistic.
