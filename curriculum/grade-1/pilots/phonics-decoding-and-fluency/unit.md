## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students learn to map sounds to letters in simple one-syllable words, decode short-vowel CVC words accurately, and reread connected text to build fluency.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Grade 1 readers need explicit foundational-skills instruction so they can move from supported word-solving toward more independent reading of simple text.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- letters and sounds work together in predictable ways in many simple words
- accurate decoding helps readers understand what they read
- rereading can make reading smoother, more accurate, and more confident

### Students should know:

- common short-vowel sounds in simple CVC patterns
- that readers can blend sounds from left to right to read a word
- that fluent reading is not speed alone; it includes accuracy, phrasing, and attention to the text

### Students should be able to do:

- decode Grade 1-appropriate short-vowel CVC words by blending sounds
- distinguish between a few closely related vowel patterns with teacher support
- reread a short decodable sentence or passage with improved accuracy and smoothness
- use oral language to explain how a word was solved

## Essential Questions

- How do letters help us read a word we do not know right away?
- What do readers do when a word does not make sense on the first try?
- What makes rereading useful?

## Assessment Evidence

### Summative evidence

- Students complete a brief teacher-observed decoding and rereading check in which they:
  - read a short list of CVC words with short-vowel patterns
  - reread a short decodable passage or sentence set
  - explain one decoding move they used on a word

### Formative evidence

- sound-by-sound blending during whole-group modeling
- partner decoding practice
- teacher notes on vowel confusions and blending accuracy
- repeated reading of a short decodable text

## Lesson Sequence

### Lesson 1

- objective: Decode short-vowel CVC words by blending sounds and reread a short decodable text with improved accuracy.
- key activities:
  - warm up with phoneme segmentation and blending
  - model left-to-right decoding of short-vowel CVC words
  - read and reread a short decodable text containing target patterns
- evidence of learning:
  - students accurately decode several target words
  - students reread a short text with fewer errors on the second reading

### Lesson 2

- objective: Compare and decode words with more than one short-vowel pattern.
- key activities:
  - sort words by vowel pattern
  - read word pairs that differ by one vowel
  - discuss how vowel changes affect the spoken word
- evidence of learning:
  - students notice the changed vowel sound
  - students read matched word pairs with teacher support

### Lesson 3

- objective: Apply decoding to short connected text and monitor for accuracy.
- key activities:
  - read a short decodable passage
  - pause and fix miscues that change the word
  - reread for smoother phrasing
- evidence of learning:
  - students self-correct or respond to prompts to fix an inaccurate word
  - students demonstrate more accurate rereading

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Representation supports:
  - use sound boxes, letter cards, visual mouth cues when helpful, and clearly spaced print
- Engagement supports:
  - keep practice brisk, include choral response and partner turns, and use short success-oriented practice rounds
- Action/expression supports:
  - allow pointing, moving tiles, oral blending, tracked reading, and teacher scribing of explained strategies
- Language/accessibility considerations:
  - distinguish decoding difficulty from language knowledge, and support multilingual learners with explicit oral modeling and clear vocabulary for routines
- Inclusive examples or framing notes:
  - use decodable content that avoids narrow stereotypes and respects varied learner experiences

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - isolated drill that never connects to real reading
  - overemphasis on speed rather than accuracy
  - confusing or poorly sequenced word sets
  - decodable texts that are technically aligned but awkward or meaningless
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - word-list construction
  - vowel-pattern progression
  - decodable text quality
  - fluency prompts and feedback language
