## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around asking and answering questions, identifying key details, and naming what a text is mostly about.
- It keeps the work grounded in the actual informational text rather than in generic topic talk.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the reading and discussion target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose a short informational text with a clear topic, helpful illustrations, and coherent details.
- Preidentify the main topic and a small number of important supporting details.
- Prepare question cards and a simple topic/detail organizer before the lesson.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may answer from background knowledge instead of from the text.
- Students may name an interesting detail without identifying the main topic.
- Some students may treat every fact as equally important.
- Some students may use the picture alone without connecting it back to the text.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask students to show which part of the text or picture helped them answer.
- Model how to tell the difference between the topic and one detail.
- Keep question sets short and purposeful.
- Revoice answers to strengthen topic vocabulary and precision.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students answer from the text or from guessing.
- Track whether students can state what the text is mostly about.
- Note whether students connect a detail back to that topic.
- Watch which students need visual, oral, or partner support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use repeated reading, clear visuals, and explicit question routines.
- Allow oral response, pointing, topic/detail cards, and simple notes or drawings where appropriate.
- Keep vocabulary supports tied directly to the text.
- Choose inclusive informational topics and avoid narrow assumptions in examples and family-facing guidance.
