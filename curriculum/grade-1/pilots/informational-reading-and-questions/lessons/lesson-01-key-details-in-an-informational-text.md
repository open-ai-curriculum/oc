## Standards and Objective

  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RI.1.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RI.1.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will ask and answer questions about key details in a short informational text and explain what the text is mostly about.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - short Grade 1-appropriate informational text with clear illustrations or labels
  - projected pages or chart paper
  - key-detail question cards
  - simple topic/detail organizer
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a text with a clear topic and a manageable set of key details
  - preidentify the main topic and a few details worth discussing

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Ask students what they already know about the topic.
  - Explain that good readers ask questions to learn more from a text.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students listen to or participate in a shared reading of a short informational text.
  - At planned stopping points, students answer who, what, where, and how questions tied to key details.
  - The class names one idea the text is mostly about.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central idea is that informational texts teach readers about a topic through important details.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students sort a few facts into “helps explain the topic” and “interesting but not the main point.”
  - Partners explain one answer using a picture, label, or sentence from the text.
  - The teacher models how to return to the text instead of guessing.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student answers at least one key-detail question orally, by pointing to the text or picture, or by using a simple organizer.
  - Students identify what the text is mostly about in simple language.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - Students share one detail they learned and explain why it mattered.
  - The class restates the main topic together.
