## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around observing plants and describing what they need.
- It keeps the science tied to observation and explanation rather than vocabulary alone.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare a real plant or clear images.
- Identify one or two plant features students can actually notice.
- Plan a short recording routine.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may name background knowledge instead of observations.
- Students may overgeneralize from one plant to all plants.
- Some students may need help distinguishing plant needs from parts.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask, “What do you notice?”
- Return students to visible evidence.
- Record observations in simple shared language.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students identify one plant feature or need.
- Track whether students refer to what they see.
- Note who needs visual, oral, or drawing support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use real objects, visuals, and oral language.
- Allow drawing, pointing, and speaking.
- Keep claims concrete and observable.
