## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around stating an opinion, giving a matching reason, and rereading writing for clarity.
- It keeps the writing work grounded in actual choice and meaning rather than generic sentence completion.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the writing target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose a familiar topic or text that allows more than one reasonable opinion.
- Prepare one short model that clearly shows an opinion and a matching reason.
- Decide which students may need drawing, dictation, or shared-writing support before independent writing.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may confuse a reason with an unrelated detail.
- Students may copy the sentence frame without expressing a real opinion.
- Some students may struggle to move from oral language into written language.
- Some students may focus on spelling and stop generating ideas.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask, “Does that reason match what you said you think?”
- Rehearse the sentence aloud before asking students to write or dictate it.
- Keep feedback focused first on meaning and matching reasons, then on conventions.
- Model rereading to notice whether a word or idea is missing.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students can state a clear opinion.
- Track whether reasons actually support the opinion.
- Note which students need oral rehearsal, dictation, or drawing to express ideas.
- Watch whether rereading helps students clarify a word or reason.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use visible sentence frames, modeled writing, oral rehearsal, and flexible output options.
- Allow drawing, dictation, shared writing, and teacher scribing as needed.
- Keep topics inclusive and avoid assuming one preferred background or experience.
- Support multilingual learners with repeated oral language frames tied to meaning.
