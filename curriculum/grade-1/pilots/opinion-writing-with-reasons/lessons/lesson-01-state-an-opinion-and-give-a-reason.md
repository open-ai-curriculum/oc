# Lesson: State an Opinion and Give a Reason

## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.1.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.1.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will state an opinion about a familiar topic and give one reason that matches it.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - a familiar shared topic, object set, or brief text
  - chart paper or interactive writing space
  - simple opinion/reason cards or sentence strips
  - drawing and writing materials
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a familiar topic that allows more than one reasonable opinion
  - prepare one clear model opinion with matching and non-matching reasons
  - decide which students may need dictation or shared-writing support

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Invite students to share something they like or prefer and explain why.
  - Introduce the idea that writers can tell an opinion and give a reason that matches it.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students discuss a shared topic or text and listen to a modeled opinion statement.
  - The teacher models a matching reason and compares it to a non-matching reason.
  - Students orally rehearse an opinion sentence and one reason before writing or dictating.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The key content is the relationship between an opinion statement and a reason that supports it.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students sort example reasons into matching and non-matching categories.
  - Partners say an opinion and reason aloud using a sentence frame if needed.
  - Students draw and write or dictate a short opinion piece with one reason.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student states an opinion clearly.
  - Each student provides at least one reason that fits the opinion.
  - The teacher checks whether the reason actually supports the opinion instead of drifting off topic.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - Students share one opinion-and-reason pair.
  - The class reflects on how to tell whether a reason matches the opinion.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes
