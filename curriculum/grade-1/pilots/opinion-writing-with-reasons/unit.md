## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students learn to state an opinion about a familiar topic or text, give one or more simple reasons, and reread their writing to make it clearer.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Grade 1 writers need explicit support to move from oral preference statements into simple written opinions with reasons. This unit builds early composing confidence, sentence-level clarity, and shared discussion around ideas.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- opinion writing tells what the writer thinks and why
- reasons help a reader understand the opinion
- writers can say ideas aloud, draw, write, and reread to improve clarity

### Students should know:

- the meaning of opinion, reason, and detail
- that a writing piece can begin with a clear opinion statement
- that rereading helps writers notice missing words or unclear parts

### Students should be able to do:

- state an opinion about a familiar topic or text
- give one or more simple reasons that match the opinion
- use drawing, dictation, or writing to develop the piece
- reread a short writing piece and add or clarify a word or idea with support

## Essential Questions

- How do writers tell what they think?
- What makes a reason match an opinion?
- How can rereading help make writing clearer?

## Assessment Evidence

### Summative evidence

- Students produce a short opinion piece in which they:
  - state an opinion on a familiar topic or text
  - include at least one matching reason
  - reread the piece with teacher support and revise one part for clarity

### Formative evidence

- oral rehearsal of opinion sentences
- turn-and-talk about reasons
- teacher notes on whether reasons match the stated opinion
- quick shared or interactive writing practice

## Lesson Sequence

### Lesson 1

- objective: State an opinion about a familiar topic and give one reason that matches it.
- key activities:
  - discuss a shared topic or text and model an opinion sentence
  - sort matching and non-matching reasons with teacher support
  - orally rehearse and then write or dictate an opinion with one reason
- evidence of learning:
  - students state an opinion clearly
  - students give a reason that fits the opinion

### Lesson 2

- objective: Add another reason or detail that strengthens an opinion piece.
- key activities:
  - reread a model opinion piece
  - discuss what makes a reason strong and connected
  - add a second reason or supporting detail with teacher support
- evidence of learning:
  - students extend a piece beyond a single unsupported statement
  - students choose a reason connected to the opinion

### Lesson 3

- objective: Reread and revise a short opinion piece for clarity.
- key activities:
  - reread writing aloud with a partner or teacher
  - notice where a word or reason is missing
  - revise by adding or clarifying a word, phrase, or reason
- evidence of learning:
  - students identify one place to improve a piece
  - students revise with support to make meaning clearer

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Representation supports:
  - use modeled writing, shared oral rehearsal, picture supports, and visible sentence frames
- Engagement supports:
  - choose familiar high-interest topics, allow talk before writing, and keep writing tasks short and purposeful
- Action/expression supports:
  - allow drawing, dictation, shared writing, oral rehearsal, and teacher-supported sentence construction
- Language/accessibility considerations:
  - support multilingual learners with repeated oral frames, vocabulary support, and flexible pathways from speaking to writing
- Inclusive examples or framing notes:
  - use topics and texts that avoid narrow cultural assumptions and allow multiple reasonable opinions

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - writing that becomes fill-in-the-blank without real choice or meaning
  - reasons that do not actually match the opinion
  - overcorrection of mechanics that disrupts idea development
  - prompts that privilege one background or experience
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - selected writing prompts or source texts
  - sentence frames and model pieces
  - revision expectations for early Grade 1 writers
