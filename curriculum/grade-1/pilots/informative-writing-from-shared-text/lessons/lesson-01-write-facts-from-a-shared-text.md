# Lesson: Write Facts From a Shared Text

## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.1.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RI.1.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will name a shared topic and write or dictate one fact learned from a shared informational text.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - a short shared informational text with clear topic and details
  - chart paper or interactive writing space
  - simple topic/fact organizer
  - drawing and writing materials
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a shared text with a clear topic and a few memorable facts
  - prepare one brief model that names the topic and gives a matching fact
  - decide which students may need dictation, drawing, or shared-writing support

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Revisit the shared text and ask what the class learned about the topic.
  - Introduce the idea that writers can teach others by writing facts about something they learned.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students reread or revisit a short shared informational text with teacher support.
  - The teacher models naming the topic and stating one fact from the text.
  - Students orally rehearse a topic-and-fact sentence before writing or dictating.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The key content is the relationship between a shared topic and factual information drawn from reading or discussion.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students sort example statements into “fact about the topic” and “not a fact about the topic.”
  - Partners say a topic-and-fact sentence aloud using a frame if needed.
  - Students draw and write or dictate a short informative sentence or pair of sentences.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student names the shared topic clearly.
  - Each student provides at least one fact that matches the topic and shared text.
  - The teacher checks whether the student is writing from the shared learning rather than from unrelated background knowledge.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - Students share one fact they wrote.
  - The class reflects on how they knew the fact belonged with the topic.
