## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around naming a topic, stating matching facts, and rereading writing for clarity.
- It keeps the writing work grounded in shared reading and learning rather than copied sentences or unsupported claims.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the writing target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose a short shared informational text with a clear topic and a few memorable details.
- Prepare one short model that names the topic and gives a matching fact.
- Decide which students may need drawing, dictation, or shared-writing support before independent writing.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may confuse a fact with an opinion or personal preference.
- Students may copy a sentence without understanding what it means.
- Some students may struggle to move from oral discussion into written factual language.
- Some students may add unrelated details from background knowledge rather than from the shared learning.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask, “How do we know that fact belongs with the topic?”
- Rehearse the topic-and-fact sentence aloud before asking students to write or dictate it.
- Keep feedback focused first on factual match and clarity, then on conventions.
- Model rereading to notice whether a topic or detail is missing.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students can name the shared topic clearly.
- Track whether facts actually match the topic and shared text.
- Note which students need oral rehearsal, dictation, or drawing to express knowledge.
- Watch whether rereading helps students clarify a fact or label.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use visible topic/fact frames, shared-text visuals, modeled writing, and flexible output options.
- Allow drawing, dictation, shared writing, and teacher scribing as needed.
- Keep texts and topics inclusive and avoid narrow assumptions.
- Support multilingual learners with repeated oral frames tied directly to the shared topic.
