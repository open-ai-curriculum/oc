## Alignment Notes

- This note supports the unit focus on naming a topic and giving a fact that matches it.
- It is written to help families reinforce classroom talk-to-writing routines in a realistic way.

## Family-Facing Guidance

- Ask your child to tell you about something learned from a shared book or topic at school.
- Ask, “What is the topic?” and “What is one fact you learned?”
- Help your child say one clear fact that matches the topic.
- If your child wants, they can draw the topic and say or write one sentence.
- Keep practice short. A brief conversation is enough.

## Suggested Family Prompt

- “What did you learn, and what is one fact about it?”

## Accessibility Notes

- Keep home practice brief and low-pressure.
- Encourage oral talk even if a child is not ready for much independent writing.
- Avoid assuming access to special materials beyond paper, pencil, or conversation time.
