## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around solving addition and subtraction situations with objects, drawings, and equations.
- It keeps the mathematics grounded in reasoning, not answer-only completion.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare counters, drawing space, and one or two clear story situations.
- Plan a model showing how to represent a story with objects or drawings.
- Choose prompts that ask students how they know.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may compute without representing the story.
- Students may confuse joining and separating situations.
- Some students may state an answer without explaining the representation.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask students to show the story with objects or drawings.
- Revoice comparisons between strategies.
- Keep the focus on the mathematical situation, not decorative story details.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students represent the story accurately.
- Track whether students explain how they found the answer.
- Note who needs support moving from objects to drawings or equations.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use manipulatives, oral language, and drawings.
- Reduce reading load not central to the math.
- Allow multiple valid representations.
