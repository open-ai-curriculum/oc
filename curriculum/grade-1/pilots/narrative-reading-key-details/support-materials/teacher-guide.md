## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around identifying key details, answering text-based questions, and retelling a narrative story clearly.
- It keeps the work grounded in actual text evidence rather than generic recall or guesswork.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the comprehension target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose a short narrative text with a clear sequence and concrete illustrations.
- Prewrite a small set of questions that target key details and not trivial facts.
- Prepare sequence cards or a short retell organizer before the lesson.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may repeat a favorite detail rather than a key detail.
- Students may answer from background knowledge instead of the story.
- Some students may retell every small event instead of the important sequence.
- Some students may need support connecting a picture detail to a verbal answer.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask students to show where they found the answer in the story or picture.
- Model the difference between an important detail and an extra detail.
- Keep retell expectations short and clear: beginning, middle, end, plus one or two key details.
- Revoice student answers to strengthen story vocabulary and precision.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students answer from the text or from guessing.
- Track whether students can identify beginning, middle, and end.
- Note whether students use one or more accurate key details in a retell.
- Watch which students need visual, oral, or partner support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use repeated reading, clear visuals, and explicit question routines.
- Allow oral response, pointing, detail cards, and simple notes or drawings where appropriate.
- Keep language supports tied directly to the text.
- Choose inclusive stories and avoid narrow assumptions in examples and family-facing guidance.
