## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.1.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.1.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify key details in a narrative text and use those details to answer questions about what happened.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - short Grade 1-appropriate narrative text with supportive illustrations
  - chart paper or projected text pages
  - key-detail question cards
  - optional detail sorting cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a text with a clear sequence and concrete details
  - preidentify a small number of key questions tied directly to the story

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Ask students what kinds of details help them remember a story.
  - Introduce the idea that some details are key because they help explain what happened.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students listen to or participate in a shared reading of a short narrative text.
  - At planned stopping points, students answer questions about who, what, and where using the story and illustrations.
  - Students help identify a detail that is important to the story.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central idea is that key details help readers understand the events of a story and answer questions accurately.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students sort a few details into “important to the story” and “not the main point.”
  - Partners explain one answer using a picture or a remembered line from the text.
  - The teacher models how to return to the story rather than guessing from memory alone.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student answers at least one key-detail question orally, by pointing to a picture, or by selecting a detail card.
  - The teacher checks whether the student’s answer matches the story.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - Students share one key detail that helped them understand the story.
  - The class briefly reviews why that detail mattered.
