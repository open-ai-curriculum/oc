## Alignment Notes

- It supports, rather than replaces, the reasoning students must do about partitioning, overlap, and product size.
- It is aligned to CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.5.NF.B.4 and supports interpretation for CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.5.NF.B.5.

## Student Directions

### 1. Name the problem

- Write the multiplication expression: `______ x ______`

### 2. Name the whole

- What is one whole in your diagram?
- Write or say: “The whole is ____________________.”

### 3. Partition the whole

- Partition the whole to show the first fraction.
- Partition the same whole to show the second fraction.
- Check: Are all parts equal in size?

### 4. Shade and label

- Shade one fraction in one direction.
- Shade the second fraction in another direction.
- Circle or outline the overlap region.
- Label the overlap as the product.

### 5. Explain the product

- Complete the stem:
  - “The product is ______ because the overlap shows ______ out of ______ equal parts.”

### 6. Check whether the answer is reasonable

- Is the factor less than 1, equal to 1, or greater than 1?
- Should the product be smaller, the same size, or larger than the starting quantity?
- Explain:
  - “I know my answer is reasonable because ____________________.”

## Optional Sentence Supports

- “I partitioned the whole into ______ equal parts.”
- “The overlap shows a part of a part because ____________________.”
- “Multiplying by ______ changes the quantity by ____________________.”

## Accessibility Notes

- The scaffold uses short, sequenced prompts and repeated structure.
- It can be read aloud or completed orally before writing.
- Teachers can provide a pre-drawn rectangle while keeping the same reasoning demands.
- The support should remain editable and printable for low-bandwidth use.
