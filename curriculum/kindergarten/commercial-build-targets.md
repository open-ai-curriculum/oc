# Kindergarten Commercial Build Targets

## Purpose

Set measurable annual production targets for a commercially credible kindergarten build.

## Interpretation

These are repository build targets, not claims that current artifacts already meet them.

The targets are designed to prevent the team from calling a strand sampler or packet compilation a full-year program.

## Annual Planning Assumptions

- school year length: 36 instructional weeks
- daily literacy block and daily math block
- science and social studies taught in recurring module bands
- arts, health, PE, and technology taught in recurring weekly or module rotations
- some subjects rely more heavily on teacher-led activity and reproducibles than on bound student-book pages

## Core Subject Notes

### ELA target interpretation

The ELA target assumes the kindergarten literacy program will include:

- read-aloud and oral-language work
- foundational skills
- phonological awareness
- letter-sound and early phonics progression
- comprehension and discussion routines
- early writing
- family-connected practice

ELA should become the first true commercial-grade subject prototype.

### Math target interpretation

The math target assumes:

- counting and cardinality
- comparison
- addition and subtraction foundations
- teen numbers
- shapes and geometry
- measurement and data routines
- sustained practice and center work

Math should be the second full commercial-grade subject prototype.

## Lighter-Form Subject Notes

Science, social studies, arts, health, PE, and technology do not all need the same bound-book density as ELA and math, but they do need:

- enough annual lesson volume to constitute a real year plan
- enough teacher-use material to stand on their own
- enough student-facing assets to support participation and documentation

## Benchmark Expectations

Across the kindergarten year, the program should eventually include:

- 3 benchmark windows in ELA
- 3 benchmark windows in Math
- recurring observational checkpoints in science, social studies, arts, health, PE, and technology
- end-of-module or end-of-unit evidence opportunities where appropriate

## Minimum Commercial-Credibility Threshold

The kindergarten build should not be described as commercially credible until:

- ELA meets at least 75% of its annual target inventory
- Math meets at least 75% of its annual target inventory
- every other subject reaches at least 60% of its annual target inventory
- the teacher-edition and reproducible systems are strong enough that teachers do not need to invent major missing assets

## Current State Note

The current repository kindergarten artifacts are substantially below these targets. The targets exist to guide the next build phases, not to certify current completeness.
