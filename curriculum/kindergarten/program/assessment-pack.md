# Kindergarten Assessment Pack Draft

## Volume Metadata

- Volume title: Kindergarten Assessment Pack Draft
- Program layer: assessment pack
- Grade / grade band: Kindergarten
- Review status: Draft

## Purpose

Provide a real assembled kindergarten assessment-pack draft built from packet assessment checks and assessment keys.

## How To Use This Volume

- Keep assessments brief, oral, visual, and low-stakes.
- Use them after instruction, not as isolated gatekeeping tasks.
- Pair assessment evidence with observation notes and classroom work.

## Assessment Access Notes

- Offer pointing, picture selection, gesture, oral language, manipulatives, and teacher-recorded response when those formats fit the target.
- Reduce language load that is not essential to the skill being assessed.
- Use [reproducibles.md](/Volumes/data/development/oc/curriculum/kindergarten/program/reproducibles.md) and [exemplar-student-responses.md](/Volumes/data/development/oc/curriculum/kindergarten/program/exemplar-student-responses.md) to support administration and interpretation.

## Assessment 1: Book Handling and Print Concepts

### Teacher Directions

- Show the student a familiar book or page.
- Ask the student to show the front cover.
- Ask the student to show where reading begins.
- Ask for one detail from the read-aloud.

### Evidence to Record

- identifies front cover
- identifies reading start
- gives one relevant detail

## Assessment 2: Story Retell

### Teacher Directions

- Give the student three event cards from a familiar story.
- Ask the student to place them in order.
- Ask the student to retell what happened.

### Evidence to Record

- sequences events
- communicates at least part of the retell meaningfully

## Assessment 3: Rhyme Listening

### Teacher Directions

- Say two word pairs aloud.
- Ask the student which pair rhymes.

### Evidence to Record

- identifies rhyme accurately
- responds through speech, gesture, or picture selection

## Assessment 4: Letter Recognition

### Teacher Directions

- Show a few taught focus letters.
- Ask the student to name one letter.
- Ask the student to match an uppercase/lowercase pair.

### Evidence to Record

- recognizes a taught letter
- matches a correct letter pair

## Assessment 5: Counting and Quantity

### Teacher Directions

- Present a small set of objects.
- Ask the student to count the set.
- Ask, "How many?"
- If appropriate, present a second small set and ask which group has more.

### Evidence to Record

- one-to-one counting
- names the total
- compares small sets when that extension is used

## Assessment 6: Weather Observation

### Teacher Directions

- Show the day's weather or a weather image.
- Ask the student what they notice.
- Ask the student to choose a matching symbol.
- If appropriate, compare today to another day or image.

### Evidence to Record

- identifies a weather condition
- matches the symbol or record correctly
- contributes one observation tied to visible evidence

## Assessment 7: Community Roles

### Teacher Directions

- Show a picture of a helper.
- Ask who the person is.
- Ask how the person helps.
- Ask where the helper might work or help.

### Evidence to Record

- identifies the helper
- explains the role
- connects the helper to a familiar place or context

## Assessment 8: Creative Response

### Teacher Directions

- Give a short prompt.
- Ask the student to create a quick response.
- Ask the student to share one thing about it.

### Evidence to Record

- creates in response to the prompt
- shares one feature or idea

## Assessment 9: Feelings and Safe Choices

### Teacher Directions

- Show a feeling card.
- Ask the student to identify the feeling.
- Ask for one healthy or safe response.

### Evidence to Record

- names or shows the feeling
- identifies a safe response

## Assessment 10: Movement and Coordination

### Teacher Directions

- Ask the student to move in a safe way.
- Give a freeze cue.
- Ask for one simple balance pose.

### Evidence to Record

- responds to cues
- demonstrates control and balance

## Assessment 11: Sequencing

### Teacher Directions

- Give the student three simple steps.
- Ask the student to put them in order.
- Ask the student to follow the sequence.
- Ask for one safe tool-use behavior if a tool routine is part of the task.

### Evidence to Record

- orders the steps
- follows the sequence
- names or shows one safe-use behavior when applicable

## Recording Guidance Across the Pack

- Mark evidence as `secure`, `developing`, or `not yet`.
- Count oral, gestural, and visual responses as valid evidence.
- If a student needs repeated prompting, record that in observation notes.

## End-of-Volume Notes

- This assessment pack is a real assembled draft, but it is not yet a final production assessment system.
- It still needs assessment review, developmental-fit review, and classroom piloting.
