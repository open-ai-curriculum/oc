# Kindergarten Non-Literacy Program Guide

## Purpose

Deepen the assembled kindergarten program across math, science, social studies, arts, health, physical education, and technology so those strands are not treated as thin add-ons to literacy.

## Status

Draft program companion. This guide expands the non-literacy program layer and should be read with the kindergarten textbook, workbook, teacher edition, grading key, and assessment pack.

## Math: Counting and Cardinality

### Student Experience

- count concrete sets with touch-and-say routines
- compare small groups visually and physically
- explain "how many" using the last number counted

### Teacher Moves

- arrange objects with clear spacing
- model one-to-one correspondence aloud
- ask students to recount when quantity is uncertain

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- count-and-circle pages
- more / fewer sorting cards
- quick oral counting strips

### Assessment Evidence

- counts each object once
- says the total accurately
- identifies which set has more when appropriate

## Science: Weather and Observation

### Student Experience

- observe daily weather conditions
- describe what is seen, felt, or noticed
- record weather with icons, pictures, or dictated language

### Teacher Moves

- use a consistent observation routine
- compare one day to another
- connect weather talk to clothing, outdoor conditions, and patterns

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- daily weather journal pages
- icon cards for sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy
- "What do you notice?" picture prompts

### Assessment Evidence

- identifies a weather condition
- contributes one real observation
- uses a symbol, drawing, or oral statement to record

## Social Studies: Family, School, and Community

### Student Experience

- name helpers at home, school, and in the community
- describe simple responsibilities
- connect roles to care, safety, and shared routines

### Teacher Moves

- use inclusive examples of families and community roles
- compare where helpers work and how they help
- use role-play and picture sorting

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- helper sort cards
- community picture matching pages
- oral sentence frames for "A ___ helps by ___."

### Assessment Evidence

- identifies a helper
- matches helper to context
- explains one way a helper supports others

## Arts: Creative Arts and Expression

### Student Experience

- create in response to color, sound, movement, or image prompts
- describe one feature of the work
- share and respond respectfully

### Teacher Moves

- model process over perfection
- provide limited but meaningful choices
- prompt students to notice color, shape, sound, rhythm, or movement

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- visual prompt cards
- music-and-movement response pages
- share-your-art reflection strips

### Assessment Evidence

- produces a prompt-connected creation
- names one artistic choice or idea
- participates safely in sharing

## Health: Healthy Habits and Feelings

### Student Experience

- identify common feelings
- connect feelings to safe and healthy responses
- practice classroom regulation routines

### Teacher Moves

- use predictable emotion vocabulary
- model breathing, asking for help, and calm-down routines
- connect body signals to feelings and choices

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- feelings cards
- safe-choice sorting sheets
- calm-plan mini pages

### Assessment Evidence

- names a feeling accurately
- identifies one safe or healthy response
- participates in a taught self-regulation routine

## Physical Education: Movement and Coordination

### Student Experience

- move, freeze, balance, and travel safely
- follow simple cues
- practice body control in shared space

### Teacher Moves

- model start/stop signals clearly
- keep movement tasks short and repeatable
- reinforce body awareness and safe spacing

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- movement reflection cards
- balance challenge visuals
- cue-following picture strips

### Assessment Evidence

- responds to movement cues
- stops safely
- shows balance or coordination with support appropriate to the task

## Technology: Digital Tools and Sequencing

### Student Experience

- follow simple first-next-last patterns
- use an age-appropriate digital tool with guidance
- identify simple safe-use norms

### Teacher Moves

- model one step at a time
- keep device use brief and visible
- connect sequencing to non-digital routines as well

### Workbook and Reproducible Focus

- picture-sequencing strips
- safe tool-use posters
- first-next-last mini tasks

### Assessment Evidence

- orders a simple sequence
- follows a basic tool-use routine
- names one safe or respectful technology behavior
