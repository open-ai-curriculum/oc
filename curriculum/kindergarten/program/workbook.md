# Kindergarten Workbook Draft

## Volume Metadata

- Volume title: Kindergarten Workbook Draft
- Program layer: workbook
- Grade / grade band: Kindergarten
- Review status: Draft

## Purpose

Provide a real kindergarten practice-book draft assembled from packet classroom assignments and home-practice tasks.

## How To Use This Volume

- Use these pages after shared instruction, not before it.
- Keep practice short, concrete, and teacher-supported.
- Accept oral, gestural, drawn, and manipulable responses where appropriate.

## Section 1: Books and Print Practice

### Practice Page 1: Show the Book Parts

- Circle the front cover in a picture of a book.
- Point to the title with a partner.
- Draw a line to where the book opens.

### Practice Page 2: Show Where Reading Starts

- Look at a short line of large print.
- Mark where reading begins.
- Trace the direction your eyes move.

## Section 2: Story Retell Practice

### Practice Page 3: Put the Story in Order

- Cut or point to three story pictures.
- Put them in first, next, last order.

### Practice Page 4: Tell What Happened

- Use the sentence frame:
  - "First ___."
  - "Next ___."
  - "Last ___."

## Section 3: Rhyme Practice

### Practice Page 5: Which Words Rhyme?

- Listen to two word pairs.
- Color the pair that rhymes.

### Practice Page 6: Match the Rhyming Pictures

- Draw a line between two pictures that rhyme.

## Section 4: Letter Practice

### Practice Page 7: Find the Focus Letter

- Circle the taught letter among a small set.
- Trace the letter shape.

### Practice Page 8: Match Uppercase and Lowercase

- Draw lines between matching letter pairs.

## Section 5: Counting Practice

### Practice Page 9: Count and Tell How Many

- Count a set of up to 5 objects.
- Say or write the number with support.

### Practice Page 10: Compare Two Sets

- Look at two small groups.
- Mark which group has more.

### Practice Page 11: Show the Last Number

- Count a small set.
- Trace or circle the last number you said.
- Tell a partner, "There are ___."

## Section 6: Weather Practice

### Practice Page 11: What Is the Weather Today?

- Choose a weather icon.
- Color or mark the one that matches today.

### Practice Page 12: Weather Journal

- Draw today's weather.
- Tell one thing you notice.

### Practice Page 13: Same or Different Weather?

- Look at two weather pictures.
- Tell one thing that is the same or different.

## Section 7: Community Practice

### Practice Page 13: Match the Helper

- Match each helper to where they help: home, school, or community.

### Practice Page 14: How Do They Help?

- Complete the frame:
  - "A ___ helps by ___."

### Practice Page 15: Match the Place

- Match a helper to a place like school, home, library, or park.

## Section 8: Creative Practice

### Practice Page 15: Create from a Prompt

- Draw, color, or collage something from a simple prompt.

### Practice Page 16: Share One Thing

- Finish the sentence:
  - "I made ___."

### Practice Page 17: Choose a Material

- Look at art materials.
- Circle what you used or want to use.
- Tell why.

## Section 9: Feelings and Health Practice

### Practice Page 17: Name the Feeling

- Look at feeling faces.
- Match each face to a feeling word or symbol.

### Practice Page 18: Choose a Safe Response

- Draw a line from the feeling to a healthy or safe choice.

### Practice Page 19: Healthy Habit Sort

- Sort picture cards into healthy habit and not a healthy habit.

## Section 10: Movement Practice

### Practice Page 19: Move and Freeze Reflection

- Circle the picture that shows safe stopping.

### Practice Page 20: Balance Reflection

- Mark the pose you tried today.
- Tell how your body felt.

### Practice Page 21: Safe Space Check

- Circle the picture that shows safe movement space.

## Section 11: Sequencing Practice

### Practice Page 21: Put the Steps in Order

- Number three simple pictures: 1, 2, 3.

### Practice Page 22: Tell First, Next, Last

- Use the frame:
  - "First ___."
  - "Next ___."
  - "Last ___."

### Practice Page 23: Safe Tool Steps

- Put pictures of tool-use steps in order.
- Point to the step that shows safe hands.

## Home Practice Strip

- Read a book and show where reading starts.
- Retell a familiar story.
- Play a rhyme game.
- Notice letters around you.
- Count small objects.
- Look at the weather.
- Name a helper.
- Make and share something creative.
- Name a feeling and a safe choice.
- Move and freeze.
- Talk about steps in order.
- Use tools carefully with a grown-up.

## End-of-Volume Notes

- This workbook draft is intentionally light and teacher-mediated.
- It is a source draft for fuller printable student pages, not yet a final designed workbook.
