## Chapter 1: Read-Alouds and Print Concepts

### Lesson Focus

Students learn book parts, where reading begins, and how to answer a simple read-aloud question.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Hold up the closed book and ask what students notice.
- Model front cover, title, author, illustrator, and where reading starts.
- Pause during the read-aloud to ask a key-detail question.

### Teacher Language

- "Show me the front cover."
- "Show me where I start reading."
- "What do you notice in the picture?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student distinguish cover, title, and page?
- Can the student locate where reading begins?
- Can the student respond to one key-detail prompt?

### Accessibility Notes

- Accept pointing, gestures, picture support, and short oral responses.
- Keep language brief and predictable.

### Family Connection

Send home a short note asking families to look at any book together and show where reading starts.

## Chapter 2: Oral Language and Story Retell

### Lesson Focus

Students retell key events from a familiar story in order.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Revisit a familiar story.
- Model retelling with first, next, and last.
- Use event cards and partner retell.

### Teacher Language

- "What happened first?"
- "What happened next?"
- "What happened last?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student place events in order?
- Can the student communicate at least part of the retell using words, gestures, or pictures?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use puppets, picture cards, and shared retell.
- Accept partial oral retells.

### Family Connection

Ask families to retell a familiar story or daily event using first, next, and last.

## Chapter 3: Phonological Awareness and Rhyme

### Lesson Focus

Students hear and identify rhyming words in oral language.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Read or chant rhyme-rich lines aloud.
- Emphasize ending sounds.
- Have students clap, sort, or signal when they hear rhyme.

### Teacher Language

- "Do these words sound the same at the end?"
- "Which two words rhyme?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student identify a rhyming pair?
- Does the student participate in the oral sound routine?

### Accessibility Notes

- Keep the lesson oral and playful.
- Avoid print-based demands during rhyme work.

### Family Connection

Invite families to play a simple spoken rhyme game at home.

## Chapter 4: Letter Recognition and Alphabet Knowledge

### Lesson Focus

Students recognize and name a small set of taught letters and match uppercase and lowercase forms.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Introduce a few focus letters only.
- Name, trace, and compare the letters.
- Match uppercase to lowercase with tactile or visual support.

### Teacher Language

- "What letter is this?"
- "Can you find the matching letter?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student identify one or more focus letters?
- Can the student match a letter pair?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use tactile letters, movement tracing, and oral repetition.
- Limit visual clutter.

### Family Connection

Ask families to notice one or two familiar letters in books or signs.

## Chapter 5: Counting and Cardinality

### Lesson Focus

Students count objects accurately and say how many are in a set.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Model one-to-one counting slowly.
- Reinforce that the last number said tells how many.
- Compare two small sets if appropriate.

### Teacher Language

- "Touch each object once."
- "How many are there?"
- "Which group has more?"

### Observation Priorities

- Does the student count each object once?
- Does the student use the last number as the total?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use manipulatives, spacing, and oral modeling.
- Reduce set size when needed.

### Family Connection

Invite families to count small household objects together.

## Chapter 6: Weather and Observation

### Lesson Focus

Students observe the weather and help record what they notice.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Observe outside or use a weather image.
- Name a visible condition.
- Add the observation to a class chart.

### Teacher Language

- "What do you notice?"
- "What is the weather like today?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student identify a weather condition?
- Can the student contribute to the class chart?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use weather icons and gestures.
- Accept drawing, pointing, or oral description.

### Family Connection

Ask families to look outside together and name the weather.

## Chapter 7: Family, School, and Community

### Lesson Focus

Students identify helpers and explain how they help at home, school, or in the community.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Show pictures of helpers.
- Sort helpers by context.
- Ask how each person helps.

### Teacher Language

- "Who is this?"
- "How does this person help?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student identify a helper?
- Can the student describe a helping role?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use inclusive community examples.
- Accept pointing and short phrases.

### Family Connection

Ask families to talk about one helper they know.

## Chapter 8: Creative Arts and Expression

### Lesson Focus

Students create an artistic response and share one feature or idea.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Give one simple prompt.
- Allow drawing, collage, movement, or rhythm.
- Invite short sharing.

### Teacher Language

- "Show me what you made."
- "Tell me one thing about it."

### Observation Priorities

- Does the student create in response to the prompt?
- Can the student share one feature of the work?

### Accessibility Notes

- Offer multiple modes of expression.
- Keep materials simple and open-ended.

### Family Connection

Encourage a simple home drawing, song, or movement response.

## Chapter 9: Healthy Habits and Feelings

### Lesson Focus

Students name a feeling and identify a healthy or safe response.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Use feeling cards or visuals.
- Model a safe response.
- Practice response language or gestures.

### Teacher Language

- "How does this person feel?"
- "What is a safe choice?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student name a feeling?
- Can the student identify a healthy response?

### Accessibility Notes

- Keep examples concrete and calm.
- Accept picture-based or gestural responses.

### Family Connection

Invite families to talk about feelings and calming choices.

## Chapter 10: Movement and Coordination

### Lesson Focus

Students move safely, stop on cue, and balance briefly.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Model movement expectations clearly.
- Practice freeze cues.
- Introduce a simple balance pose.

### Teacher Language

- "Move."
- "Freeze."
- "Balance."

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student move safely in space?
- Can the student stop on cue?
- Can the student attempt a balance?

### Accessibility Notes

- Offer seated or supported options.
- Adjust pace and intensity.

### Family Connection

Suggest a short move-and-freeze game at home.

## Chapter 11: Digital Tools and Sequencing

### Lesson Focus

Students order simple steps and follow them in sequence.

### Core Teaching Moves

- Model first, next, and last.
- Order picture steps.
- Follow the sequence with an unplugged or guided tool task.

### Teacher Language

- "What comes first?"
- "What comes next?"
- "What comes last?"

### Observation Priorities

- Can the student order steps?
- Can the student follow the sequence?

### Accessibility Notes

- Use picture cards and oral rehearsal.
- Keep device use optional and teacher-guided.

### Family Connection

Ask families to talk through steps in a daily routine.
