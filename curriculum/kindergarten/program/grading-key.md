Provide a real assembled kindergarten grading-key draft built from packet answer keys, scoring guidance, and assessment keys.

- Use observational evidence first.
- Treat the categories as instructional guides, not high-stakes scores.

## Section 1: Read-Alouds and Print Concepts

### What Counts as Secure

- student identifies the front cover
- student shows where reading begins
- student gives one relevant detail from the text or picture

### What Counts as Developing

- student identifies one feature with prompting
- student points near the correct reading start
- student gives a loosely related detail with support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student cannot identify the front cover
- student points randomly on the page
- student response is unrelated or absent

## Section 2: Oral Language and Story Retell

### What Counts as Secure

- student places major events in order
- student retells using speech, gestures, pictures, or props

### What Counts as Developing

- student retells part of the sequence with support
- student communicates using one event clearly but not the whole sequence

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet connect events to a meaningful order

## Section 3: Phonological Awareness and Rhyme

### What Counts as Secure

- student identifies a rhyming pair accurately

### What Counts as Developing

- student identifies rhyme with repetition or teacher support

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet distinguish rhyme reliably

## Section 4: Letter Recognition and Alphabet Knowledge

### What Counts as Secure

- student names a taught letter
- student matches uppercase and lowercase forms correctly

### What Counts as Developing

- student recognizes or matches with prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet identify the focus letters

## Section 5: Counting and Cardinality

### What Counts as Secure

- student counts each object once
- student says the final number as the total
- student can compare two small sets using counting or clear visual reasoning

### What Counts as Developing

- student counts with minor errors or needs prompting to state the total

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student cannot sustain one-to-one counting or does not connect the final number to quantity

## Section 6: Weather and Observation

### What Counts as Secure

- student identifies a relevant weather condition
- student matches the condition to a symbol or shared record
- student contributes one observation tied to what is actually seen

### What Counts as Developing

- student notices with prompting or uses only one part of the record task

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student response does not align to the observed weather

## Section 7: Family, School, and Community

### What Counts as Secure

- student identifies a helper and explains how the person helps
- student connects the helper to a familiar place such as home, school, or community

### What Counts as Developing

- student identifies the helper or gives only a partial explanation

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student response does not connect to a helper role

## Section 8: Creative Arts and Expression

### What Counts as Secure

- student creates a prompt-connected response
- student shares one feature or idea

### What Counts as Developing

- student completes one part of the task or needs prompting to share

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet respond to the prompt

## Section 9: Healthy Habits and Feelings

### What Counts as Secure

- student identifies a feeling
- student names a safe or healthy response
- student connects the response to the situation or feeling shown

### What Counts as Developing

- student completes one part or identifies a response only with prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet connect feelings to safe actions

## Section 10: Movement and Coordination

### What Counts as Secure

- student moves safely
- student freezes on cue
- student balances briefly

### What Counts as Developing

- student completes parts of the movement task with prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet demonstrate safe control

## Section 11: Digital Tools and Sequencing

### What Counts as Secure

- student orders steps correctly
- student follows the sequence
- student identifies one safe or respectful tool-use behavior

### What Counts as Developing

- student completes one part or requires prompting

### What Counts as Not Yet

- student does not yet demonstrate sequencing understanding

## Grading Notes Across Kindergarten

- Use observation, talk, gesture, and performance evidence.
- Do not over-weight written output for kindergarten learners.
- Repeated guided practice matters more than single-attempt precision.

## End-of-Volume Notes

- This grading key is a real assembled draft, but it is not yet a final assessment manual.
- It still needs assessment review, subject review, and classroom calibration.
