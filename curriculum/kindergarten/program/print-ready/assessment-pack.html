Assessment Access Notes

Offer pointing, picture selection, gesture, oral language, manipulatives, and teacher-recorded response when those formats fit the target. Reduce language load that is not essential to the skill being assessed.

Use reproducibles and exemplar student responses to support administration and interpretation.

Assessments 1-4

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
1. Book Handling and Print ConceptsShow a familiar book, ask for front cover, reading start, and one detail from the read-aloud.Identifies front cover, reading start, and gives one relevant detail.
2. Story RetellGive three event cards, ask the student to place them in order and retell what happened.Sequences events and communicates the retell meaningfully.
3. Rhyme ListeningSay two word pairs aloud and ask which pair rhymes.Identifies rhyme accurately through speech, gesture, or picture selection.
4. Letter RecognitionShow focus letters, ask the student to name one and match an uppercase/lowercase pair.Recognizes a taught letter and matches a correct letter pair.

Assessments 5-8

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
5. Counting and QuantityPresent a small set, ask the student to count and tell how many; if appropriate, compare a second set.One-to-one counting, names the total, compares small sets when used.
6. Weather ObservationShow the day's weather or image, ask what the student notices, choose a symbol, and compare days when appropriate.Identifies a weather condition, matches the symbol or record, contributes one visible observation.
7. Community RolesShow a helper, ask who the person is, how the person helps, and where the helper might work.Identifies the helper, explains the role, connects the helper to a familiar place or context.
8. Creative ResponseGive a short prompt, ask for a quick response, and ask the student to share one thing about it.Creates in response to the prompt and shares one feature or idea.

Assessments 9-11

AssessmentTeacher DirectionsEvidence to Record
9. Feelings and Safe ChoicesShow a feeling card, ask the student to identify the feeling and give one healthy or safe response.Names or shows the feeling and identifies a safe response.
10. Movement and CoordinationAsk the student to move safely, freeze on cue, and show one simple balance pose.Responds to cues and demonstrates control and balance.
11. SequencingGive three simple steps, ask the student to order and follow them, and ask for one safe tool-use behavior if applicable.Orders the steps, follows the sequence, names or shows one safe-use behavior.
