Chapter 1: Books and Print

Page 1: A Book Has Parts

Books help us read stories and learn new things.

  • a front cover
  • a title
  • pages

We open a book and get ready to read.

Page 2: Where Reading Starts

Readers look for where the words begin. We start in a special place on the page and move across the line as we read.

Talk About It
  • Show the front cover.
  • Point to the title.
  • Show where reading begins.

Chapter 2: Stories Can Be Retold

Page 3: First, Next, Last

Stories have events. We can remember what happened first, next, and last.

Page 4: Tell the Story Again

You can retell with words, pictures, and gestures. Retelling helps us remember and understand.

Talk About It
  • What happened first?
  • What happened next?
  • What happened last?

Chapter 3: Listening for Rhyme

Page 5: Some Words Sound Alike

Some words sound the same at the end. Those words rhyme.

Page 6: Hear the Ending Sound

Cat, hat. Hop, mop. Sun, ball. Which words rhyme?

Talk About It
  • Say two rhyming words.
  • Clap when you hear a rhyme.
Kindergarten Textbook, page 5

Chapter 4: Letters Have Names and Shapes

Page 7: Letters Look Different

Letters have names. Letters have shapes. Some letters are uppercase. Some letters are lowercase.

Page 8: Match the Letter Pair

These two letters can match: uppercase letter and lowercase letter. We look closely to notice what is the same and what is different.

Talk About It
  • Name a letter you know.
  • Match an uppercase and lowercase pair.
Kindergarten Textbook, page 6

Chapter 5: Counting Tells How Many

Page 9: Count One by One

Touch each object once. Say one number for each object.

Page 10: The Last Number Tells How Many

When you finish counting, say the last number again. That number tells how many are in the set.

Page 11: Compare Two Small Sets

We can ask which group has more, which group has fewer, or whether the groups are the same.

Talk About It
  • Count a small group.
  • Tell how many.
  • Which group has more?
Kindergarten Textbook, page 7

Chapter 6: We Can Observe Weather

Page 11: Look at the Sky

Scientists look closely. We can notice sun, clouds, rain, and wind.

Page 12: Record What You Notice

We can use words, pictures, and symbols to show today’s weather.

Page 13: Weather Can Change

Some days are sunny. Some days are cloudy or rainy. We can compare today to yesterday and notice what changed.

Talk About It
  • What is the weather like today?
  • What helped you know?
Kindergarten Textbook, page 8

Chapter 7: People Help in Communities

Page 13: Helpers Are Everywhere

People help at home, school, and in the community. Helpers do important jobs.

Page 14: Roles and Responsibilities

A teacher helps students learn. A caregiver helps children grow. A firefighter helps keep people safe.

Page 15: Places in Our Community

People help in many places such as homes, schools, libraries, and parks.

Talk About It
  • Name one helper.
  • Tell how that person helps.

Chapter 8: We Can Create and Share

Page 15: Art Can Show an Idea

We can create with drawing, color, music, and movement.

Page 16: Share Your Work

When we share, we can say what we made, what we noticed, and what it means to us.

Page 17: Art Can Use Many Materials

Artists can use crayons, paper, paint, music, and movement. Each material helps us create in a different way.

Talk About It
  • Make something simple.
  • Share one part of your work.
Kindergarten Textbook, page 10

Chapter 9: Feelings and Healthy Choices

Page 17: We Can Name Feelings

People can feel happy, sad, mad, and scared.

Page 18: We Can Make Safe Choices

When we have a big feeling, we can breathe slowly, ask for help, or take a calm break.

Page 19: Healthy Habits Help Our Bodies

Healthy habits include washing hands, resting, eating healthy foods, and moving our bodies.

Talk About It
  • Name a feeling.
  • Name one safe choice.
Kindergarten Textbook, page 11

Chapter 10: Move with Control

Page 19: Our Bodies Can Move

We can walk, hop, stretch, and balance.

Page 20: Stop and Freeze

Safe movers listen for a cue. We can stop. We can freeze. We can balance with control.

Page 21: Share Space Safely

When we move with others, we keep our bodies in our own space, watch where we are going, and slow down when we need to.

Talk About It
  • Show one safe movement.
  • Freeze on cue.
  • Hold a balance pose.
Kindergarten Textbook, page 12

Chapter 11: Follow the Steps in Order

Page 21: First, Next, Last

Some tasks have steps. We can put steps in order.

Page 22: Follow a Sequence

When we know the order, we can finish the task. We can say first, next, and last.

Page 23: Tools Need Safe Steps

Some tools are digital tools. We use them by listening to directions, taking turns, and using careful hands.

Talk About It
  • Put three steps in order.
  • Follow the steps together.
