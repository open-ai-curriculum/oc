# Kindergarten Exemplar Student Responses

## Purpose

Provide assembled examples of what draft kindergarten student responses may look like across the program layer so teachers can calibrate expectations beyond answer-only completion.

## Usage Notes

- These exemplars are illustrative drafts, not normative final benchmarks.
- Kindergarten evidence may be oral, gestural, drawn, dictated, or teacher-recorded.
- Neatness and stamina should not be mistaken for understanding.

## Literacy Exemplars

### Books and Print

- Student points to the front cover and then to where reading begins.
- Student says, "Start here," while tracing left to right with a finger.

### Story Retell

- Student says, "First the girl lost the dog. Next she looked. Last she found it."
- Student uses three picture cards in order with one short oral phrase for each.

### Rhyme

- Student claps on `cat / hat` and does not clap on `sun / ball`.

### Letters

- Student names `M` and matches it to `m`.

## Non-Literacy Exemplars

### Counting and Cardinality

- Student touches four counters once each and says, "One, two, three, four. Four."

### Weather and Observation

- Student says, "Cloudy," and draws clouds in the weather journal box.

### Family, School, and Community

- Student points to a teacher picture and says, "Teacher helps us learn."

### Creative Arts and Expression

- Student draws colored lines and says, "I made a rainbow picture."

### Healthy Habits and Feelings

- Student identifies a sad face and says, "Ask for help."

### Movement and Coordination

- Student freezes when the cue stops and balances for a short count.

### Digital Tools and Sequencing

- Student places picture cards in first / next / last order and says the steps aloud.

## Teacher Reminder

- Record what the student communicates, not just what is written down.
- Note prompting level when it changes the interpretation of performance.
