## Purpose

Provide a real kindergarten student-facing textbook draft assembled from the governed packet set.

## How To Use This Volume

- Read aloud with students.
- Model responses orally, visually, and physically.
- Pair textbook pages with workbook practice and teacher-edition routines.

## Accessibility and Response Notes

- Treat oral language, gestures, pointing, drawing, and shared response as valid participation.
- Keep pages teacher-mediated; this volume is not designed for independent silent reading.
- Use visuals, repeated language, and concrete objects whenever the lesson calls for them.

## Chapter 1: Books and Print

### Page 1: A Book Has Parts

Books help us read stories and learn new things.

A book has:

- a front cover
- a title
- pages

We open a book and get ready to read.

### Page 2: Where Reading Starts

Readers look for where the words begin.

We start in a special place on the page.

We move across the line as we read.

### Talk About It

- Show the front cover.
- Point to the title.
- Show where reading begins.

## Chapter 2: Stories Can Be Retold

### Page 3: First, Next, Last

Stories have events.

We can remember what happened:

- first
- next
- last

### Page 4: Tell the Story Again

You can retell with:

- words
- pictures
- gestures

Retelling helps us remember and understand.

### Talk About It

- What happened first?
- What happened next?
- What happened last?

## Chapter 3: Listening for Rhyme

### Page 5: Some Words Sound Alike

Listen carefully.

Some words sound the same at the end.

Those words rhyme.

### Page 6: Hear the Ending Sound

Cat, hat.

Hop, mop.

Sun, ball.

Which words rhyme?

### Talk About It

- Say two rhyming words.
- Clap when you hear a rhyme.

## Chapter 4: Letters Have Names and Shapes

### Page 7: Letters Look Different

Letters have names.

Letters have shapes.

Some letters are uppercase.

Some letters are lowercase.

### Page 8: Match the Letter Pair

These two letters can match:

- uppercase letter
- lowercase letter

We look closely to notice what is the same and what is different.

### Talk About It

- Name a letter you know.
- Match an uppercase and lowercase pair.

## Chapter 5: Counting Tells How Many

### Page 9: Count One by One

Touch each object once.

Say one number for each object.

### Page 10: The Last Number Tells How Many

When you finish counting, say the last number again.

That number tells how many are in the set.

### Page 11: Compare Two Small Sets

Sometimes we look at two groups.

We can ask:

- Which group has more?
- Which group has fewer?
- Are they the same?

We can use our eyes, our fingers, and careful counting.

### Talk About It

- Count a small group.
- Tell how many.
- Which group has more?

## Chapter 6: We Can Observe Weather

### Page 11: Look at the Sky

Scientists look closely.

We can notice:

- sun
- clouds
- rain
- wind

### Page 12: Record What You Notice

We can use:

- words
- pictures
- symbols

to show today's weather.

### Page 13: Weather Can Change

Some days are sunny.

Some days are cloudy or rainy.

We can compare today to yesterday and notice what changed.

### Talk About It

- What is the weather like today?
- What helped you know?

## Chapter 7: People Help in Communities

### Page 13: Helpers Are Everywhere

People help at:

- home
- school
- in the community

Helpers do important jobs.

### Page 14: Roles and Responsibilities

A teacher helps students learn.

A caregiver helps children grow.

A firefighter helps keep people safe.

### Page 15: Places in Our Community

People help in many places:

- homes
- schools
- libraries
- parks

Different places have different jobs and rules.

### Talk About It

- Name one helper.
- Tell how that person helps.

## Chapter 8: We Can Create and Share

### Page 15: Art Can Show an Idea

We can create with:

- drawing
- color
- music
- movement

### Page 16: Share Your Work

When we share, we can say:

- what we made
- what we noticed
- what it means to us

### Page 17: Art Can Use Many Materials

Artists can use:

- crayons
- paper
- paint
- music
- movement

Each material helps us create in a different way.

### Talk About It

- Make something simple.
- Share one part of your work.

## Chapter 9: Feelings and Healthy Choices

### Page 17: We Can Name Feelings

People can feel:

- happy
- sad
- mad
- scared

### Page 18: We Can Make Safe Choices

When we have a big feeling, we can:

- breathe slowly
- ask for help
- take a calm break

### Page 19: Healthy Habits Help Our Bodies

Healthy habits include:

- washing hands
- resting
- eating healthy foods
- moving our bodies

Healthy habits help us feel ready to learn and play.

### Talk About It

- Name a feeling.
- Name one safe choice.

## Chapter 10: Move with Control

### Page 19: Our Bodies Can Move

We can:

- walk
- hop
- stretch
- balance

### Page 20: Stop and Freeze

Safe movers listen for a cue.

We can stop.

We can freeze.

We can balance with control.

### Page 21: Share Space Safely

When we move with others, we:

- keep our bodies in our own space
- watch where we are going
- slow down when we need to

Safe movement helps everyone participate.

### Talk About It

- Show one safe movement.
- Freeze on cue.
- Hold a balance pose.

## Chapter 11: Follow the Steps in Order

### Page 21: First, Next, Last

Some tasks have steps.

We can put steps in order.

### Page 22: Follow a Sequence

When we know the order, we can finish the task.

We can say:

- first
- next
- last

### Page 23: Tools Need Safe Steps

Some tools are digital tools.

We use them by:

- listening to directions
- taking turns
- using careful hands

Safe steps help tools work well.

### Talk About It

- Put three steps in order.
- Follow the steps together.
