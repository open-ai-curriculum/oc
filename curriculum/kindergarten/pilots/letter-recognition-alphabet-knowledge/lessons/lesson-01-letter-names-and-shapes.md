## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.D
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will recognize and name a small taught set of uppercase and lowercase letters and match corresponding letter pairs with support.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - large uppercase and lowercase letter cards for the focus letters
  - magnetic or paper letters for matching
  - simple alphabet chart
  - optional tracing cards or tactile letters
- Teacher preparation:
  - preselect two or three focus letters with distinct forms
  - choose a clear, readable print style and avoid visually confusing decorative fonts

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Sing or recite a short alphabet routine and pause at the focus letters.
  - Ask students what they notice about the shape of a focus letter.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students view large uppercase and lowercase versions of each focus letter, hear the teacher say the name, and repeat it.
  - Students compare the paired letters and notice what is the same and different.
  - Students match letter pairs using cards, magnets, or a floor sort.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central idea is that letters have recognizable names and shapes, and uppercase and lowercase forms can belong to the same alphabet letter.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students search for the focus letters on an alphabet chart.
  - Students use gestures or tracing motions to describe a focus letter’s shape.
  - The teacher prompts students to name a letter before matching it.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student identifies at least one taught letter by name and matches an uppercase/lowercase pair through pointing, holding, or speaking.
  - The teacher notes whether students rely on visual matching only or can also say the letter name.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - The class reviews the focus letters in a quick call-and-response routine.
  - Students hold up or point to a named letter one last time before transition.
