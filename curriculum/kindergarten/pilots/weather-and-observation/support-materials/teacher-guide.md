## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around observing weather and noticing simple patterns over time.
- It keeps the science grounded in real observation rather than vocabulary-only work.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare a simple weather chart with repeatable symbols.
- Choose a consistent observation routine and time.
- Plan one or two concrete prompts tied to what students can actually see.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may name favorite weather instead of observed weather.
- Students may overgeneralize from one day to all days.
- Some students may need help distinguishing sun, clouds, wind, or rain.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Ask, “What do we notice today?”
- Return students to visible evidence.
- Revisit the chart across several days to discuss patterns.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students can describe one observed condition.
- Track whether students use the class chart accurately.
- Note who needs visual or oral language support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use picture symbols, shared charts, and repeated oral frames.
- Allow pointing, drawing, and speaking.
- Keep weather claims concrete and observable.
