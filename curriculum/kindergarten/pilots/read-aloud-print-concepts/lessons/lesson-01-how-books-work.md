## Standards and Objective

  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.1

- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify basic book parts and show where reading starts on a page during a shared read-aloud.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - one high-quality picture book with clear front cover and large print
  - chart paper or projected book image
  - picture cards for front cover, title, author, and illustrator
  - optional individual mini-book or book photo cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - preselect pages where print is easy to track visibly
  - prepare simple prompts and gestures for front, back, title, and reading direction

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Show the closed book and ask students what they notice before reading begins.
  - Invite predictions about where the story starts and how to hold the book.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students observe the teacher model how to hold and open the book, then identify the front cover, title, author, and illustrator.
  - During the read-aloud, students follow the teacher’s pointer as the class tracks where reading begins and where the eye moves across a line of print.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central content is that books have recognizable parts and that print follows consistent conventions that help readers make meaning.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students turn and talk about what they noticed on the cover.
  - Students use gestures or picture cards to show front cover, back cover, and where reading starts on a displayed page.
  - The teacher asks simple key-detail questions about the read-aloud and prompts students to use pictures and words from the story.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student points to or names one book part and shows where reading starts on a sample page.
  - Students answer one key-detail question orally, with gesture, or with picture support.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - The class reviews a short chant: “Front cover, title, open wide, start right here, our eyes will glide.”
  - Students show with their hands where the book opens and where reading begins.
