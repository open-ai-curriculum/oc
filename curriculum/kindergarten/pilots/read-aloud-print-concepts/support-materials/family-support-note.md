## Family Message

You can help your child grow as a reader by talking about books together. You do not need a long lesson. A few minutes of noticing and talking is enough.

Try these simple prompts:

- “Can you show me the front cover?”
- “Where do we start reading?”
- “Who made this book?”
- “What do you notice in the picture?”
- “What happened in the story?”

## Easy At-Home Ideas

- Let your child hold the book and show how it opens.
- Point to the title and say the name of the book together.
- Track one line of print with your finger while reading.
- Pause and let your child answer or ask a question about the story or picture.
- Use oral language, pointing, or gestures. Full sentences are not required every time.
