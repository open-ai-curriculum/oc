## How To Use

- Focus on a small subset of students each day if whole-class note taking is unrealistic.
- Use the checklist during shared reading, partner talk, or a short follow-up conference.
- Treat “needs support” as planning information, not a label.
- Note whether a student needed picture support, gesture support, repeated prompting, or language scaffolds.

## Accessibility Notes

- The checklist is printable, editable, and usable in low-bandwidth settings.
- It supports quick notation during live instruction.
- It should be paired with multimodal student response options rather than replacing them.
