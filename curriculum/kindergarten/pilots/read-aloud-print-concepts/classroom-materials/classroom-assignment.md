## Purpose

Define the concrete student task used during or immediately after the lesson.

## Materials

- displayed book or projected page
- picture cards for front cover, title, author, and illustrator
- optional mini-book card for each student

## Student Task

### Part 1: Show The Book Parts

Students:

- point to the front cover
- point to the title
- point to where the book opens

### Part 2: Show Where Reading Starts

Students:

- look at one displayed page
- point to where reading begins
- move a finger in the direction reading goes

### Part 3: Answer One Read-Aloud Question

Students respond to one key-detail prompt using:

- speech
- gesture
- picture support
- pointing to the page

## Expected Evidence Of Learning

- student identifies at least one correct book part
- student shows where reading begins
- student responds to one key-detail question about the read-aloud

## Teacher Notes

- This is a live classroom task, not a paper worksheet.
- A teacher may use the observation checklist while students complete the task.

## Accessibility Notes

- Allow oral, gestural, and picture-supported response.
- Keep the task brief and highly visible.
- Use partner support if individual response is intimidating or unclear.
