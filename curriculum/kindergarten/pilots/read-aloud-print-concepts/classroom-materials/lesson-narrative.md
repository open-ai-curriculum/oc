## Purpose

Provide a teacher-ready instructional narrative for Lesson 1 so a teacher can deliver the lesson without reconstructing the flow from the unit and lesson outline alone.

## Teacher Narrative

### Opening Script

Hold up the closed book and say:

"Before we read, we are going to look carefully at how books work. Good readers know how to hold a book, open it, and find where reading begins."

Ask:

- "What do you notice first?"
- "Where do you think the story starts?"

Accept pointing, gestures, one-word responses, and full sentences.

### Modeling Sequence

1. Hold the book correctly and say:
   "This is the front cover."
2. Point to the title and say:
   "This is the title. The title gives the name of the book."
3. Point to the author and illustrator and say:
   "The author writes the words. The illustrator makes the pictures."
4. Open the book slowly and say:
   "When I start reading, I begin here."
5. Track one line of print and say:
   "My eyes move this way across the words."

### Guided Participation

Invite students to:

- point to the front cover
- show where the book opens
- point to where reading begins on a displayed page

Use short prompts:

- "Show me the front cover."
- "Show me where I start reading."
- "Point to the title."

### Read-Aloud Moves

Read part of the text aloud and pause to ask:

- "What happened on this page?"
- "What do you notice in the picture?"

If a student answers generally, prompt:

- "What in the book helped you know that?"

### Likely Student Responses

- accurate pointing to the front cover
- confusion between title and cover illustration
- pointing randomly on the page when asked where reading begins
- picture-based answers to key-detail questions

### Corrective Moves

- If students point to the picture instead of the title, say:
  "The picture is on the cover, but the title is the name of the book."
- If students point mid-page, model again:
  "Watch where I start."
- If students answer off-text, redirect:
  "Let’s look at the page again."

### Closing Script

Say:

"Today we learned that books have parts and that reading starts in a special place. Tomorrow we will keep learning how print works."

Lead the chant:

"Front cover, title, open wide, start right here, our eyes will glide."

## Assessment Notes

- Observe whether students can distinguish the front cover from the picture and title.
- Note whether students locate where reading starts independently, with prompting, or only after modeling.
- Treat oral, gestural, and pointing responses as valid evidence during the lesson.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use gestures, visuals, repeated phrasing, and oral rehearsal throughout.
- Keep teacher language short and predictable.
- Accept multimodal participation without requiring full oral sentences from every learner.
