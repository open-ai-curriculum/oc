## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around counting accurately, naming how many, and comparing groups.
- It keeps the instructional focus on concrete quantity rather than memorized number words alone.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare small sets of countable objects with clear spacing.
- Model touch-and-count routines before independent practice.
- Plan prompts that ask students to explain how they know how many.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may double-count or skip objects.
- Students may say the count sequence without connecting it to quantity.
- Students may compare groups by appearance rather than count.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Prompt students to touch or move each object once.
- Ask, “How many are there?” after the count is complete.
- Compare groups with objects visible rather than abstract symbols alone.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice one-to-one correspondence.
- Track whether students state the total accurately.
- Note whether students can compare two groups using more, less, or equal language.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use real objects, visuals, and oral rehearsal.
- Allow pointing, moving objects, and verbal response.
- Keep language concrete and concise.
