## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will retell key events from a familiar story in order using oral language, picture cards, and sequencing words with support.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - familiar narrative picture book
  - three to five sequencing picture cards
  - first / next / last visual supports
  - optional puppet or retell props
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a story with clear events and strong picture support
  - preselect key events that are central to the story rather than minor details

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Ask students what they remember from the familiar story.
  - Show three event cards and ask which one happened first.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students listen to a reread or short recap of the familiar story.
  - The teacher models a simple retell using first, next, and last.
  - Students help place picture cards in order and echo parts of the retell.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central idea is that stories have meaningful events that can be remembered and retold in sequence.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students work with a partner or small group to place picture cards in order.
  - Students use sentence stems such as “First…” and “Next…” to retell the story.
  - The teacher prompts students to focus on important events rather than unrelated details.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student retells at least part of the story in sequence using oral language, picture placement, or gesture.
  - Students answer one key-detail question to show the retell stays connected to the text.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - The class completes a shared retell with gestures and sequencing words.
  - Students say which card shows the end of the story and why.
