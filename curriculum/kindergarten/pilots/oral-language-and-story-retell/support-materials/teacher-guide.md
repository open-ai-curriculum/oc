## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around listening comprehension, sequencing, and supported oral retell.
- It keeps the work grounded in an actual story rather than generic discussion prompts.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without lowering the language or comprehension target.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose a familiar story with clear events and strong picture support.
- Preselect three to five central events and avoid overloading the retell with too many details.
- Prepare first / next / last visuals and any props before the read-aloud.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may name favorite details rather than the central events.
- Students may sequence events by preference instead of actual order.
- Some students may understand the story but need language support to express the retell.
- Students may over-rely on one picture without connecting it back to the text.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Model a short retell before expecting independent student production.
- Use concrete prompts such as “What happened first?” rather than broad retell requests at the start.
- Revoice and extend student language to strengthen sequencing vocabulary.
- Keep students anchored to the text by returning to the pictures or rereading key pages.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students can identify beginning, middle, and end.
- Track whether students use sequencing language or need sentence stems.
- Note whether students can answer a key-detail question connected to the same story.
- Watch whether students rely on gesture, picture support, oral language, or a combination.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use repeated modeling, clear visuals, and predictable oral routines.
- Allow pointing, acting out, picture placement, and short oral phrases as meaningful participation.
- Keep oral directions short and repeated.
- Choose inclusive stories and avoid text choices that assume narrow family or cultural experiences.
