## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will listen to spoken words in songs, chants, and read-aloud language and identify words that rhyme.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - short rhyme-rich chant, poem, or picture book excerpt
  - picture cards representing simple rhyming and non-rhyming words
  - pocket chart or floor sorting space
- Teacher preparation:
  - preselect clear rhyming examples that are easy to hear orally
  - avoid examples that are visually similar but not phonetically transparent for oral work

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- How will the lesson activate prior knowledge or frame the task?
  - Say two pairs of words, one rhyming and one not, and ask students which pair “sounds the same at the end.”
  - Introduce the idea that today students will listen like word detectives.

### Core Learning Experience

- What will students do?
  - Students join in a short chant or poem with repeated rhyme.
  - The teacher emphasizes the ending sounds of selected words and invites students to echo them.
  - Students identify a rhyming pair with gesture, oral response, or picture selection.
- What is the key content, text, problem, source, or phenomenon?
  - The central idea is that some spoken words share ending sounds and can be noticed through listening and repetition.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- How will students process, practice, or analyze the learning?
  - Students sort a small set of picture cards into rhyme and not-rhyme categories with teacher support.
  - Partners repeat a rhyming pair and tell how the words sound alike.
  - The teacher models one near-miss pair so students can hear the difference.

### Check for Understanding

- What evidence will show whether students are meeting the lesson objective?
  - Each student identifies at least one rhyming pair through pointing, speaking, or selecting a card.
  - The teacher notes whether the student responds accurately with clear oral models and support.

### Closing

- How will the lesson synthesize or reflect on the learning?
  - The class repeats the key chant and pauses to fill in a rhyming word together.
  - Students give a thumbs up when two words rhyme and a hands-flat signal when they do not.
