## Family Message

You can help your child notice sounds in words by singing, chanting, and playing with language together. A few playful minutes is enough.

Try these simple ideas:

- Say two words and ask, “Do these sound the same at the end?”
- Make up silly rhyming words in a song or chant.
- Clap the beats in names or favorite foods.
- Ask your child to listen for the first sound in a familiar word.

## Easy At-Home Ideas

- Use names in the family for syllable clapping.
- Repeat favorite nursery rhymes, songs, or short poems.
- Let your child answer with speaking, clapping, or gesture.
- Use your home language too. Listening to sound patterns in any language is valuable.
