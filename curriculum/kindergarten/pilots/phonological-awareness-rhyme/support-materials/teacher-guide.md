## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around hearing rhyme, clapping syllables, and noticing beginning sounds.
- It keeps phonological-awareness work oral and interactive rather than shifting prematurely into print-heavy tasks.
- The guide is intended to improve teacher usability without reducing the listening demands of the unit.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Choose rhyme examples that are easy to hear in the classroom and appropriate for the local speech community.
- Rehearse the chant or rhyme so the repeated pattern is clear and engaging.
- Prepare picture cards that represent familiar, concrete vocabulary.

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may match words by meaning or picture rather than sound.
- Students may repeat a rhyme pattern without actually hearing the sound similarity.
- Some students may need repeated oral modeling before they can produce an example.
- Dialect and pronunciation differences may affect whether some examples are heard as rhyming.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Say the two target words slowly and emphasize the ending sound pattern.
- Ask students to listen first, then respond with gesture, picture choice, or oral language.
- Avoid overcorrecting dialect features while still choosing instructionally clear examples.
- Keep the pace brisk and playful so repetition supports learning rather than fatigue.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice who can identify a rhyming pair with gesture or oral response.
- Track who needs repeated models or picture support.
- Note whether students are hearing ending sounds rather than sorting by topic or picture category.
- Watch whether students remain engaged when the routine repeats.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use picture supports, movement, and call-and-response participation.
- Keep oral directions brief and model each response type.
- Allow gesture, clapping, pointing, and speaking as meaningful forms of participation.
- Avoid requiring handwriting or fine motor output during phonological-awareness routines.
