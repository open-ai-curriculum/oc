## Alignment Notes

- This guide supports the unit goals around roles, routines, and belonging in family, school, and community settings.
- It helps teachers use inclusive, concrete examples.

## Teacher Preparation Notes

- Prepare varied images of helpers, families, and community spaces.
- Select examples that avoid narrow assumptions about household structure.
- Plan prompts that ask what people do rather than who “counts.”

## Likely Misconceptions Or Developmental Challenges

- Students may assume all families or communities look the same.
- Students may focus on uniforms or appearance rather than roles.
- Some students may need help connecting school and community responsibilities.

## Recommended Teacher Moves

- Use multiple examples for the same type of role.
- Ask, “How does this person help?”
- Revoice student ideas with inclusive language.

## Observation Guidance

- Notice whether students can identify one role.
- Track whether students can describe how a person helps.
- Note which students need vocabulary or representation support.

## Accessibility Notes

- Use diverse visuals, oral language, and pointing options.
- Keep prompts concrete and non-judgmental.
- Avoid requiring personal disclosure about home life.
