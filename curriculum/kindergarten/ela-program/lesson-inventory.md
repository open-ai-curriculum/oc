# Kindergarten ELA Lesson Inventory

## Purpose

Provide the first full-year lesson inventory for the kindergarten ELA commercialization build.

## Inventory Model

The kindergarten ELA program uses a five-lesson weekly pattern:

- `A`: interactive read-aloud and oral language
- `B`: phonological awareness or foundational-skills focus
- `C`: shared reading, print, or decoding application
- `D`: writing/language/application lesson
- `E`: review, assessment, discussion, or family-connection lesson

Across 36 weeks, this creates a 180-lesson annual inventory.

## Weekly Lesson-Slot Pattern

### Lesson A

Interactive read-aloud and oral language:

- vocabulary
- comprehension
- discussion
- topic or story meaning

### Lesson B

Phonological awareness or foundational skills:

- rhyme
- syllables
- sound comparison
- letter-sound work
- blending or segmenting

### Lesson C

Shared reading or controlled-text application:

- print concepts
- concept of word
- choral reading
- decodable or patterned text practice

### Lesson D

Writing and language application:

- draw-dictate-write
- label and sentence routines
- opinion or informative response
- oral rehearsal to written production

### Lesson E

Review, assessment, or family connection:

- formative check
- conference
- cumulative review
- family-linked task

## Existing Pilot Coverage Within The Inventory

The current pilots cover only a small portion of this annual inventory:

- `read-aloud-print-concepts`: mostly Weeks 1-4
- `oral-language-and-story-retell`: mostly Weeks 1-3 and 9-12
- `phonological-awareness-rhyme`: mostly Weeks 5-8 with spirals later
- `letter-recognition-alphabet-knowledge`: mostly Weeks 5-16 with spirals later
