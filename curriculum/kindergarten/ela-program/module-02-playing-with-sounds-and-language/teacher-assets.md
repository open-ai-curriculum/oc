# Module 2 Teacher Assets

## Purpose

This file defines the canonical teacher-use asset layer for Module 2.

## Weekly Teacher Overview

### Week 5

- instructional priority:
  - launch rhyme and chant routines while beginning a small taught letter set
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - keep sound work oral and playful
  - model the difference between listening for sound and naming print
  - revisit the same chants until responses are automatic

### Week 6

- instructional priority:
  - move from hearing rhyme to producing rhyme and counting syllables
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - use body movement for syllable work
  - allow invented oral answers without overcorrecting immediately
  - revisit taught letters briefly every day

### Week 7

- instructional priority:
  - begin comparing beginning sounds with picture support
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - separate sound identity from letter naming when needed
  - choose examples that are clear in the local speech community
  - keep picture sorting highly scaffolded

### Week 8

- instructional priority:
  - review and gather evidence on rhyme, syllables, beginning sounds, and letter matching
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - use conference tasks that mirror practiced routines
  - avoid adding new examples during assessment
  - record whether support changed the outcome

## Lesson Preparation Notes

- prepare chant cards, picture cards, and taught letter cards one week ahead
- pretest rhyme and beginning-sound examples for phonological clarity
- use one consistent alphabet card format throughout the module
- prepare movement cues for clap, tap, step, and hold-up-card routines

## Observation And Conference Tools

### Daily Observation Focus

- joins rhyme routine
- can hear same-ending sounds
- claps syllables with group support
- identifies taught letters consistently

### End-Of-Module Conference Form

- identifies a rhyming pair:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- claps syllables in familiar words:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- notices or sorts a beginning sound:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- names and matches taught uppercase/lowercase letters:
  - secure / developing / not yet

## Family Communication Asset

### Family Message

This month students are playing with rhyme, syllables, beginning sounds, and early alphabet routines. Families can help by singing short rhyming songs, clapping parts in words, and naming a few taught letters in playful ways.

## Intervention And Extension Guidance

### Intervention

- use fewer choices in rhyme tasks
- return to oral-only tasks before adding print cards
- choose highly familiar picture vocabulary
- keep alphabet sets very small and cumulative

### Extension

- invite students to generate an additional rhyme or non-rhyme example
- compare two pictures with the same beginning sound and explain the connection
- add lowercase-to-uppercase speed review for secure learners
