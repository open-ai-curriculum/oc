## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.6
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will rehearse one or two sound-play and alphabet routines they can share with a family member or caregiver.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - take-home routine card
  - rhyme picture pair
  - focus-letter mini card
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare a short family-facing explanation of the routine

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Tell students they will practice showing someone at home what they learned about sounds and letters.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model a simple home routine:
  - say a rhyme pair
  - clap a word
  - name a taught letter

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students practice with partners as if the partner were a family member.
- Review the take-home card and what to say.

### Check for Understanding

- Students demonstrate one take-home routine successfully with support.

### Closing

- Celebrate the end of Module 2 and preview that more reading work is coming.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the take-home routine oral and visual.
- Use family-inclusive language.
- Support students who may not have print materials at home with picture and oral options.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson checks transfer of simple sound-play and alphabet routines beyond the classroom.
- Evidence matches the objective because students rehearse the same routine they will take home.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Family routines should be brief and realistic.
- Keep the home connection joyful rather than burdensome.
