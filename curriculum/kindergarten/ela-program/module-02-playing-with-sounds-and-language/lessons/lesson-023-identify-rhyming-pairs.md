- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify which two spoken words rhyme from supported oral choices.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - rhyme picture cards
  - oral word list
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare three sets of simple choices

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review that rhyming words sound the same at the end.

### Core Learning Experience

- Say three words and model identifying the two that rhyme.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students listen to several word sets and select the rhyming pair with a partner.
- Use pictures when helpful.

### Check for Understanding

- Students identify at least one rhyming pair correctly.

### Closing

- Repeat the strongest rhyming pair with rhythm.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use high-imageability vocabulary.
- Repeat the word set more than once.
- Accept gesture, picture choice, or oral response.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses supported rhyme discrimination.
- Evidence matches the objective because students must compare actual spoken words.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid unclear near-rhymes.
- Keep the comparison load low.
