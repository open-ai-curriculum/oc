## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.D
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will recognize and name the first small taught set of uppercase and lowercase letters.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - large uppercase and lowercase letter cards
  - alphabet chart
- Teacher preparation:
  - select two or three initial focus letters

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Sing or recite a short alphabet opening routine.

### Core Learning Experience

- Introduce the focus letters one at a time.
- Name each uppercase and lowercase pair and compare their shapes.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students repeat the letter names and find the matching pair.
- Use a movement cue for each focus letter.

### Check for Understanding

- Students point to, hold up, or name a focus letter when prompted.

### Closing

- Review the names of the focus letters together.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use large, visually clear fonts.
- Limit the taught set.
- Allow responses through pointing and holding cards.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson formatively assesses initial letter-name recognition.
- Evidence matches the objective because students identify the taught letters directly.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Introduce only a few letters for mastery.
- Ensure the letter forms are visually unambiguous.
