## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will listen to a short chant and identify words that rhyme.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - rhyme-rich chant or poem
  - picture cards for rhyming words
- Teacher preparation:
  - select two obvious rhyming pairs

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Teach the class to echo the short chant with rhythm and movement.

### Core Learning Experience

- Read or chant the text again and emphasize the rhyming words with voice and gesture.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students clap when they hear two words that sound the same at the end.
- Show picture cards and ask which two rhyme.

### Check for Understanding

- Students identify a rhyming pair orally, by gesture, or by picture choice.

### Closing

- Repeat one rhyming pair together and celebrate hearing the pattern.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use repetition, rhythm, and movement.
- Accept gestures and picture choices.
- Keep the oral text short and memorable.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson formatively assesses rhyme recognition.
- Evidence matches the objective because students respond to actual spoken word pairs.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Select rhyme pairs that are clear in the local speech context.
- Keep the work playful rather than quiz-like.
