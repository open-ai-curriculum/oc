## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.D
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.A
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will sort familiar picture cards by beginning sound using taught letters as anchors.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture cards
  - two focus-letter headers
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose picture examples aligned to taught sounds

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review the two focus letters and their common sounds.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model placing one picture under the correct sound-letter header.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students sort additional pictures with partners.
- Discuss why each picture belongs where it does.

### Check for Understanding

- Students correctly place or explain one picture sort decision.

### Closing

- Review that letters can help us organize the sounds we hear.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use high-contrast headers and simple pictures.
- Limit to two categories.
- Allow oral and gesture explanations.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses the bridge between oral sound awareness and taught letters.
- Evidence matches the objective because students must sort based on sound.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Do not overstate this as formal reading yet.
- Keep the sound-letter connection concrete and taught.
