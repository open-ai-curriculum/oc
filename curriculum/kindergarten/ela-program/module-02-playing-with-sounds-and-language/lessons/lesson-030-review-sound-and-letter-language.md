- Lesson objective:
  - Students will review module language by talking about rhyme, syllables, letters, and sounds in supported oral routines.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture cards
  - letter cards
  - oral sentence frames
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a small set of review prompts

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Remind students of the words rhyme, syllable, letter, and sound.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model using the vocabulary in simple oral statements.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students complete oral frames with partners and as a group.
- Mix in quick letter and sound responses.

### Check for Understanding

- Students use at least one module word appropriately during a supported exchange.

### Closing

- Review that the class is learning new ways to talk about language.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Pair each vocabulary word with a gesture or picture.
- Keep oral frames short.
- Accept approximated language when meaning is clear.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses oral language use tied to the module content.
- Evidence matches the objective because students must use the vocabulary in context.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Vocabulary should serve understanding, not replace practice.
- Keep the oral routines concrete and anchored in recent activities.
