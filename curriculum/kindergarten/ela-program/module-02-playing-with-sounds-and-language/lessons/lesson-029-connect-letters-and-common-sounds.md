## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.L.K.1.A
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will connect taught letters to a common sound using pictures and oral examples.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - focus-letter cards
  - picture cards for initial sounds
- Teacher preparation:
  - select one reliable picture example per taught sound

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review letter names first, then introduce the idea that letters can help us talk about sounds.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model a focus letter and say its common sound with a picture example.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students sort or match pictures to the taught letters.
- Use oral repetition of the letter and sound.

### Check for Understanding

- Students connect at least one picture to the correct taught letter/sound.

### Closing

- Review the focus letters and their common sounds.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use simple picture vocabulary.
- Keep the taught sound set small.
- Accept pointing and repeating as evidence.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses early letter-sound connection with support.
- Evidence matches the objective through direct picture/letter matching and oral response.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid multiple competing sound values in the launch.
- Use pictures that are unambiguous in local language use.
