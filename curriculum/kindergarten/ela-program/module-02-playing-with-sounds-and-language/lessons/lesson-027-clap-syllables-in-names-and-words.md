## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.B
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will clap and count syllables in familiar names and classroom words.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - name cards or picture cards
  - beat markers
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose one- to three-syllable familiar words

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Explain that words can have one beat or more than one beat.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model clapping syllables in student names and classroom objects.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students clap and say the beats together.
- Compare short and longer words.

### Check for Understanding

- Students clap the syllables in at least one familiar word accurately with support.

### Closing

- Review that some words have one beat and some have more.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use movement and voice together.
- Choose familiar words from the classroom environment.
- Accept stepping or tapping instead of clapping.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses syllable awareness in spoken language.
- Evidence matches the objective through oral and physical demonstration.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the focus oral; do not add print demands.
- Select words whose syllable patterns are clear in local speech.
