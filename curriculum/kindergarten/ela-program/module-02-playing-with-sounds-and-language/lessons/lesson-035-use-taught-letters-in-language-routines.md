- Lesson objective:
  - Students will use taught letters and sounds during simple oral naming and sorting routines.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - letter cards
  - picture cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare a few oral prompts linking letters, names, and sounds

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review the focus letters and their names.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model saying a letter, its common sound, and one picture example.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students sort pictures or objects under the taught letter cards.
- Use oral repetition and partner practice.

### Check for Understanding

- Students connect one picture to a taught letter/sound during the routine.

### Closing

- Review how letters and sounds help us notice words.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use familiar vocabulary and concrete visuals.
- Accept pointing and one-word responses.
- Limit to a few taught letters.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses early integration of letter and sound knowledge.
- Evidence matches the objective because students use taught letters during actual routines.
