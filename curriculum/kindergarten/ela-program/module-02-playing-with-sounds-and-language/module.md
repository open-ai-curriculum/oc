## Module Purpose

- What is the module about?
  - Students listen closely to spoken language and begin alphabet routines through rhyme, songs, syllable play, beginning-sound noticing, and the launch of taught uppercase and lowercase letter pairs.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Kindergarten readers need strong oral sound awareness and repeated encounters with letters, but the work must stay playful, oral, and developmentally appropriate instead of becoming abstract, print-heavy drill.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- some words sound alike in meaningful ways
- spoken words can be broken into parts such as syllables
- beginning sounds can be heard and compared
- letters have names and visual forms that connect to sounds

### Students should know:

- what rhyme means in familiar oral routines
- that words can have one or more syllables
- that uppercase and lowercase letters can match
- names and common sounds for a small taught set of letters

### Students should be able to do:

- identify and produce simple rhyming words in supported routines
- clap and count syllables in familiar words
- compare beginning sounds in spoken words with picture support
- identify, match, and talk about taught letters and common sounds

## Essential Questions

- How do words sound the same?
- How can we listen for parts in a word?
- How do letters help us notice and talk about sounds?

## Assessment Evidence

### Summative evidence

- Students complete a short end-of-module sound-and-letter conference in which they:
  - identify one rhyming pair from oral choices
  - clap syllables in familiar words
  - identify or sort a beginning sound with support
  - name and match a small taught set of uppercase/lowercase letters

### Formative evidence

- oral participation in songs, chants, and rhyme routines
- teacher notes during syllable clapping and picture sorts
- letter-name and letter-match quick checks
- movement-based sound-play responses

## Lesson Sequence

### Week 5

- KELA-021 through KELA-025
- focus: hear rhyme, join chants and songs, introduce alphabet routine, notice first taught letters

### Week 6

- KELA-026 through KELA-030
- focus: produce rhyme, clap syllables, identify taught letters, connect letters to common sounds

### Week 7

- KELA-031 through KELA-035
- focus: beginning sounds, alphabet review, picture sorts, repeated shared text with taught letters

### Week 8

- KELA-036 through KELA-040
- focus: cumulative review, sound-play checks, alphabet-family practice, end-of-module conference

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Representation supports:
  - picture cards, gestures, movement, visual letter cards, and repeated oral models
- Engagement supports:
  - songs, chants, call-and-response, partner play, and short repeated routines
- Action/expression supports:
  - speaking, pointing, clapping, stepping, matching, holding up cards, and teacher-recorded responses are valid
- Language/accessibility considerations:
  - keep directions brief, support multilingual learners with repetition and visual cues, and avoid fine-motor writing as the primary evidence path
- Inclusive examples or framing notes:
  - use names, objects, and examples that reflect varied identities and classroom experiences respectfully

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - moving too quickly from oral sound awareness into abstract print drill
  - using phonetically awkward or dialect-dependent examples
  - making alphabet work rote and disconnected from language
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - rhyme sets
  - picture-sort items
  - chosen focus-letter examples and fonts
