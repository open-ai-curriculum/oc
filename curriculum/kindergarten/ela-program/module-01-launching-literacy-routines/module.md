## Module Purpose

- What is the module about?
  - Students learn how literacy time works in kindergarten by building routines for book handling, listening, discussion, retelling, print tracking, and early shared response.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Kindergarten literacy requires strong routines before later comprehension, writing, and foundational-skills work can succeed. This module establishes those routines in developmentally appropriate, teacher-mediated ways.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- books and print are organized in predictable ways
- read-alouds help listeners understand stories and ideas
- good listeners talk, point, act, and ask questions to make meaning
- stories can be retold in sequence with support

### Students should know:

- front cover, back cover, title, author, illustrator, page, and word
- where reading begins and how print moves on a page
- first, next, and last as sequence language
- classroom routines for turn-and-talk, partner listening, and group discussion

### Students should be able to do:

- orient a book correctly and identify basic book parts
- track print in shared reading with teacher support
- answer and ask simple questions about a read-aloud
- retell a familiar story in order using oral language, gestures, and pictures

## Essential Questions

- How do we know how to use a book?
- What do listeners and readers do to understand a story?
- How can we tell a story again so someone else understands it?

## Assessment Evidence

### Summative evidence

- Students complete a short end-of-module literacy conference in which they:
  - show how to hold and open a familiar book
  - identify front cover, title, and where reading begins
  - sequence three story events from a familiar read-aloud
  - answer and ask one simple question about a story

### Formative evidence

- daily oral responses during read-aloud
- partner talk and gesture-based checks
- teacher notes on book handling and print tracking
- quick retell, sequencing, and discussion routines

## Lesson Sequence

### Week 1

- KELA-001 through KELA-005
- focus: launching read-aloud expectations, book parts, cover talk, listening, and first questions

### Week 2

- KELA-006 through KELA-010
- focus: print directionality, beginning of text, sequence language, and shared story talk

### Week 3

- KELA-011 through KELA-015
- focus: key-detail questions, concept of word, retell practice, and shared text routines

### Week 4

- KELA-016 through KELA-020
- focus: cumulative review, conference checks, oral retell, and family reading transfer

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Representation supports:
  - large-format texts, pictures, gestures, sequence cards, and sentence stems
- Engagement supports:
  - repeated routines, movement, chants, partner talk, and predictable lesson structure
- Action/expression supports:
  - oral, gestural, pictorial, acted, pointed, and teacher-recorded responses are valid
- Language/accessibility considerations:
  - keep directions short, repeat key language, and support multilingual learners with visuals and modeled oral language
- Inclusive examples or framing notes:
  - choose stories and examples that reflect varied identities, homes, and classroom experiences respectfully

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - reducing kindergarten literacy to compliance routines rather than meaning-making
  - using weak read-aloud texts
  - expecting independent reading behaviors that exceed beginning-of-year kindergarten capacity
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - anchor-text selection
  - question quality
  - sequence cards and print visuals
