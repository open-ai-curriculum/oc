## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.C
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will track a short line of shared text with a pointer and notice the difference between pictures and words.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - sentence strip or chart text
  - pointer
  - picture/word comparison cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare one familiar line of text with strong spacing

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Display a picture and a line of words. Ask how they are different.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model pointing under each word in the line as it is read.
- Explain that words tell the story in print and pictures help us think.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students track the line in the air or on mini copies.
- Compare pointing to the picture versus pointing to the words.

### Check for Understanding

- Students show where the words are and track the line from beginning to end.

### Closing

- Review: pictures and words both help us, but words are what we read in print.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use oversized print.
- Support with arm movements before fine-motor tracking.
- Allow students to stand and trace the line if needed.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses concept of word at a beginning level.
- Evidence matches the objective because students engage directly with the shared text.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the sentence strip meaningful and familiar.
- Avoid abstract vocabulary like "symbol" or "text feature" here.
