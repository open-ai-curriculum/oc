## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will use the book cover and pictures to notice details and share ideas before and during a read-aloud.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture book with a clear cover image
  - chart labeled "I notice"
  - sentence stem card: "I notice ___"
- Teacher preparation:
  - select two cover details students can easily see
  - plan two think-alouds using the sentence stem

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Display the closed book and ask, "What can we learn before we even start reading?"

### Core Learning Experience

- Model noticing the picture, title area, and one obvious detail on the cover.
- Read the first pages and pause to compare what students noticed with what the text shows.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students turn and talk using "I notice ___."
- Record several student observations on the chart.
- Prompt students to point to the part of the cover that matches their idea.

### Check for Understanding

- Each student shares or points to one cover detail and connects it to the story or picture.

### Closing

- Review: good readers and listeners notice details before and during reading.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use enlarged cover projection or chart copy if needed.
- Offer pointing or picture-selection as valid evidence.
- Repeat the sentence stem orally several times.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson formatively assesses observation and oral language tied to a real text.
- The evidence matches the objective because students must use the actual cover and pictures.
- The task is text-grounded and not generic.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid prediction-only talk; keep students anchored to actual visible details.
- Use a cover image rich enough to support discussion without confusion.
