## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will practice turn-and-talk routines by sharing one idea about a book and listening to a partner.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - anchor chart for turn-and-talk
  - familiar book cover or page image
  - partner cue card: look, listen, share
- Teacher preparation:
  - model partner body position and short response turns

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Teach the partner routine: face your partner, listen, take turns, and share one idea.

### Core Learning Experience

- Show a familiar page from the book.
- Model a turn-and-talk with another adult or a student volunteer.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students talk with partners using the frame "I noticed ___" or "My favorite part was ___."
- Teacher circulates and coaches turn-taking and listening.

### Check for Understanding

- Students complete one partner exchange and one student shares the partner's idea back to the group.

### Closing

- Reflect on what good partners do during literacy time.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep response frames short.
- Allow students to point to the page if full speech is hard.
- Pair students strategically for support.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses collaborative speaking and listening routines.
- Evidence matches the objective because students must complete an actual partner exchange.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the partner talk anchored to a shared book or page.
- Explicitly teach listening, not just talking.
