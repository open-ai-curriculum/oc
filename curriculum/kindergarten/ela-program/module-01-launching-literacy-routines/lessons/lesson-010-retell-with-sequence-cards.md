## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will use sequence cards and oral language to retell a familiar story in order.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - familiar story
  - sequence cards
  - first/next/last labels
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare small-group card sets if possible

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review first, next, last from Lesson 7.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model ordering the cards and retelling the story in three parts.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Small groups or partners arrange cards in order.
- Students retell using sequence language, gestures, or repeated teacher prompts.

### Check for Understanding

- Students complete a supported retell with the cards in correct order.

### Closing

- Celebrate that stories can be told again clearly when we use the right order.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the number of cards limited.
- Use large visuals and oral repetition.
- Allow retell through gesture and pointing plus teacher narration.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses sequence-based oral retell.
- Evidence matches the objective because the retell depends on actual story events.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Retell should preserve meaning, not become card memorization only.
- Use familiar stories for success at this stage.
