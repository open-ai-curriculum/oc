## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.B
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify front cover, back cover, title, author, and illustrator on a classroom read-aloud book.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - one picture book
  - book-part cards
  - optional mini-book image cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - place sticky tabs on target parts for modeling

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Revisit yesterday's cover-noticing routine and introduce the idea that books have named parts.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model front cover, back cover, title, author, and illustrator.
- Read the title aloud and explain what the author and illustrator do.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students help match labels to the book parts.
- Partners use gestures or words to show front cover and back cover.
- Revisit the first page of the text to connect title and beginning of reading.

### Check for Understanding

- Students point to or name one or more book parts when prompted.

### Closing

- Review a short chant naming the book parts.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use visual cards and repeated oral labels.
- Allow students to respond by pointing or holding a matching picture card.
- Keep terminology limited to a small set for the lesson.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson formatively assesses recognition of named book parts.
- Evidence matches the objective because students identify the actual parts on a real book.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the terminology connected to book use, not isolated vocabulary drilling.
- Ensure visuals clearly distinguish front and back cover.
