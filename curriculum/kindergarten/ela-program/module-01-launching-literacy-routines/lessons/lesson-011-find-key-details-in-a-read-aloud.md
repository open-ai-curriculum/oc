## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify one or two key details from a read-aloud and connect them to a question.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture book
  - detail cards or picture choices
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a page spread with obvious key details

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Tell students they will listen for important details that help answer a question.

### Core Learning Experience

- Read a short portion of the story.
- Pause and ask one concrete key-detail question.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Show detail cards and discuss which one answers the question.
- Students explain with sentence frames or pointing.

### Check for Understanding

- Students select or say one key detail tied to the question.

### Closing

- Review that key details are the important parts that help us understand.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use picture-supported answer options.
- Limit the number of details under discussion.
- Repeat the question and reread the page if needed.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses simple key-detail comprehension.
- Evidence matches the objective because students connect a detail to a question.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid trivial questions that do not matter to comprehension.
- Keep the questions visibly answerable from the text and image.
