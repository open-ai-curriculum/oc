## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.6
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will rehearse one or two literacy routines they can share with a family member, such as showing the front cover, where reading begins, or retelling first/next/last.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - take-home routine card
  - familiar book or mini-book image
  - sequence strip
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare one simple family-facing explanation of the routine

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Tell students they will practice showing someone at home what they learned about books and stories.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model how to show a family member the front cover, title, and where reading starts.
- Model a short first/next/last retell with a familiar story.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students practice the routine with partners as if the partner were a family member.
- Review the take-home card and what to say.

### Check for Understanding

- Students demonstrate one take-home routine successfully with support.

### Closing

- Celebrate the end of Module 1 and preview that more literacy learning is coming.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the take-home routine oral and visual, not worksheet-heavy.
- Use family-inclusive language such as grown-up, caregiver, or family member.
- Support students who may not have a book at home by providing an image card or oral routine option.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson checks transfer of a simple literacy routine beyond the classroom.
- Evidence matches the objective because students rehearse the exact routine they will share.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Family connection should extend literacy habits, not create homework burden.
- Keep the routine short and realistic for varied home contexts.
