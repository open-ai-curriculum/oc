## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.B
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will identify where reading begins on a page and follow print from left to right with support.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - big-book or projected page with clear print
  - pointer
  - start-here marker
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a page with one or two short lines of print

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Display the page and ask, "Where would a reader start?"

### Core Learning Experience

- Model starting at the left side of the first line and moving across.
- Repeat with a second line if appropriate.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Invite students to air-trace the direction of reading.
- Students use finger or pointer motions from left to right.
- Compare the beginning of the line to the end of the line.

### Check for Understanding

- Students show where reading begins and trace the direction of print.

### Closing

- Review the phrase: "We start here, and our eyes move across."

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use large print and visible spacing.
- Allow full-body arm movements before finger tracking.
- Keep the text amount minimal.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson formatively assesses print directionality.
- Evidence matches the objective because students act on the print.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the task in real connected text, not isolated arrows alone.
- Avoid overloading students with too many print concepts at once.
