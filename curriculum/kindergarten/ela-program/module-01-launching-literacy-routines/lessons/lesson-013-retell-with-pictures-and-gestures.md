## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.2
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will retell key parts of a familiar story using pictures, gestures, and oral language.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - familiar read-aloud
  - retell picture cards
  - gesture prompts
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose three to four moments students can act out simply

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review that retelling can happen with words, pictures, and bodies.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model a short retell while pointing to pictures and acting out one event.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students retell with partners, using picture cards and gestures.
- Teacher prompts: "What happened first?" "Show me."

### Check for Understanding

- Each student contributes one retell part using one or more response modes.

### Closing

- Reflect that retelling helps us remember the story.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Offer multiple response modes.
- Use pictures large enough for shared viewing.
- Keep expectations focused on sequence and meaning, not complete sentences.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses multimodal retell ability.
- Evidence matches the objective because students retell actual story events.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Multimodal retell is especially appropriate at this stage and should not be treated as a lesser response.
- Keep the story familiar to reduce memory overload.
