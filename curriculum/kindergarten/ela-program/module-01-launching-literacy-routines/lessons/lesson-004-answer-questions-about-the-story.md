## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will answer simple who or what questions about key details in a read-aloud.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - familiar picture book from Lessons 1-3
  - key-detail question cards with pictures
- Teacher preparation:
  - select three concrete questions tied to visible story details

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Remind students that listeners can show understanding by answering questions about the story.

### Core Learning Experience

- Reread part of the familiar story.
- Pause and model answering one who or what question using words and pictures from the text.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Ask two additional key-detail questions.
- Let students answer through talk, pointing, or picture choices.
- Encourage sentence frames such as "It was ___" or "The story showed ___."

### Check for Understanding

- Students answer at least one key-detail question using the text and pictures.

### Closing

- Review: strong listeners use the story to answer questions.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use visible picture support for each question.
- Accept one-word, gesture, and pointing responses.
- Keep questions concrete and tied to the page.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses early comprehension in a teacher-mediated format.
- Evidence matches the objective because it depends on actual story details.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid inferential prompts that overreach beginning-of-year kindergarten readiness.
- Keep questions tied to the chosen text, not generic.
