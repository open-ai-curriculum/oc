- Lesson objective:
  - Students will learn and practice the core routines for gathering, listening, looking at a book, and responding during read-aloud.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - one high-quality picture book
  - carpet spot or meeting-area markers
  - visual cue cards for eyes, ears, hands, and turn-and-talk
- Teacher preparation:
  - preteach a short listening chant
  - mark stopping points for two brief questions

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Gather students in the literacy space and model how readers sit, look, and listen.
- Teach the chant: "Eyes are watching, ears are ready, bodies calm, minds steady."

### Core Learning Experience

- Show the book without opening it.
- Model how the class will look carefully, listen to the story, and hold thoughts until discussion time.
- Read a short portion of the book and pause once to model a noticing statement.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students practice the listening routine with a partner using the visual cue cards.
- Pause during the read-aloud and ask, "What did you notice?"
- Invite oral, gestural, and picture-supported responses.

### Check for Understanding

- Students demonstrate the literacy routine by showing listening posture and giving one response after the pause.

### Closing

- Repeat the listening chant together.
- Name two things strong listeners did today.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use picture cues and gestures for every routine step.
- Accept pointing, gestures, and one-word responses.
- Keep the story segment short and success-oriented.
