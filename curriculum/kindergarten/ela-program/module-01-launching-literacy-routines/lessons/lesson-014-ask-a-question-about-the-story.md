## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RL.K.1
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.K.1
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will ask one simple question about a familiar story using teacher-supported stems.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - familiar story
  - question stem cards: "Who is ___?" "What happened?" "Where is ___?"
- Teacher preparation:
  - select one page spread where question asking is natural

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Explain that readers and listeners sometimes ask questions to understand more.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model asking one simple question about the story.
- Read the relevant page and answer the question together.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students choose a question stem and practice asking with a partner or the teacher.
- Use picture support to help students point to what they want to ask about.

### Check for Understanding

- Students ask or complete one supported question tied to the story.

### Closing

- Review that questions help us think more deeply about what we hear.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Provide fixed stems and picture prompts.
- Accept partially verbalized questions and teacher-restated questions.
- Keep the questioning routine low-stakes and supportive.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses beginning question generation tied to a text.
- Evidence matches the objective because the question must be about the actual story.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Kindergarten question work should stay concrete.
- Avoid praising random question generation detached from the book.
