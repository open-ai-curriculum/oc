# Module 1 Multilingual Supports

## Purpose

This file defines module-specific multilingual supports for Module 1.

## Core Design Principles

- preserve meaning-making through oral language even when English output is limited
- accept pointing, gesture, acting, drawing, and teacher-recorded speech as valid evidence
- connect home-language storytelling habits to classroom retell routines

## Lesson-Type Scaffolds

### Read-Aloud Discussion

- pre-teach no more than two target words before the read-aloud
- pair each target word with picture support and a gesture
- provide oral frames:
  - "I notice..."
  - "I think..."
  - "First I see..."

### Book-Part And Print Lessons

- model vocabulary with the physical book before expecting oral repetition
- use consistent gestures for front, back, open, and begin
- allow children to demonstrate book orientation silently before naming parts

### Retell Lessons

- offer sequence cards before requiring full oral retell
- accept retell in home language followed by brief English labeling when possible
- use frames:
  - "First..."
  - "Next..."
  - "Last..."

## Home-Language Bridge Opportunities

- invite families to retell a familiar story in any language at home
- encourage adults to name book parts in home language and English when possible
- allow students to share home-language story words during partner discussion if meaning is clear

## Cross-Linguistic Notes For Teachers

- do not interpret quiet processing as lack of comprehension
- expect some students to understand sequence language before producing it
- separate book-use knowledge from English expressive skill during conferences
