## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.D
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.K.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will help label a familiar picture by using taught letters and oral rehearsal.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture card
  - dry-erase board or chart
  - letter cards
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a simple label tied to taught sounds

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Show the picture and say the word together.

### Core Learning Experience

- Stretch the beginning sound orally and connect it to the taught letter.
- Model placing or writing the first letter in the label.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students help identify the first sound and choose the letter card.
- Teacher records the label with student contribution.

### Check for Understanding

- Students contribute at least one sound or letter to the shared label.

### Closing

- Reread the label and connect it back to the picture.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the label short and concrete.
- Use oral rehearsal before any writing.
- Allow students to point to letters instead of handwriting them.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses early sound-linked labeling in a shared-writing format.
- Evidence matches the objective through direct contribution to the label.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep expectations modest and heavily scaffolded.
- Shared writing is more appropriate here than independent writing.
