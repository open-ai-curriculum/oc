## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.1.D
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.A
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will match uppercase and lowercase forms of the taught letters with increased accuracy and fluency.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - uppercase and lowercase card pairs
  - match mats
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare a small set of taught pairs only

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review that the same letter can look two ways.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model matching one pair quickly and naming both forms.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students practice matching, naming, and checking with partners.

### Check for Understanding

- Students accurately match and name at least one pair.

### Closing

- Review the matched pairs together.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use uncluttered fonts and high-contrast cards.
- Limit the number of pairs in play.
- Allow pointing and card placement as evidence.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses uppercase/lowercase pairing fluency.
- Evidence matches the objective because students directly manipulate the target pairs.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Avoid visually dense layouts.
- Stay within the taught-letter set.
