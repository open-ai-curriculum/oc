## Standards and Objective

- Lesson objective:
  - Students will rehearse one shared-reading or letter-sound routine they can show a family member or caregiver.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - take-home routine card
  - mini shared-text strip
  - focus-letter card
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare one simple family-facing explanation of the routine

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Tell students they will practice showing someone at home what they learned about letters, sounds, and shared reading.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model one home routine:
  - name the letter
  - say the sound
  - point to the word in the shared strip

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students practice the routine with partners.
- Review the take-home card together.

### Check for Understanding

- Students demonstrate one take-home literacy routine successfully with support.

### Closing

- Celebrate the end of Module 4 and preview that these skills will keep helping with reading and writing.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the home routine oral and visual.
- Use family-inclusive language.
- Provide options that do not require special materials at home.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson checks transfer of a simple reading/writing routine beyond the classroom.
- Evidence matches the objective because students rehearse the exact take-home routine.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Family routines should remain short and realistic.
- Do not create homework burden from foundational review.
