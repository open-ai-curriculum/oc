# Kindergarten ELA Year Overview

## Purpose

Define the annual purpose, strand balance, and year-long arc for the kindergarten ELA program.

## Core Position

The kindergarten ELA program should build oral language, listening comprehension, phonological awareness, alphabet knowledge, early decoding, early writing, and text-based discussion through a teacher-mediated, developmentally appropriate literacy model.

It should not collapse into:

- isolated worksheet-heavy foundational-skills drill
- comprehension tasks detached from actual texts
- independent-reading expectations that exceed kindergarten developmental realities

## Annual Strand Balance

The kindergarten ELA program should integrate all of these strands across the year:

1. interactive read-aloud and listening comprehension
2. oral language and discussion
3. print concepts and shared reading
4. phonological awareness
5. alphabet knowledge and letter-sound work
6. early decoding and encoding
7. early writing and drawing-to-write routines
8. language and vocabulary development

## Full-Year Arc

### Beginning of year

Primary focus:

- print awareness
- book handling
- oral language routines
- story listening and retell
- rhyme, syllables, and listening play
- launch of alphabet knowledge

### Middle of year

Primary focus:

- stronger shared reading routines
- initial letter-sound connections
- early blending and segmenting
- richer retell and discussion
- response to informational texts and knowledge-building texts
- drawing, dictating, and writing about texts and topics

### End of year

Primary focus:

- broader application of foundational skills
- text-based speaking, drawing, and early writing
- stronger informational and narrative comprehension
- cumulative transfer into Grade 1 literacy demands

## Annual Design Rules

- reading, listening, speaking, and writing should stay connected
- foundational-skills work should stay developmentally appropriate and mostly brief, repeated, and cumulative
- kindergarten texts should be high quality, concrete, and knowledge-building
- student response should remain multimodal: oral, gestural, pictorial, manipulable, dictated, and early written
- teacher mediation remains central; kindergarten ELA is not an independent textbook-reading model

## Target Annual Scale

The kindergarten ELA program should eventually reach:

- 8 to 10 modules
- 140 to 180 lessons
- 220 to 320 student-facing pages
- 350 to 500 teacher-edition pages
- 25 to 40 formal and informal assessment artifacts
- 150 to 220 reproducibles and copy masters

## Pilot Strand Alignment

The current repository literacy pilots align to the year like this:

- `read-aloud-print-concepts`: launch of print concepts, interactive read-aloud, and classroom literacy routines
- `oral-language-and-story-retell`: early and continuing story comprehension and expressive language work
- `phonological-awareness-rhyme`: beginning-of-year and spiraled oral sound work
- `letter-recognition-alphabet-knowledge`: alphabet and early foundational-skills launch
