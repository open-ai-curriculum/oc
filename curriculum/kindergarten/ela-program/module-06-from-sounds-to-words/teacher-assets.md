# Module 6 Teacher Assets

## Purpose

This file defines the canonical teacher-use asset layer for Module 6.

## Weekly Teacher Overview

### Week 21

- instructional priority:
  - launch oral blending and segmenting with taught sound-symbol supports
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - keep counters, boxes, and oral rehearsal central
  - avoid moving to print before oral success is visible
  - maintain a very small taught word set

### Week 22

- instructional priority:
  - introduce the first controlled texts and connect them to dictated sentence work
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - preview routine words before reading
  - treat controlled text as supported practice, not independent reading proof
  - reread instead of constantly introducing new text

### Week 23

- instructional priority:
  - strengthen blending, routine words, and supported rereading
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - use consistent hand motions for blending and segmenting
  - check whether difficulty comes from memory load or sound-symbol knowledge
  - revisit dictated sentences with oral rehearsal first

### Week 24

- instructional priority:
  - gather end-of-module decoding and writing evidence
- teacher moves to emphasize:
  - use taught patterns only
  - keep assessments short and precise
  - note when support changes the outcome

## Lesson Preparation Notes

- prepare sound boxes, letter cards, and controlled word cards in advance
- keep one documented taught-pattern list posted for teacher use
- preflight every controlled text for pattern accuracy and language quality
- prepare dictated sentence routines with clear teacher scribing norms

## Observation And Conference Tools

### Daily Observation Focus

- orally blends simple words
- segments a familiar word
- reads or identifies controlled words
- contributes to shared sentence work

### End-Of-Module Conference Form

- blends a simple taught word:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- segments a familiar simple word:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- reads or identifies controlled words:
  - secure / developing / not yet
- contributes to a shared sentence:
  - secure / developing / not yet

## Family Communication Asset

### Family Message

This month students are learning how sounds come together to make simple words. Families can help by playing sound games, listening for sounds in familiar words, and rereading a very short teacher-sent text together.

## Intervention And Extension Guidance

### Intervention

- return to oral blending before using printed words
- reduce the number of word choices
- use picture support with controlled-word review
- allow the student to supply a sound rather than a full written word in dictated sentences

### Extension

- add one new taught-pattern word to a familiar routine
- invite students to reread a short controlled text with reduced teacher modeling
- ask students to contribute an additional routine word in shared sentence work
