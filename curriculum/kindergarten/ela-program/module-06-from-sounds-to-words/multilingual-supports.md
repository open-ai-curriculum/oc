# Module 6 Multilingual Supports

## Purpose

This file defines module-specific multilingual supports for Module 6.

## Core Design Principles

- decoding instruction must remain explicit without treating multilingual learners as if they are starting without language knowledge
- oral rehearsal and teacher modeling remain essential even when the target shifts toward word reading
- assessment should separate pattern knowledge from English expressive fluency

## Lesson-Type Scaffolds

### Blending And Segmenting Lessons

- model with counters and gesture before requiring spoken response
- use clear pause language:
  - "Listen."
  - "Say the sounds."
  - "Put them together."
- allow students to point to sound boxes while the teacher voices the sounds

### Controlled Text Lessons

- preview high-frequency routine words orally and visually
- read the text aloud once for meaning before asking students to participate in supported decoding
- use partner echo reading before any independent attempt

### Dictated Sentence Lessons

- orally rehearse the sentence several times
- allow students to contribute one sound, one letter, or one routine word
- accept teacher scribing paired with student pointing or oral contribution

## Home-Language Bridge Opportunities

- invite families to play oral sound games in any language while keeping school decoding examples in English
- send one short explanation that the goal is hearing and using taught English sound-symbol patterns, not fluent independent reading yet

## Cross-Linguistic Notes For Teachers

- some English vowel contrasts or consonants may be unfamiliar and need extra modeling
- multilingual learners may understand the decoding routine before they can explain it verbally in English
- score based on demonstrated pattern knowledge, not on accent or sentence-length output
