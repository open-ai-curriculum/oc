- Lesson objective:
  - Students will segment familiar words and use the parts to help build the words with letters.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - counters
  - letter cards
  - picture prompts
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a small set of known words for segment-and-build practice

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review that segmenting helps writers and readers hear the parts inside words.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model segmenting a word, then matching the sounds to letters.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students segment and help build one or more familiar words.

### Check for Understanding

- Students segment at least one word and contribute to its build.

### Closing

- Review that sounds can help us make the word in print.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use manipulatives and movement.
- Keep the word set familiar and simple.
- Accept approximated oral segmentation with teacher support.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses the link between segmenting and encoding.
- Evidence matches the objective through direct sound-to-letter contribution.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Keep the teacher support high.
- Avoid making this feel like formal spelling assessment.
