## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.A
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.3.B
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.4
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will read a very short controlled text with teacher support using taught patterns and picture cues.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - short controlled text or decodable strip
  - pointer
  - picture cue
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a text aligned tightly to taught patterns

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Preview the text picture and remind students of the taught patterns it uses.

### Core Learning Experience

- Teacher models reading the short controlled text once.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students reread with choral support, pointer tracking, and teacher prompting.

### Check for Understanding

- Students successfully participate in rereading the controlled text.

### Closing

- Review that the class can now use known sounds and letters in a real text.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the text very short.
- Provide strong picture support and teacher modeling.
- Avoid requiring independent reading beyond the supported routine.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses supported entry into controlled text reading.
- Evidence matches the objective through direct reading of the text.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Controlled text must still sound like real language.
- Avoid awkward or overly synthetic text just because it is decodable.
