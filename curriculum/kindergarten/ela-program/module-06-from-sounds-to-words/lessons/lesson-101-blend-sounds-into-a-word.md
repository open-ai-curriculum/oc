## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.E
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will orally blend a short sequence of taught sounds into a whole word with support.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - sound cards or picture cards
  - counters or sound markers
- Teacher preparation:
  - choose a small set of simple taught words

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Explain that today the class will put sounds together to make words.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model stretching two or three simple sounds, then blending them into one word.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students slide counters or move their hands as they blend.
- Repeat with several familiar examples.

### Check for Understanding

- Students blend at least one simple sound sequence into a word.

### Closing

- Review that blending means putting sounds together.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use movement and visual markers.
- Keep the word set very small.
- Allow students to echo before attempting independently.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses oral blending.
- Evidence matches the objective through live sound combination.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Stay in taught sound patterns only.
- Keep the routine oral before moving to print.
