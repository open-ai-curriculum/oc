## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.2.D
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will segment a familiar simple word into its spoken parts with support.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - picture cards
  - counters or tapping boxes
- Teacher preparation:
  - select a few familiar segmentable words using taught patterns

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Review that blending puts sounds together, while segmenting pulls them apart.

### Core Learning Experience

- Model saying a simple word and tapping each sound separately.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students tap, move counters, or step the sounds in familiar words.

### Check for Understanding

- Students segment at least one familiar word into spoken parts.

### Closing

- Review that readers and writers listen carefully to the parts in words.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Use movement and counters.
- Keep the words short and familiar.
- Accept approximate oral responses with teacher refinement.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses oral segmenting.
- Evidence matches the objective through direct spoken-part analysis.

## Subject-Specific Notes
