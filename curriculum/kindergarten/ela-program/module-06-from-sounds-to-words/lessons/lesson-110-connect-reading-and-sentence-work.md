## Standards and Objective

- Standards addressed:
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RF.K.4
  - CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.K.2
- Lesson objective:
  - Students will connect what they read in a short controlled text to what they help write in a simple shared sentence.

## Materials and Preparation

- Materials needed:
  - controlled text
  - shared sentence from Lesson 109
- Teacher preparation:
  - prepare a simple comparison prompt

## Lesson Flow

### Opening

- Reread the short controlled text.

### Core Learning Experience

- Reread the shared sentence and compare how both use known words or sounds.

### Guided Practice / Discussion / Investigation

- Students point to matching known letters or words across the two texts.
- Discuss how reading and writing use the same letter-sound knowledge.

### Check for Understanding

- Students identify one connection between the controlled text and the shared sentence.

### Closing

- Review that reading and writing work together.

## Accessibility and Inclusion Notes

- Keep the comparison narrow and visible.
- Use pointing and teacher restatement.
- Avoid overloading students with too much text.

## Assessment Notes

- This lesson assesses transfer between reading and writing routines.
- Evidence matches the objective through explicit text-to-text connection.

## Subject-Specific Notes

- Focus on one or two visible connections only.
- Keep the texts very familiar.
