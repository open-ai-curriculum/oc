# Unit 2 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-017 Count To 6

Extend the counting path beyond 5 while preserving one-to-one movement. Students should see that the routine is the same even though the set is bigger.

## KMATH-018 Count To 7

Keep the set visually organized at first. Watch whether students lose track near the end of the count.

## KMATH-019 Count To 8

Offer sets in different arrangements but keep the total small enough that students can still be successful with support.

## KMATH-020 Count To 9

Use repeatable materials and emphasize steady pacing. Encourage recounting when uncertainty appears.

## KMATH-021 Count To 10

Launch 10 as the biggest set so far and help students organize their count carefully. Reinforce the total after counting.

## KMATH-022 Match Numerals To Sets Within 10

Bring numeral cards and sets together. Students should connect quantity to symbol, not memorize symbols in isolation.

## KMATH-023 Compare Two Sets Within 10

Guide students to compare with matching or counting. Keep the work concrete and visible.

## KMATH-024 Build A Set That Is More

Ask students to make a group that has more than a given group. The mathematical idea is comparison, not only counting.

## KMATH-025 Build A Set That Is Less

Mirror the “more” routine with “less,” keeping the language and action clear and concrete.

## KMATH-026 Build A Set That Is Equal

Show that two groups can have the same number even when arranged differently. Matching and counting are both valid routes.

## KMATH-027 Represent 10 On A Ten-Frame

Introduce the ten-frame as a structure that helps show a whole quantity. Fill it carefully and discuss what students see.

## KMATH-028 Sort And Count By Category

Sort a mixed set into categories and count one or more of the categories. This connects data and quantity work.

## KMATH-029 Explain How Two Sets Are Different

Ask students to compare sets using quantity language and simple explanation. Accept pointing and teacher-recorded language if needed.

## KMATH-030 Review Numbers To 10

Run a cumulative review of count, show, and compare routines within 10. Keep materials familiar and transitions brisk.

## KMATH-031 Benchmark Practice

Preview the benchmark tasks with the same materials that students have used instructionally. Reduce novelty so the benchmark measures skill, not confusion.

## KMATH-032 Benchmark Window 1

Use a short benchmark routine with counting, matching, comparing, and ten-frame evidence. Record both accuracy and support used.
