## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students extend counting routines to 10, compare sets within 10, sort and count categories, and begin using ten-frames as a stable representation.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Counting to 10 and comparing quantities are core Kindergarten math foundations. Students need repeated practice with real quantities and visual structures before later composition and operations work.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- counting can be extended beyond 5 with the same one-to-one rules
- sets within 10 can be compared by matching, counting, or discussing quantity
- ten-frames help show and talk about quantity

### Students should know:

- number words to 10
- comparison language: more, less, equal, same number
- that categories can be counted after sorting

### Students should be able to do:

- count sets to 10 accurately
- match numerals to sets within 10
- compare two sets within 10
- represent 10 on a ten-frame
- explain how two sets are alike or different

## Essential Questions

- How do we count larger sets carefully?
- How can we compare two groups fairly?
- How can a ten-frame help us show how many?

## Assessment Evidence

### Summative evidence

- Students complete a benchmark-style unit conference in which they:
  - count a set within 10
  - match a numeral to a quantity
  - compare two sets
  - show a quantity on a ten-frame

### Formative evidence

- daily counting routines
- quantity matching
- compare-and-explain tasks
- category count tasks

## Lesson Sequence

### Week 5

- KMATH-017 through KMATH-020
- focus: extend counting to 6, 7, 8, and 9

### Week 6

- KMATH-021 through KMATH-024
- focus: count to 10, match numerals, compare sets, build a set that is more

### Week 7

- KMATH-025 through KMATH-028
- focus: build sets that are less or equal, represent 10 on a ten-frame, sort and count categories

### Week 8

- KMATH-029 through KMATH-032
- focus: explain set differences, review numbers to 10, benchmark practice, benchmark window 1

## Accessibility And Inclusion Notes

- use real objects, ten-frames, compare mats, and category mats
- allow touching, moving, drawing, circling, and oral responses
- keep the quantity load stable before adding verbal complexity

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - counting larger sets without organizing them
  - comparing by visual impression alone
  - introducing ten-frames as a speed tool instead of a meaning tool
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - set layouts to 10
  - compare tasks
  - ten-frame representations
