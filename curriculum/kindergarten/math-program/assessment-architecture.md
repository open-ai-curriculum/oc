# Kindergarten Math Assessment Architecture

## Purpose

Define the recurring assessment system for the Kindergarten Math commercialization build.

## Assessment Layers

### Daily Formative Checks

Examples:

- quick count-and-show
- compare two sets
- identify a shape or attribute
- explain a math move with counters or drawings

### Unit-End Checks

Each unit should include:

- 1 short end-of-unit assessment
- 1 observation or performance-based check
- scoring guidance
- exemplar evidence descriptions

### Benchmark Windows

The annual math program will use:

- Window 1: early counting and comparison
- Window 2: composition, shapes, and first operations
- Window 3: teen numbers, measurement, and data
- Window 4: end-of-year cumulative readiness

## Evidence Types

Valid math evidence may include:

- oral counting
- touching or moving objects
- pointing
- sorting
- drawing
- circling
- teacher-recorded explanation
- acting out a math story

## Calibration Requirements

Future units should include:

- conference or observation forms
- secure / developing / not yet decision rules
- exemplar evidence descriptions
- notes on multilingual and accessibility interpretation

## Reporting Expectations

The Kindergarten Math system should eventually support:

- benchmark summaries
- unit-end summaries
- Grade 1 transition notes

## Status

Draft commercialization architecture. Final benchmark instruments and reporting tools are not yet authored.
