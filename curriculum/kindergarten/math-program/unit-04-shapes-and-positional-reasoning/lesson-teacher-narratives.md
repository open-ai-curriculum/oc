# Unit 4 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-049 Identify Flat And Solid Shapes

Start with real objects and shape models so students can feel and see the difference between flat and solid forms.

## KMATH-050 Name Circles, Triangles, Rectangles, And Squares

Introduce a small shape set and revisit it often. Keep the focus on the shape name staying the same across examples.

## KMATH-051 Describe Shapes By Attributes

Prompt students to talk about sides, corners, or whether a shape rolls or stacks, depending on the task. Use concrete prompts.

## KMATH-052 Sort Shapes By Attributes

Model one sort rule at a time and ask students to explain the grouping with simple language or pointing.

## KMATH-053 Find Shapes In The Classroom

Turn the room into a geometry environment. Ask students to match classroom objects to known shapes.

## KMATH-054 Use Positional Words With Objects

Place real objects and describe their locations. Have students move objects as they say the words.

## KMATH-055 Build A Picture From Shapes

Use cutout shapes to build a simple image. Discuss which shapes were used and where they are placed.

## KMATH-056 Compare Shapes That Are The Same And Different

Place two shapes side by side and ask how they match and differ. Keep the comparison anchored in visible attributes.

## KMATH-057 Compose A Larger Shape From Smaller Shapes

Use several smaller shapes to make a larger or combined form. Emphasize that shapes can work together.

## KMATH-058 Describe Where An Object Is

Return to positional language with concrete classroom arrangements. Ask students to place and describe.

## KMATH-059 Review Shape Names And Attributes

Run a cumulative review of shape names and key attributes using familiar examples.

## KMATH-060 Review Position Words

Use a quick movement-based review so students hear and enact the words.

## KMATH-061 Explain A Shape Sort

Ask students to sort and then explain the rule they used. Accept gesture and teacher-recorded language if needed.

## KMATH-062 Build And Describe A Shape Design

Invite students to build a design and then name the shapes and positions they used.

## KMATH-063 Benchmark Practice

Use familiar shape and position tasks to preview the benchmark format without adding novelty.

## KMATH-064 Benchmark Window 2

Record evidence on shape identification, sorting, composition, and positional language using stable materials and prompts.
