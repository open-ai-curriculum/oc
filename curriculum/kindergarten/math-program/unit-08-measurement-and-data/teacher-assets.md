# Unit 8 Teacher Assets

## Weekly Teacher Overview

### Week 29

- priority:
  - establish direct comparison with real objects before any recording task
- emphasize:
  - compare one shared attribute at a time

### Week 30

- priority:
  - build class data routines students can see and manipulate
- emphasize:
  - students should help create the graph, not only read it

### Week 31

- priority:
  - introduce nonstandard measurement carefully and consistently
- emphasize:
  - use the same unit repeatedly without gaps or overlaps

### Week 32

- priority:
  - consolidate graph interpretation and measurement language
- emphasize:
  - ask students what the graph shows before asking abstract comparison questions

## Preparation Notes

- gather classroom objects with clear contrast in length, weight, and other attributes
- prepare category cards and large graph spaces students can manipulate
- keep nonstandard units consistent within a task

## Family Communication Asset

This month students are comparing objects and making simple graphs. Families can help by asking children to compare two household objects as longer or shorter, or by making a small tally or picture graph about favorite snacks or books.

## Intervention And Extension

### Intervention

- reduce the number of objects and categories in comparison and graph tasks
- use gesture, repeated vocabulary, and one shared attribute at a time

### Extension

- ask students to create the class question for a graph
- compare measurement results across two objects and explain why one used more units
