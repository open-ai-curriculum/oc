## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students count beyond 10, build and describe teen numbers with ten-and-some-ones language, match numerals to concrete representations, and compare teen quantities using frames, counters, and drawings.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Teen numbers are often recited without place-value meaning. Kindergarten learners need repeated concrete experiences showing that a teen number is one ten and some ones so later number work in Grade 1 has a stable base.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- teen numbers can be built from one full ten and some extra ones
- counting beyond 10 continues the number sequence in order
- numeral names, spoken number words, and concrete representations can all refer to the same quantity

### Students should know:

- the teen numerals from 11 to 19
- that a ten-frame or stick of ten can help organize a teen number
- that teen numbers can be compared by looking at their ones after the ten is fixed

### Students should be able to do:

- count aloud beyond 10
- build teen numbers with concrete materials
- explain a teen number as ten and some ones
- match a teen numeral to a representation and compare teen quantities

## Essential Questions

- What makes a number a teen number?
- How can we show ten and some ones?
- How can frames and materials help us compare teen numbers?

## Accessibility And Inclusion Notes

- use full ten-frames, linking cubes, bundles of ten, numeral cards, and oral counting supports
- allow pointing, touching, moving counters, verbal explanations, and teacher-recorded language
- separate the ten from the extra ones visually before expecting verbal explanation

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - treating teen numbers as memorized labels instead of ten-and-ones structures
  - moving to symbolic comparison without concrete models
  - overloading students with multiple new representations at once
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - teen numeral naming prompts
  - ten-frame and bundle models
  - comparison tasks using teen numbers
