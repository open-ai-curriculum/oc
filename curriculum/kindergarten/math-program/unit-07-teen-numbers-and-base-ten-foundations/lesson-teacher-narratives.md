# Unit 7 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-097 Count Beyond 10

Extend familiar counting routines past 10 with objects, movements, and spoken sequence support. The goal is stability, not speed.

## KMATH-098 Show 11 As Ten And One

Model a full ten and one extra one. Use explicit language: "one ten and one more."

## KMATH-099 Show 12 As Ten And Two

Repeat the structure with 12 so students see the pattern, not a disconnected fact. Keep the ten fixed and add two ones.

## KMATH-100 Show 13 And 14 With Concrete Materials

Build 13 and 14 side by side. Discuss how both have one ten but different numbers of ones.

## KMATH-101 Show 15 And 16 With Concrete Materials

Continue the teen sequence using familiar materials. Encourage students to say the number name and the ten-and-ones structure together.

## KMATH-102 Show 17, 18, And 19 With Concrete Materials

Complete the teen sequence and notice how the ones increase while the ten stays constant.

## KMATH-103 Match Teen Numerals To Representations

Present multiple concrete models and numeral cards. Ask students to match carefully and justify orally or by pointing.

## KMATH-104 Explain A Teen Number As Ten And Some Ones

Invite repeated language practice with sentence stems such as "14 is ten and four ones."

## KMATH-105 Compare Teen Numbers With Materials

Place two teen numbers side by side and compare the ones after confirming both have one ten.

## KMATH-106 Build Teen Numbers On A Frame

Use full and partial ten-frames to reinforce structured representations. Avoid loose counting alone.

## KMATH-107 Review Counting Beyond 10

Run mixed routines that move from oral count sequence to concrete model and back again.

## KMATH-108 Review Teen-Number Representations

Ask students to identify the same teen number across frames, bundles, cubes, and numerals.

## KMATH-109 Benchmark Practice

Use familiar teen-number prompts in a calm format so students practice the assessment routine without new content.

## KMATH-110 Benchmark Window 3

Gather evidence of counting beyond 10, numeral matching, and ten-and-ones explanation with minimal scaffolding.

## KMATH-111 Unit 7 Performance Task

Have students build, label, and explain one teen number using concrete materials and oral language.

## KMATH-112 Unit 7 Check

Record whether students can recognize, build, and explain teen numbers with stable teacher prompts and known tools.
