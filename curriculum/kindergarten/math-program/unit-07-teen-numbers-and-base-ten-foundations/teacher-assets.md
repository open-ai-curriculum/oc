# Unit 7 Teacher Assets

## Weekly Teacher Overview

### Week 25

- priority:
  - count beyond 10 and establish teen numbers as quantities, not just names
- emphasize:
  - keep one full ten visible whenever possible

### Week 26

- priority:
  - deepen construction of 15 through 19 and connect spoken names to models
- emphasize:
  - say "ten and ___ ones" alongside the teen name

### Week 27

- priority:
  - compare teen numbers and stabilize numeral-to-model matching
- emphasize:
  - the ten stays the same; the ones change

### Week 28

- priority:
  - consolidate teen-number understanding and gather benchmark evidence
- emphasize:
  - use familiar representations and clear oral prompts

## Preparation Notes

- prepare ten-frames, linking cubes, numeral cards, and bundle models
- prebuild example teen numbers for teacher modeling
- keep comparison mats available for side-by-side teen-number work

## Family Communication Asset

This month students are learning that teen numbers are made of one ten and some extra ones. Families can help by counting collections beyond 10 and asking children to show a number like 14 as ten and four more.

## Intervention And Extension

### Intervention

- return to 11, 12, and 13 with one fixed ten-frame and loose counters
- keep teen numbers concrete and avoid rushing to numeral writing

### Extension

- compare two teen numbers and explain which has more ones
- represent the same teen number with different concrete materials
