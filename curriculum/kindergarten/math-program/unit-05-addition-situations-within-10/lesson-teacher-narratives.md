# Unit 5 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-065 Act Out A Put-Together Story

Use two visible groups and physically combine them. Keep the language concrete: “Here are some. Here are some more. Now they are together.”

## KMATH-066 Draw An Add-To Story

Translate the acted story into a drawing. The drawing should still show the change clearly, not only the final total.

## KMATH-067 Use Counters To Show Joining Sets

Ask students to build the two groups first, then join them. This helps preserve the structure of the addition situation.

## KMATH-068 Show How Many After Adding One

Use the simplest add-to situation so students can focus on the idea of one more. Keep the number range small.

## KMATH-069 Show How Many After Adding More

Extend the joining routine beyond adding one while preserving story clarity. Use counters and retell the action aloud.

## KMATH-070 Match A Story To A Drawing

Present a simple story and two or more drawings, then ask which drawing fits. This reinforces representation matching.

## KMATH-071 Explain What Happened In A Math Story

Invite students to say, point, or act what changed in the story. The explanation matters as much as the total.

## KMATH-072 Show Addition With A Ten-Frame

Use a ten-frame after students understand the joining action. The frame should clarify quantity, not replace meaning.

## KMATH-073 Compare Two Add-To Stories

Place two similar addition stories side by side and ask how they are alike and different. Focus on quantities and action.

## KMATH-074 Use A Number Sentence With Teacher Support

Introduce a teacher-recorded number sentence only after the story and model are secure. Keep the equation tied to the action.

## KMATH-075 Solve An Addition Story With Objects

Ask students to solve a concrete story independently or with routine support using counters or manipulatives.

## KMATH-076 Solve An Addition Story With Drawings

Shift the solving path from objects to drawings when appropriate, but keep the story context explicit.

## KMATH-077 Mixed Review Of Numbers And Adding

Blend counting and addition tasks so students continue to connect operations to quantity, not only procedure.

## KMATH-078 Unit 5 Performance Check

Use one addition story, one model, and one explanation task to gather concrete evidence of understanding.

## KMATH-079 Practice Benchmark Routines

Preview the style of tasks students will see in later benchmark work using familiar addition routines and materials.

## KMATH-080 Unit 5 Check

Record whether students can model, solve, and explain addition situations with routine prompts and familiar tools.
