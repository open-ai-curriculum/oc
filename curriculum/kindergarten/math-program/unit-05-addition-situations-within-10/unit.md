## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students model addition situations within 10 by acting out stories, drawing, using counters, showing quantities on ten-frames, and beginning to connect stories to teacher-supported number sentences.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Kindergarten addition should emerge from concrete joining and put-together situations that children can see, move, and explain. This unit builds early operations meaning before formal symbolic fluency.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- addition situations can show putting together or adding to
- joining quantities changes how many there are altogether
- objects, drawings, frames, and stories can all represent the same addition idea

### Students should know:

- that an addition story has parts and a total
- that counters and drawings can help solve a joining problem
- that a teacher-supported number sentence can record a story

### Students should be able to do:

- act out a simple addition story
- draw a joining story
- use counters or a ten-frame to show the result
- explain what happened in a math story

## Essential Questions

- What happens when we put two groups together?
- How can a math story show adding?
- How can counters, drawings, and frames help us solve a joining problem?

## Accessibility And Inclusion Notes

- use story props, counters, drawings, ten-frames, and teacher-recorded explanations
- allow acting, pointing, moving objects, drawing, circling, and oral explanations
- keep the action of the story visible before introducing symbolic recording

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - reducing addition to answer-getting without story meaning
  - moving to equations before students understand the joining action
  - using large quantities before the situation is conceptually secure
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - story contexts
  - ten-frame addition prompts
  - teacher-supported number sentence use
