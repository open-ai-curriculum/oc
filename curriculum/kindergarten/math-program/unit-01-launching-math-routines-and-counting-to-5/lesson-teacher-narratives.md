# Unit 1 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-001 Count Up To 3 Objects And Say How Many

Use a very small set and model touching one object at a time. End by restating the last number as the total so students hear the cardinality idea immediately.

## KMATH-002 Count Up To 5 Objects With One-To-One Pointing

Extend the routine to 5 while preserving slow, visible counting. Watch for skipping or double-counting and correct through re-counting, not verbal correction alone.

## KMATH-003 Show A Set That Matches A Number Word

Say a number word and ask students to build that quantity with objects. The key move is checking the built set together and naming how many.

## KMATH-004 Compare Two Small Sets As More Or Less

Place two small sets side by side and guide students to compare by matching or counting. Keep the language anchored in actual quantities, not visual impression alone.

## KMATH-005 Sort Objects By One Attribute

Use one obvious sort rule such as color or type. After sorting, count one category and discuss what stayed the same and what changed.

## KMATH-006 Count Sounds, Movements, And Objects

Broaden counting beyond physical objects to show that counting tracks events as well as things. Keep each count sequence short and repeatable.

## KMATH-007 Match Numeral To Quantity Within 5

Show one numeral card and one set at a time. Students should say, build, or point to the matching quantity.

## KMATH-008 Explain How You Know How Many

Invite short explanations after counting. Accept gesture and teacher-recorded language if needed, but keep the explanation tied to the counted set.

## KMATH-009 Count Scattered Objects Within 5

Use scattered sets so students must organize their attention. Model moving or touching objects deliberately if needed.

## KMATH-010 Compare Two Sets As Equal Or Not Equal

Guide students to decide whether two small sets match. Use matching, counting, or rearranging strategies.

## KMATH-011 Sort And Count Groups

Combine sorting and counting by asking students to sort a mixed set and then count one or more groups. This builds early category-and-quantity thinking.

## KMATH-012 Count On A Counting Mat

Use a stable mat to reinforce one-to-one correspondence. Students should move objects into each space and then say the total.

## KMATH-013 Count And Circle The Total

Link concrete counting to a very light recording path. Students count a set, then circle a numeral or quantity marker.

## KMATH-014 Show 5 In Different Ways

Ask students to build 5 with two different arrangements. This plants the earliest seed for composition without formal part-whole language yet.

## KMATH-015 Review Counting And Comparing Routines

Run a brisk mixed review of count, show, sort, and compare routines. Use familiar materials so the focus is consolidation.

## KMATH-016 Unit 1 Check

Use a short conference or station-based check with the same objects and mats students already know. Record both accuracy and level of support.
