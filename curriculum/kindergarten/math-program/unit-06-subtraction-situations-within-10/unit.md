## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students model subtraction situations within 10 by acting out stories, removing counters, drawing changes, finding what is left, and connecting stories to teacher-supported number sentences.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - Kindergarten subtraction should emerge from visible change: taking away, taking apart, and comparing what is left. This unit builds that understanding through concrete action before formal symbolic expectations.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- subtraction situations can show taking away or taking apart
- removing or separating changes how many remain
- objects, drawings, and stories can all represent the same subtraction idea

### Students should know:

- subtraction stories begin with a whole amount and show a change
- counters and drawings can help find what is left
- a teacher-supported number sentence can record the story

### Students should be able to do:

- act out a subtraction story
- draw a take-away story
- use counters to find what is left
- explain what changed in a story

## Essential Questions

- What happens when some are taken away?
- How can a story show subtraction?
- How can counters and drawings help us find what is left?

## Accessibility And Inclusion Notes

- use story props, counters, drawings, and teacher-recorded explanations
- allow acting, pointing, removing objects, drawing, circling, and oral explanations
- keep the change in the story visible before introducing symbolic recording

## Subject-Specific Quality Notes

- What discipline-specific risks need attention?
  - treating subtraction as isolated answer-getting instead of change
  - moving too quickly from action to equations
  - confusing subtraction with comparison when the story structure is unclear
- What content or task types require extra review?
  - story contexts
  - missing-part prompts
  - teacher-supported number sentence use
