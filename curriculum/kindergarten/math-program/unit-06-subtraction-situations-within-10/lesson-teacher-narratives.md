# Unit 6 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-081 Act Out A Take-Away Story

Begin with a whole set, remove some, and ask what is left. Keep the removal visible and slow so the change is unmistakable.

## KMATH-082 Draw A Take-Away Story

Translate the acted subtraction story into a drawing. Show the starting amount, the change, and what remains.

## KMATH-083 Use Counters To Show Taking Away

Model building the whole first, then removing counters. This helps students keep the story structure intact.

## KMATH-084 Show How Many Are Left

Focus on what remains after a visible subtraction action. Recount if needed, but keep the action central.

## KMATH-085 Compare Add-To And Take-Away Stories

Place one addition story and one subtraction story side by side. Discuss what changed in each and what stayed the same.

## KMATH-086 Find A Missing Part With Concrete Objects

Use counters to show the whole and one part, then ask what is missing. Keep the quantities small and concrete.

## KMATH-087 Explain What Changed In A Subtraction Story

Invite students to explain the subtraction action in words, gestures, or with teacher-recorded language.

## KMATH-088 Match A Subtraction Story To A Drawing

Present a subtraction story and multiple drawings, then ask which one matches the story structure.

## KMATH-089 Use A Number Sentence With Teacher Support

Introduce a teacher-supported subtraction sentence only after the story and model are secure. Keep the recording tied to the action.

## KMATH-090 Solve A Subtraction Story With Objects

Have students solve a take-away problem with counters or props. Watch whether they preserve the starting amount and removal step.

## KMATH-091 Solve A Subtraction Story With Drawings

Move from concrete objects to drawing while keeping the change visible. The drawing should show the whole, the part removed, and what remains.

## KMATH-092 Review Subtraction Routines

Run a mixed review of act-out, model, and explain routines using familiar small-number stories.

## KMATH-093 Mixed Addition And Subtraction Review

Ask students to distinguish which stories are joining and which are taking away. This helps stabilize operations meaning.

## KMATH-094 Unit 6 Performance Check

Use one subtraction story, one model, and one explanation task to gather concrete evidence of understanding.

## KMATH-095 Practice Mixed Problem Solving

Preview benchmark-style mixed problem routines using familiar addition and subtraction contexts.

## KMATH-096 Unit 6 Check

Record whether students can model, solve, and explain subtraction situations with routine prompts and familiar tools.
