# Unit 3 Lesson Teacher Narratives

## KMATH-033 Make 4 In Two Ways

Use counters to show 4, then rearrange into two groups. Keep the total visible so students see that the number stays the same.

## KMATH-034 Make 5 In Two Ways

Extend the make-and-break routine to 5. Encourage students to verbalize or point to the two parts and the whole.

## KMATH-035 Make 6 In Two Ways

Introduce 6 carefully with concrete models. Watch that students still preserve total quantity when rearranging.

## KMATH-036 Build And Break Apart 7

Use a part-whole mat so students can see where the two parts and total belong. Keep the discussion concrete.

## KMATH-037 Build And Break Apart 8

Repeat the part-whole routine with 8 using counters first. Ask students what changed and what stayed the same.

## KMATH-038 Show Parts And Whole With Objects

Have students build the whole and then separate it into two parts. Reinforce the connection between the parts and the total.

## KMATH-039 Show Parts And Whole With Drawings

Translate object work into simple drawings. Keep the drawing load light so the mathematics stays central.

## KMATH-040 Use A Ten-Frame To Show The Same Number In Two Ways

Use the ten-frame as a representation, not a speed drill. Ask students to notice how the same total can look different.

## KMATH-041 Explain Two Ways To Make A Number

Invite short oral explanations with objects or drawings. Accept teacher-recorded language if needed.

## KMATH-042 Find A Missing Part With Objects

Build the whole and one part, then ask what is missing. Let students solve with counters before discussion.

## KMATH-043 Compare Two Ways To Make The Same Number

Place two representations side by side and ask how they are alike and different. The goal is structure, not only answer-getting.

## KMATH-044 Record A Math Story With Pictures

Tell a simple math story and have students draw the parts and whole. Keep the story grounded in action or objects.

## KMATH-045 Review Compose And Decompose Routines

Run a mixed review of make, break apart, and explain routines with familiar numbers.

## KMATH-046 Practice Mixed Counting And Composition

Mix counting and part-whole work so students connect composition back to quantity, not just memorized pairs.

## KMATH-047 Unit 3 Assessment Task

Use a concrete conference task where students show a number in two ways and explain a missing part with support.

## KMATH-048 Unit 3 Check

Record what students can do with familiar materials and routine prompts. Note support levels carefully.
