# Unit 9 Teacher Assets

## Weekly Teacher Overview

### Week 33

- priority:
  - reconnect counting, composition, geometry, and addition ideas
- emphasize:
  - use familiar routines to rebuild confidence and coherence

### Week 34

- priority:
  - review subtraction, teen numbers, measurement, and data without overloading students
- emphasize:
  - keep tasks short and concrete

### Week 35

- priority:
  - gather final benchmark and explanation evidence
- emphasize:
  - accept multimodal evidence, not only verbal precision

### Week 36

- priority:
  - use center review and reflection to capture transition-ready strengths and needs
- emphasize:
  - look for consistency across settings, not one isolated performance

## Preparation Notes

- gather familiar manipulatives from prior units into stable review tubs
- prepare center rotation cards and evidence-recording sheets
- preselect mixed tasks that sample the year's domains without crowding too many into one block

## Family Communication Asset

This month students are reviewing the big math ideas from Kindergarten and showing what they know in different ways. Families can help by counting, comparing, solving small story problems, and asking children to explain their thinking with objects or drawings.

## Intervention And Extension

### Intervention

- revisit one domain at a time during center review
- use smaller number ranges and more concrete materials

### Extension

- ask students to connect two domains in one explanation, such as a shape sort with counting or a teen number with comparison
- invite students to describe which math idea feels easiest and why
