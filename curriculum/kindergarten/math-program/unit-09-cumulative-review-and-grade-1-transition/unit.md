## Unit Purpose

- What is the unit about?
  - Students revisit and consolidate the year's major mathematics through mixed counting, composition, operations, teen-number, geometry, measurement, and data routines while teachers gather transition-ready evidence for Grade 1.
- Why does it matter for learners?
  - End-of-year review should do more than repeat isolated skills. Kindergarten students need structured opportunities to reconnect ideas, show flexible understanding with familiar tools, and leave the year with a stable base for Grade 1 mathematics.

## Desired Results

### Students should understand:

- the year's math ideas connect across counting, operations, place value, geometry, measurement, and data
- familiar tools can help solve different kinds of math tasks
- review can reveal what they already know and what still needs support

### Students should know:

- counting, comparing, composing, operating, and representing routines from across the year
- that teen numbers are one ten and some ones
- that math thinking can be shown with objects, drawings, words, and teacher-recorded explanations

### Students should be able to do:

- solve mixed kindergarten math tasks with familiar supports
- explain a math idea using pictures, objects, gestures, or words
- participate in center-based review routines
- show readiness evidence across the major kindergarten domains

## Essential Questions

- What math ideas do we know well now?
- How can we use our tools and pictures to show our thinking?
- How do the math ideas from this year fit together?

## Accessibility And Inclusion Notes

- use familiar materials from earlier units so cognitive demand stays on the math idea, not the tool
- allow objects, drawings, gestures, oral explanation, pointing, and teacher-recorded responses
- organize review tasks into short, predictable formats with repeated language supports
