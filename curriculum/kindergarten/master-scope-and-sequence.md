# Kindergarten Master Scope And Sequence

## Purpose

Provide the first full-year planning spine for kindergarten across literacy, math, science, social studies, arts, health, PE, and technology.

## Planning Assumptions

- 36 instructional weeks
- literacy and math taught daily
- science and social studies taught in recurring module bands
- arts, health, PE, and technology appear in recurring weekly cycles and thematic integrations
- pacing remains draft until subject specialists review it

## Year Structure

### Quarter 1: School Launch And Foundational Routines

Weeks 1-9

- literacy:
  - print concepts
  - oral language and listening routines
  - story retell
  - rhyme and phonological awareness
  - letter recognition launch
- math:
  - counting to 5
  - one-to-one correspondence
  - comparing small sets
  - sorting and classifying
- science:
  - observing weather
  - noticing patterns in the sky and seasons
- social studies:
  - family, school, and classroom community
  - roles, rules, and routines
- arts:
  - creating with classroom materials
  - sharing and responding to artwork
- health:
  - feelings, routines, healthy choices, and safety basics
- PE:
  - safe movement space
  - locomotor basics
  - movement control and stopping
- technology:
  - safe tool handling
  - first, next, last sequencing

### Quarter 2: Pattern, Sound, Number, And Observation Growth

Weeks 10-18

- literacy:
  - stronger phonological awareness
  - uppercase/lowercase fluency
  - initial sound awareness
  - vocabulary growth through read-aloud
  - early shared writing
- math:
  - numbers to 10
  - comparing sets
  - composing and decomposing small numbers
  - describing shapes and positional language
- science:
  - weather records over time
  - living things and basic needs
- social studies:
  - helpers in the school and neighborhood
  - maps, places, and community spaces
- arts:
  - pattern in music, visual art, and movement
  - representing stories and ideas through art
- health:
  - hygiene
  - body awareness
  - healthy food and rest routines
- PE:
  - balance
  - pathway movement
  - following movement directions with partners
- technology:
  - sequencing with pictures and oral steps
  - safe collaborative technology routines

### Quarter 3: Applying Skills In Connected Tasks

Weeks 19-27

- literacy:
  - early decoding and encoding
  - retelling with greater detail
  - responding to text through speaking, drawing, and shared writing
  - informational listening and key details
- math:
  - addition and subtraction situations within 10
  - teen-number foundations
  - measurement comparisons
  - shape composition and comparison
- science:
  - plant and animal needs
  - observation journals
  - simple investigations
- social studies:
  - symbols, traditions, and shared community life
  - past and present in familiar settings
- arts:
  - revising and presenting a creative work
  - dramatic and musical response to stories
- health:
  - emotions and self-regulation
  - safe choices in common situations
- PE:
  - coordination sequences
  - rhythm and movement patterns
  - cooperative games
- technology:
  - simple algorithmic thinking
  - digital citizenship and turn-taking

### Quarter 4: Consolidation, Expression, And Transition To Grade 1

Weeks 28-36

- literacy:
  - broader application of foundational skills
  - stronger oral and written response
  - cumulative read-aloud comprehension
  - speaking and listening for sharing and explanation
- math:
  - counting beyond 10
  - operations review
  - data and comparison review
  - shapes and spatial review
- science:
  - comparison of observations across time
  - communicating findings with drawings, labels, and oral explanation
- social studies:
  - citizenship and responsibility review
  - community participation and helping roles
- arts:
  - culminating sharing and performance
  - reflection on creative choices
- health:
  - safe habits review
  - self-care and help-seeking routines
- PE:
  - coordinated movement review
  - game participation and self-control
- technology:
  - review of safe tool use
  - sequencing and simple problem solving

## Benchmark And Review Windows

### Window 1: Weeks 8-9

- literacy launch review
- counting and routine check
- teacher observation of school-entry readiness behaviors

### Window 2: Weeks 17-18

- midyear literacy review
- midyear math review
- content-subject observational synthesis

### Window 3: Weeks 26-27

- applied literacy and math review
- science and social studies evidence review
- support planning for final quarter

### Window 4: Weeks 35-36

- end-of-year synthesis
- transition notes for Grade 1
- family-facing summary artifacts
