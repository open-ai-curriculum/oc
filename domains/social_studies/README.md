# Social Studies Domain

## Purpose

This domain defines a governed `social_studies` knowledge graph using the same learning spine and graph architecture already established elsewhere in the repository.

The domain is intentionally modeled as one K-12 dependency-aware social studies system rather than as disconnected course silos. Shared inquiry, evidence, explanation, and public-communication capabilities sit upstream of history, civics, geography, and economics branches.

## Scope

The current scaffold covers the full intended territory at the family level:

- shared inquiry, evidence, explanation, and disciplinary discourse
- history and historical-source reasoning
- civics, institutions, participation, and policy reasoning
- geography, place, spatial reasoning, and human-environment interaction
- economics, exchange, incentives, systems, and tradeoff analysis
- advanced secondary synthesis, argument, and public communication

The intended target scope for this domain is:

- kindergarten through Grade 12 social studies capability modeling
- cross-disciplinary inquiry and evidence use across social studies strands
- advanced secondary analysis, modeling, and public-argument pathways

## Initial Downstream Slice

The first maintained downstream slice in this domain is an inquiry-to-discipline spine:

- observation, notice, questioning, and evidence talk
- supported explanation from maps, images, artifacts, and texts
- chronology and historical-source comparison
- community, rules, institutions, and civic participation
- maps, place, movement, and human-environment interaction
- needs, wants, exchange, and incentives

The currently seeded node families are:

- `Q` inquiry, evidence, and discourse
- `H` history and source reasoning
- `C` civics and collective decision-making
- `G` geography and human-environment systems
- `E` economics and exchange systems
- `A` advanced secondary synthesis and public argument

This slice is meant to preserve the same repository discipline already used in other domains:

- explicit dependencies
- mastery gates
- classified failure modes
- bounded interventions
- learner-state observability

## Claim Boundary

This domain package establishes governed structure for social studies node work from kindergarten through Grade 12. It does not by itself constitute classroom-ready curriculum, reviewed standards alignment, validated learner-state logic, or production-grade sequencing.
