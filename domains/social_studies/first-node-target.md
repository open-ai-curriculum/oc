# First Social Studies Node Target

## Purpose

Identify the first concrete node-package wave for the `social_studies` domain.

## Recommendation

The first implementation wave should begin at the real entry point of the domain:

1. `Q1` observe, describe, and question
2. `Q2` compare, classify, and sequence evidence
3. `H1` chronology, identity, and community memory
4. `C1` community rules, roles, and fairness
5. `G1` place, maps, and environmental features
6. `E1` needs, wants, choice, and exchange

## Why This Wave

This is the highest-value first build because:

- it starts at shared inquiry and evidence rather than assuming later disciplinary readiness
- it preserves one integrated inquiry entry surface for social studies
- it gives every major social studies branch a governed starting node
- it creates the earliest learner-state surface for the domain without over-claiming K-12 completeness

## Immediate Follow-On Wave

The next implementation wave should deepen the first explanatory layer:

1. `Q3` supported explanation from evidence
2. `H2` historical sources and past-present comparison
3. `C2` institutions, rights, responsibilities, and processes
4. `G2` human-environment interaction and regions
5. `E2` production, consumption, and local markets

## Secondary Frontier

After the first two waves, the next priorities should be:

1. `Q4` investigation design, representation, and data use
2. `H3` causation, context, and change over time
3. `C3` civic decision-making and deliberation
4. `G3` spatial patterns, movement, and resources
5. `E3` incentives, trade, and simple systems

## Next Phase

Once the entry and explanatory layers are fully specified, the next phase should shift to:

1. adding node packages at the middle-school and early-secondary frontier
2. defining failure taxonomies that distinguish evidence, language, and source-access breakdowns
3. attaching artifact registries for source analysis, map/data work, and civic discussion
4. introducing standards overlays only after human review of the domain model

## Claim Boundary

This target document defines implementation priority only. It does not mean the listed nodes are already classroom-ready or fully reviewed.
