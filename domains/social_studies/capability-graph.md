# Social Studies Capability Graph

## Purpose

Provide a scoped view of the initial `social_studies` capability graph for K-12 domain scaffolding.

## Entry Node

- `Q1` observe, describe, and question

## First Priority Wave

1. `Q1` observe, describe, and question
2. `Q2` compare, classify, and sequence evidence
3. `H1` chronology, identity, and community memory
4. `C1` community rules, roles, and fairness
5. `G1` place, maps, and environmental features
6. `E1` needs, wants, choice, and exchange

## Cross-Branch Logic

The graph explicitly includes cross-branch dependencies so later secondary work does not become siloed:

- `H3 -> C4` to preserve historical context inside constitutional and policy reasoning
- `G4 -> C5` to preserve spatial reasoning inside comparative civics
- `G4 -> E5` to preserve scale and movement reasoning inside macroeconomic and trade analysis
- `C4 -> G6` to preserve policy reasoning inside geospatial application
- `C4 -> E6` to preserve public-policy logic inside economic tradeoff analysis
- `H5`, `C5`, `G5`, and `E5` into `A1` to preserve interdisciplinary capstone synthesis

## Frontier

The current family-level frontier is:

- `H6` historiography, public history, and capstone argument
- `C6` advanced civic argument, policy design, and participation
- `G6` geospatial analysis and policy application
- `E6` economic analysis, policy tradeoffs, and capstone modeling
- `A2` capstone argument, modeling, and public communication

## Claim Boundary

This capability graph is a scaffolded planning surface. It does not yet claim node-package completeness, standards review, or production-grade runtime coverage.
