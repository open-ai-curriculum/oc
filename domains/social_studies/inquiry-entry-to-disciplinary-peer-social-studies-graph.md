# Inquiry Entry To Disciplinary Peer Social Studies Graph

## Purpose

Define the broader bounded social studies graph that sits at peer depth around the inquiry-entry and early explanatory branch work.

This document is different from [inquiry-entry-to-explanatory-spine.md](/Volumes/data/development/oc/domains/social_studies/inquiry-entry-to-explanatory-spine.md).

That document answers:

- what must exist before the middle branch layer is legitimate

This document answers:

- what social studies branches should exist at comparable developmental and analytical depth around that entry slice, even before the domain moves fully into advanced secondary capstone territory

## Design Rule

The repository should not confuse:

- a valid prerequisite path

with:

- a sufficiently broad bounded social studies graph

Both are required.

## Graph Scope

The peer-level target here is roughly:

- shared inquiry and evidence foundations
- branch-entry social studies concepts
- first explanatory branch nodes
- first analytic middle branch nodes
- the bridge from disciplinary analysis into advanced synthesis

## Core Branches

### A. Shared Inquiry And Evidence Branch

- `Q1` Observe, describe, and question
- `Q2` Compare, classify, and sequence evidence
- `Q3` Supported explanation from evidence
- `Q4` Investigation design, representation, and data use
- `Q5` Evaluation of sources, models, and claims
- `Q6` Synthesis, argument, and public communication

### B. History And Source Reasoning Branch

- `H1` Chronology, identity, and community memory
- `H2` Historical sources and past-present comparison
- `H3` Causation, context, and change over time
- `H4` Sourcing, corroboration, and historical argument

### C. Civics And Collective Decision-Making Branch

- `C1` Community rules, roles, and fairness
- `C2` Institutions, rights, responsibilities, and processes
- `C3` Civic decision-making and deliberation
- `C4` Constitutional principles and public policy

### D. Geography And Human-Environment Systems Branch

- `G1` Place, maps, and environmental features
- `G2` Human-environment interaction and regions
- `G3` Spatial patterns, movement, and resources
- `G4` Geographic reasoning with maps, data, and scale

### E. Economics And Exchange Systems Branch

- `E1` Needs, wants, choice, and exchange
- `E2` Production, consumption, and local markets
- `E3` Incentives, trade, and simple systems
- `E4` Markets, labor, and public resource decisions

### F. Advanced Secondary Synthesis Bridge

This branch is not yet the full capstone surface. It is the bounded bridge into it.

- `H5` Thematic historical analysis and periodization
- `C5` Comparative government and civic action
- `G5` Global interdependence, migration, and sustainability
- `E5` Macroeconomic patterns, global trade, and finance basics
- `A1` Interdisciplinary research and evidence synthesis

## Why This Graph Matters

This peer graph is the smallest bounded social studies graph that can:

- preserve inquiry as the upstream backbone
- show cross-branch progression through first analytic middle-layer work
- represent the strongest current AP-facing history, civics, geography, and economics dependencies
- bridge into `A1` without pretending the whole advanced-secondary capstone layer is the primary entry surface

## Immediate Operational Uses

This bounded peer graph is the right scope for:

- entry and middle-layer graph audits
- focused export views
- future bounded reporting cuts for the strongest and weakest assessment surfaces
- graph-specific refactor and decomposition decisions that should not require loading the entire PreK-12 domain every time

## Claim Boundary

This graph is a maintained bounded view over the authoritative social studies graph. It does not replace the canonical graph or establish classroom-release claims.
