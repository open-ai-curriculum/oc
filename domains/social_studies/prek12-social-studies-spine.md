# PreK-12 Social Studies Spine

## Purpose

Define the initial governed K-12 spine for the `social_studies` domain.

## Core Modeling Rule

The social studies branches extend a shared inquiry and evidence spine. They do not operate as disconnected topic lists.

## Families

### Q. Inquiry, Evidence, And Discourse

- `Q1` observe, describe, and question
- `Q2` compare, classify, and sequence evidence
- `Q3` supported explanation from evidence
- `Q4` investigation design, representation, and data use
- `Q5` evaluation of sources, models, and claims
- `Q6` synthesis, argument, and public communication

### H. History And Source Reasoning

- `H1` chronology, identity, and community memory
- `H2` historical sources and past-present comparison
- `H3` causation, context, and change over time
- `H4` sourcing, corroboration, and historical argument
- `H5` thematic historical analysis and periodization
- `H6` historiography, public history, and capstone argument

### C. Civics And Collective Decision-Making

- `C1` community rules, roles, and fairness
- `C2` institutions, rights, responsibilities, and processes
- `C3` civic decision-making and deliberation
- `C4` constitutional principles and public policy
- `C5` comparative government and civic action
- `C6` advanced civic argument, policy design, and participation

### G. Geography And Human-Environment Systems

- `G1` place, maps, and environmental features
- `G2` human-environment interaction and regions
- `G3` spatial patterns, movement, and resources
- `G4` geographic reasoning with maps, data, and scale
- `G5` global interdependence, migration, and sustainability
- `G6` geospatial analysis and policy application

### E. Economics And Exchange Systems

- `E1` needs, wants, choice, and exchange
- `E2` production, consumption, and local markets
- `E3` incentives, trade, and simple systems
- `E4` markets, labor, and public resource decisions
- `E5` macroeconomic patterns, global trade, and finance basics
- `E6` economic analysis, policy tradeoffs, and capstone modeling

### A. Advanced Secondary Synthesis And Public Argument

- `A1` interdisciplinary research and evidence synthesis
- `A2` capstone argument, modeling, and public communication

## Initial Implementation Priority

The first wave should start where the domain actually begins:

1. `Q1` observe, describe, and question
2. `Q2` compare, classify, and sequence evidence
3. `H1` chronology, identity, and community memory
4. `C1` community rules, roles, and fairness
5. `G1` place, maps, and environmental features
6. `E1` needs, wants, choice, and exchange

## Claim Boundary

This spine defines dependency shape and implementation priority only. It does not mean the nodes are fully specified, reviewed, or ready for classroom release.
