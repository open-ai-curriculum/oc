# Social Studies Parity Report

## Purpose

Summarize the current parity posture of the social studies domain relative to the mathematics domain.

## Overall Judgment

Social studies has now reached broad structural and operational parity with mathematics.

It has not yet reached full governance and assurance parity.

## What Is Now At Parity

- domain topology and node-count footprint
- standards package scaffolding and state-profile surface
- external-framework registry surface
- maintained external framework profiles for every AP-facing social studies overlay family
- ready-for-mapping public-framework audit posture for the strongest two AP-facing surfaces
- standards-augmented graph artifacts
- assessment overlay package
- bounded graph-cut package surfaces
- assessment-specific focused graph cuts for all maintained AP-facing social studies overlay families
- Gephi, Neo4j, and operational-graph exports
- artifact registry and runtime slice surfaces
- build and expansion planning documents
- cross-branch integration and external assessment scan documents

## What Is Not Yet At Parity

- stronger official external standards crosswalk depth
- course-specific refinement in the most compressed AP-facing history and economics areas
- stronger official blueprint-level or identifier-level codification depth
- higher-resolution history and economics topology where current overlays remain intentionally compressed

## Current Parity Posture By Category

| Category | Judgment |
| --- | --- |
| Structural parity | Strong |
| Standards-authority parity | Moderate and improving |
| Standards-augmented graph parity | Strong at repository-governed level |
| Operational export and overlay parity | Strong |
| Governance and assurance parity | Strong and improving |

## Final Claim Boundary

The repository can now responsibly claim that social studies has broad parity with mathematics at the level of graph architecture, standards scaffolding, external-framework registries, maintained AP-facing framework profiles, a limited ready-for-mapping audit tier, overlays, runtime/attachment surfaces, and exportability.

It cannot yet responsibly claim parity in official external standards completeness or course-specific graph resolution in the most compressed history and economics areas.
