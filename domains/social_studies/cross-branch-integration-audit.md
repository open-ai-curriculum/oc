# Cross-Branch Integration Audit

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the implemented social studies graph has enough cross-branch integration to function as a coherent executable knowledge graph rather than as parallel history, civics, geography, and economics tracks.

## Audit Standard

An integration surface is considered healthy when:

1. the graph names the interaction explicitly rather than relying on inference
2. the dependency logic reflects real disciplinary coordination
3. the interaction appears at the level where it becomes instructionally important

## Overall Judgment

The social studies graph is strong enough to support the claim that it is one governed K-12 domain rather than a loose collection of course silos.

The remaining integration issues are no longer about missing branch adjacency. They are about refinement depth inside already-explicit bridges.

## Integration Surfaces Reviewed

### 1. Inquiry To Every Discipline

Judgment: strong

Relevant nodes:

- `Q1` through `Q6`
- `H1` through `H6`
- `C1` through `C6`
- `G1` through `G6`
- `E1` through `E6`

Assessment:

- inquiry is modeled as a true upstream spine rather than a parallel skill strand
- the branch families repeatedly depend on inquiry nodes at meaningful stages

Result:

- no further action required for baseline parity

## Integration Findings

### Finding A

The graph has enough explicit cross-branch dependency structure to support the claim that social studies is modeled as one executable domain.

### Finding B

The remaining thin areas are concentrated in:

- course-specific history detail
- United States and comparative-government institutional detail
- macroeconomics and microeconomics distinction depth

These are refinement opportunities, not unresolved integration blockers.

### Finding C

The runtime and artifact expansion materially improved integration confidence by showing that branch bridges are not just declared in topology but also used in operational slices.

## Final Judgment

The dedicated cross-branch integration audit supports treating the current social studies graph as:

- broadly coherent at the K-12 domain level
- operationally integrated enough for standards, assessment, runtime, and export use
- still improvable in course-specific richness without being structurally blocked
