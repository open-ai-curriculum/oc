# AP United States History Focused Graph

## Purpose

Define the bounded social studies graph cut that most directly supports the current AP United States History overlay.

This package isolates the current history-facing assessment surface so chronology, sourcing, argument, and related cross-branch dependencies can be governed and inspected as one bounded view.

## Focus Rule

This is a bounded overlay-supporting history cut, not a separate AP course graph. It remains governed by the canonical social studies graph and preserves the repository's current compression boundaries.

## Included Core Nodes

### History Backbone

- `H1` chronology, identity, and community memory
- `H2` historical sources and past-present comparison
- `H3` causation, context, and change over time
- `H4` sourcing, corroboration, and historical argument
- `H5` thematic historical analysis and periodization
- `H6` historiography, public history, and capstone argument

### Shared Inquiry And Argument Support

- `Q4` investigation design, representation, and data use
- `Q5` evaluation of sources, models, and claims
- `Q6` synthesis, argument, and public communication

### Cross-Branch Social Context

- `C2` institutions, rights, responsibilities, and processes
- `C3` civic decision-making and deliberation
- `C4` constitutional principles and public policy
- `C5` comparative government and civic action
- `G3` spatial patterns, movement, and resources
- `G4` geographic reasoning with maps, data, and scale
- `G5` global interdependence, migration, and sustainability
- `E3` incentives, trade, and simple systems
- `E4` markets, labor, and public resource decisions

### Advanced Synthesis Bridge

- `A1` interdisciplinary research and evidence synthesis
- `A2` capstone argument, modeling, and public communication

## Why These Nodes Are In Scope

This cut covers:

- chronology and change over time
- source use, corroboration, and argument
- government, society, migration, reform, and economic context
- public communication of historical claims

## Deliberately Out Of Scope

This cut does not attempt to model:

- United States-specific chronology as first-class fine-grained course-era nodes
- branch-specific institution detail beyond what already exists in the canonical civics branch

## Main Current Strength

The graph already models historical reasoning, sourcing, causation, and argument strongly enough to support bounded assessment analysis.

## Main Current Limitation

Course-specific chronology and institution detail remain compressed, so this cut supports coverage and topology work more than reviewed course-equivalence claims.

## Claim Boundary

This focused graph documents the current history-facing assessment slice in the repository. It does not establish official blueprint verification or exam-preparation approval.
