# K-12 Expansion Architecture

## Purpose

Describe how the social studies domain should continue expanding while preserving a single governed graph from kindergarten through Grade 12.

## Core Rule

New nodes, standards overlays, artifact layers, and delivery views should extend the canonical graph rather than create parallel course maps disconnected from the dependency model.

## Target Scope

The target domain is one continuous graph covering:

- kindergarten and elementary inquiry foundations
- upper-elementary and middle-school social studies reasoning
- secondary history, civics, geography, and economics analysis
- advanced interdisciplinary synthesis and public communication

## Expansion Priorities

1. deepen the first inquiry and branch-entry nodes into full node packages
2. add middle-school bridges where explanation, causation, policy, and spatial reasoning intensify
3. harden secondary cross-branch dependencies before adding strand-specific delivery views
4. add standards overlays only after the family-level graph and claim boundaries are human-reviewed
5. add domain-specific validators and export checks once machine-readable overlays exist

## Architectural Guardrails

- preserve shared inquiry nodes as the upstream backbone
- preserve dependency-sensitive mastery logic
- preserve explicit claim boundaries where standards or review status remain unresolved
- preserve cross-branch links among history, civics, geography, and economics
- do not collapse language-access issues into content-mastery claims

## Claim Boundary

This document defines expansion intent only. It does not authorize unsupported standards claims, reviewed curriculum claims, or learner-impacting deployment.
