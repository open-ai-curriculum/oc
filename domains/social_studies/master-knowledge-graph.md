# Social Studies Master Knowledge Graph

## Purpose

This document is the human-readable view of the canonical `social_studies` master knowledge graph.

## Graph Rule

The graph starts with shared inquiry and evidence capacities, then branches into history, civics, geography, and economics, and finally recombines into advanced interdisciplinary synthesis and public communication.

## Family Overview

### Q. Inquiry, Evidence, And Discourse

This shared upstream branch governs how learners notice, question, compare, explain, investigate, evaluate, and communicate across the social studies strands.

### H. History And Source Reasoning

This branch governs chronology, historical-source interpretation, causation, corroboration, periodization, and historiographic synthesis.

### C. Civics And Collective Decision-Making

This branch governs rules, institutions, rights, responsibilities, policy reasoning, and civic participation.

### G. Geography And Human-Environment Systems

This branch governs place, maps, regions, movement, spatial patterns, and geospatial reasoning.

### E. Economics And Exchange Systems

This branch governs needs and wants, markets, incentives, tradeoffs, labor, and macroeconomic basics.

### A. Advanced Secondary Synthesis And Public Argument

This branch recombines the secondary disciplinary branches into interdisciplinary research, capstone modeling, and public argument.

## Initial Implementation Posture

All nodes in this graph are currently `seeded`. That means the repository has a canonical dependency structure, but not yet the reviewed node-package depth required for classroom-ready release claims.

## Claim Boundary

This graph defines topology and intended coverage only. It does not claim reviewed standards mappings, validated assessments, or completed node execution packages.
