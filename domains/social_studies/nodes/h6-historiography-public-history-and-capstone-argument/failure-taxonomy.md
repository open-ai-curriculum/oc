# H6 Failure Taxonomy

### H6-FM-01 Interpretation Comparison Collapse

- description: learner treats competing historical interpretations as isolated summaries and cannot compare their reasoning or evidentiary basis

### H6-FM-02 Public-History Medium Blindness

- description: learner presents historical claims without accounting for how audience medium or public purpose changes the form of the argument

### H6-FM-03 Capstone Claim Without Historiographic Position

- description: learner assembles evidence but does not situate the capstone argument within a defensible interpretive position

### H6-FM-04 Interpretation-Density Or Public-History Vocabulary Risk

- description: learner performance may be limited by dense interpretive language or public-history vocabulary rather than by the underlying historical reasoning alone
