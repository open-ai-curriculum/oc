# Q1 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `Q1` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For Q1-FM-01 Observation Omission

Primary interventions:

- use teacher-guided noticing with one feature at a time
- highlight or point to relevant areas before releasing responsibility
- contrast missed details with correctly noticed details

Expected effect:

- learner attends more reliably to salient accessible features

Reverification target:

- one direct observation item

### For Q1-FM-02 Guess Over Observation

Primary interventions:

- separate "what we can see" from "what we think" in a simple T-chart
- model sentence stems for observation versus inference
- revisit the same source with explicit evidence language

Expected effect:

- learner distinguishes direct observation from unsupported guesswork

Reverification target:

- one meaning-gate item

### For Q1-FM-03 Question Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- provide question stems tied to observed details
- ask the learner to point to the feature that prompted the question
- compare connected versus disconnected questions

Expected effect:

- learner asks questions anchored to the source or situation

Reverification target:

- one direct item and one transfer item

### For Q1-FM-04 Access Or Language Barrier Risk

Primary interventions:

- reduce language load and increase response-mode options
- verify that the learner can access the source visually, aurally, or tactually
- re-administer with simplified directions or interpreted support where appropriate

Expected effect:

- task signal better reflects inquiry capability rather than avoidable access barriers

Reverification target:

- one re-administered direct item with adjusted supports

## Escalation Rule

If Q1 remains `blocked` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate for human review of language access, sensory access, and task-fit assumptions rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.
