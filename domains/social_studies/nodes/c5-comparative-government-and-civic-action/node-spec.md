# C5 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `C5`
- name: `comparative_government_and_civic_action`
- domain: `social_studies`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `comparative-civics-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner compares civic systems, explains how institutions shape participation, and evaluates bounded options for civic action.

## Dependencies

- `C4` constitutional principles and public policy
- `G4` geographic reasoning with maps, data, and scale
- `Q5` evaluation of sources, models, and claims

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `C5` only when all of the following are true:

1. compares how at least two civic systems or structures shape participation or power
2. matches a civic action to the relevant institutional pathway or context
3. explains why institutional differences matter for the problem under study
4. succeeds in changed civic-system or action contexts
5. remains stable on delayed recheck

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.85`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `14`
