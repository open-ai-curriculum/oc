# Social Studies Downstream Expansion Plan

## Purpose

Define the next coherent downstream refinement frontier beyond the current social studies scaffold, overlays, exports, and bounded runtime slices.

## Current Downstream Frontier

The graph already reaches:

- advanced disciplinary analysis through `H6`, `C6`, `G6`, and `E6`
- interdisciplinary synthesis through `A1`
- public-facing capstone communication through `A2`

The next downstream work should not add disconnected course labels. It should refine the territory that current overlays and audits show is still compressed.

## Next Downstream Refinement Branches

### 1. Course-Specific History Refinement

Refine next:

- United States history chronology and institutional development
- world-history periodization and regional comparison
- public-history and historiography distinctions inside `H5` and `H6`

Rationale:

- AP United States History and AP World History overlays are currently substantial but compressed
- the present history branch is broad enough for parity scaffolding but not yet specific enough for stronger course-level claims

### 2. Government And Comparative Politics Refinement

Refine next:

- branch-level and court-level institutional distinctions inside `C4` and `C5`
- comparative-government country-case or system-case overlays
- policy-design and civic-action distinctions inside `C6`

Rationale:

- AP United States Government and AP Comparative Government overlays show real coverage but still compress important institutional distinctions

### 3. Geography And Economics Thematic Refinement

Refine next:

- AP Human Geography theme-specific secondary slices
- explicit macroeconomics and microeconomics sub-branches inside `E5` and `E6`
- stronger geography-economics crossover surfaces in global systems work

Rationale:

- the graph can already support assessment overlays, but economics and geography course specificity remains compressed

## Sequencing Recommendation

Recommended refinement order:

1. history course-specific refinement
2. government/comparative politics refinement
3. economics macro/micro refinement
4. geography thematic refinement

## Why This Order

- the current assessment overlays show the clearest compression pressure in history and government
- economics and geography are operationally usable now, but course-level parity would benefit from later decomposition

## First Recommended Target

The strongest first downstream refinement target is:

- secondary history refinement around `H4`, `H5`, and `H6`

Why:

- it improves both AP United States History and AP World History overlays at once
- it strengthens the most visible current compression area in the social studies graph
