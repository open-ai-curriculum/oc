# Reading-Writing Cross-Branch Coverage Review

## Purpose

Assess whether the current ELA/literacy graph keeps reading, writing, oral language, and language control connected as one governed system.

## Overall Judgment

The graph currently behaves as one integrated literacy system rather than as disconnected reading and writing strands.

## Evidence

- foundational reading depends on oral-language and print-entry conditions
- writing depends on language control and reading-response surfaces
- advanced rhetoric and literary argument depend on both reading and writing branches
- disciplinary literacy and research writing remain integrated with analysis and revision

## Remaining Risk

Future expansion could still introduce drift if:

- standards hardening is done strand-by-strand without dependency checks
- course-level nodes are added without preserving cross-branch links
- artifact attachments overfit to one strand and stop attaching node-primarily

## Claim Boundary

This review records structural cross-branch coherence only. It does not certify instructional sequencing or classroom outcomes.
