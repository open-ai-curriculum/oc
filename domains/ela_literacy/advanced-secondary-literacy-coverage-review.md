# Advanced Secondary Literacy Coverage Review

## Purpose

Assess whether the current ELA/literacy graph is broad enough at the advanced-secondary frontier to support rhetoric, synthesis, research writing, and literary argument as a legitimate capstone layer.

## Overall Judgment

The current advanced-secondary frontier is structurally legitimate because it includes:

- disciplinary literacy and cross-text synthesis
- research synthesis and citation control
- rhetorical analysis and synthesis
- literary interpretation and intertextual argument

## Strengths

- advanced nodes are not isolated from upstream reading, writing, and language dependencies
- rhetorical and literary capstones are differentiated
- research writing is present as a distinct bridge rather than hidden inside generic writing

## Remaining Gap

The advanced layer is still compressed relative to the likely long-term domain target.

Future graph-fidelity expansion may need to separate:

- disciplinary literacy from broader rhetorical synthesis
- argument from research orchestration
- advanced literary comparison from broader theory or interpretive frameworks

## Claim Boundary

This is a structural coverage judgment only. It does not claim reviewed course-level standards alignment or classroom-ready secondary literacy sequences.
