# Early Literacy Frontier Structural Audit

## Purpose

Record the structural state of the first maintained ELA/literacy frontier from oral-language readiness through literacy-entry decoding, comprehension, and sentence production.

## Current Structural Position

- oral-language entry nodes are present and operationalized
- print and symbol-control nodes are present and operationalized
- foundational decoding and encoding nodes are present and operationalized
- early comprehension, language, and writing bridges are present and operationalized

## Structural Finding

The early-literacy frontier is no longer a seed-only scaffold. It now has:

- explicit graph dependencies
- node execution contracts
- learner-state schemas
- intervention maps
- transition rules

## Remaining Structural Gap

No domain-specific validator currently inspects ELA/literacy package completeness the way mathematics is inspected indirectly through the current repository validator.

## Claim Boundary

This audit records repository structure only. It does not certify instructional validity, classroom readiness, or reviewed standards alignment.
