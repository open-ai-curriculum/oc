# Foundational Literacy Peer Coverage Review

## Purpose

Assess whether the current ELA/literacy master graph is comprehensive for the full literacy territory from pre-K entry through the peer level of the current sentence-level literacy frontier.

The authoritative sources reviewed here are:

- [master-knowledge-graph.md](master-knowledge-graph.md)
- [master-knowledge-graph.yaml](master-knowledge-graph.yaml)
- [pre-k-to-sentence-level-literacy-peer-graph.md](pre-k-to-sentence-level-literacy-peer-graph.md)

## Overall Judgment

The graph is now materially comprehensive as a governed early-literacy territory map from pre-K readiness through the peer level of sentence-level reading and writing.

What is already strong:

- oral-language readiness is explicit rather than hidden
- print access and symbol control are present
- foundational decoding and encoding are present
- literal and sentence-level comprehension are present
- first sentence and idea-capture writing surfaces are present
- language-control dependencies are present rather than ignored

## Coverage Assessment By Branch

### 1. Oral-Language Access

Assessment: strong

The `O` family covers:

- shared attention and response readiness
- oral-language comprehension for routines
- recount and sequence language

This is an appropriate literacy-entry floor.

### 2. Print And Symbol Control

Assessment: strong

The `P` family covers:

- print awareness
- text tracking
- phonological awareness
- alphabet knowledge and letter formation

This is enough to make formal foundational reading claims dependency-aware.

### 3. Foundational Reading

Assessment: strong

The `F` family covers:

- alphabetic principle
- simple-word decoding and encoding
- connected-text fluency

This is a legitimate literacy-entry reading spine.

### 4. Early Comprehension

Assessment: adequate and coherent

The current graph includes:

- `R1` literal comprehension of shared text
- `R2` sentence-level comprehension of supported text

That is enough for the current peer-level boundary, though later inferential granularity may still need expansion.

### 5. Early Writing And Language

Assessment: strong

The current graph includes:

- `W1` idea capture
- `W2` sentence generation and transcription
- `L1` sentence formation and basic conventions

That prevents early literacy from collapsing into reading-only structure.

## Remaining Edge Conditions

The major remaining issue at this frontier is not missing branch presence, but possible future granularity expansion between:

- decoding and fluency
- literal comprehension and broader meaning-making
- sentence formation and sentence writing execution

## Recommended Judgment

If the question is:

"Is the current graph already a comprehensive pre-K through sentence-level literacy peer graph?"

The answer is:

- yes, at the current boundary

If the question is:

"Is this the final granularity required for long-term literacy architecture?"

The answer is:

- no; later graph-fidelity expansion is still likely warranted

## Claim Boundary

This review is a structural coverage judgment only. It does not certify reviewed standards alignment or classroom-ready instructional completeness.
