# Cross-Branch Integration Audit

## Purpose

Check whether major ELA/literacy branches still behave as one governed literacy system rather than drifting into disconnected reading, writing, or language subgraphs.

## Cross-Branch Findings

- reading remains connected to oral-language and foundational access dependencies
- writing remains connected to reading-response and language-control dependencies
- advanced nodes remain dependent on upstream reading, writing, and language branches
- disciplinary literacy and research writing are modeled as synthesis layers, not isolated endcaps

## Integration Strengths

- no advanced-writing node bypasses reading-analysis prerequisites
- no advanced-reading node bypasses language or composition dependencies where they materially matter
- foundational access nodes still unlock later reading and writing surfaces explicitly

## Monitoring Need

Future expansion should continue checking that:

- state standards work does not fragment the canonical graph
- artifact attachments remain node-primary
- course-level surfaces do not bypass the dependency model

## Claim Boundary

This audit is structural and architectural. It does not certify curriculum quality or instructional sequencing validity.
