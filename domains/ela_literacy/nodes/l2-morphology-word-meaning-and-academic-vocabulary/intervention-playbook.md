# L2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `L2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For L2-FM-01 Context-Blind Word Meaning

Primary interventions:

- require the learner to interpret the word within a full sentence, not in isolation only
- contrast two sentence contexts using the same or similar word
- ask what in the surrounding text helped determine meaning

Expected effect:

- learner ties word meaning to context rather than recall alone

Reverification target:

- one context-based word-meaning task

### For L2-FM-02 Morphology Cue Miss

Primary interventions:

- highlight one useful prefix, suffix, or root at a time
- connect the word part to a familiar word family
- keep morphology as a meaning-support tool rather than a memorization list

Expected effect:

- learner notices and uses available word-part clues to support meaning

Reverification target:

- one morphology-supported meaning task

### For L2-FM-03 Receptive-Expressive Split

Primary interventions:

- pair comprehension of the word with one short production use
- provide a sentence stem that requires meaningful use of the target word
- keep expressive demand bounded while preserving semantic accuracy

Expected effect:

- learner carries vocabulary knowledge from recognition into use

Reverification target:

- one interpret-and-use vocabulary task

### For L2-FM-04 Vocabulary-Transfer Fragility

Primary interventions:

- rotate the target vocabulary into changed texts and prompts
- preserve the semantic target while varying topic or surface format
- avoid overfitting to one practiced passage

Expected effect:

- learner generalizes morphology and vocabulary control beyond one task set

Reverification target:

- one changed-context vocabulary task

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of sentence-level comprehension sufficiency, text-background mismatch, and whether morphology instruction is outrunning the learner's decoding and context-processing capacity.
