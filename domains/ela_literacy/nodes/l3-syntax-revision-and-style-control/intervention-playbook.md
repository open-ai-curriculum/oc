# L3 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `L3` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For L3-FM-01 Surface-Edit Only

Primary interventions:

- compare an error-corrected sentence with a meaning-improved revision
- require one revision that improves clarity, not just correctness
- use before-and-after examples to make revision purpose visible

Expected effect:

- learner shifts from correction-only editing toward meaningful revision

Reverification target:

- one syntax-or-style improvement task

### For L3-FM-02 Purpose-And-Audience Blindness

Primary interventions:

- state the audience or purpose before revising
- compare two revision choices for different rhetorical effects
- ask which wording best fits the intended task

Expected effect:

- learner makes revision choices that fit audience and purpose more deliberately

Reverification target:

- one audience-sensitive revision task

### For L3-FM-03 Sentence-Revision Instability

Primary interventions:

- move from one-sentence revision to a short passage with two or three revision targets
- keep revision focus stable across multiple opportunities
- identify whether breakdown occurs in noticing, planning, or executing the revision

Expected effect:

- learner sustains revision control beyond a single isolated sentence

Reverification target:

- one short multi-sentence revision task

### For L3-FM-04 Vocabulary-And-Syntax Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- simplify the revision target to one sentence where diction and syntax must work together
- compare a precise but awkward version against a clear and precise version
- revise for clarity first, then style refinement second

Expected effect:

- learner coordinates word choice and syntax to improve clarity and effect together

Reverification target:

- one clarity-plus-style revision task

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of whether `L1` and `L2` are actually secure enough, and whether the revision demand exceeds the intended `L3` boundary.
