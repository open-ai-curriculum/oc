# W5 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `W5` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For W5-FM-01 Source-Accumulation Without Synthesis

Primary interventions:

- require one claim that integrates more than one source
- organize notes by idea rather than by source order
- use a writing frame that makes synthesis moves explicit

Expected effect:

- learner integrates sources into coherent written reasoning instead of stacking notes

Reverification target:

- one short synthesis-writing task

### For W5-FM-02 Attribution-Or-Citation Drift

Primary interventions:

- make attribution visible during drafting rather than only at the end
- use one source at a time to stabilize source boundaries before mixing them
- check whether the reader can tell where ideas or evidence came from

Expected effect:

- learner maintains clearer attribution and more stable citation control

Reverification target:

- one attribution-controlled writing task

### For W5-FM-03 Copying-Or-Paraphrase Instability

Primary interventions:

- compare copied language, weak paraphrase, and genuine synthesis
- require a source-free restatement of the idea before drafting
- keep the synthesis target bounded to one claim or paragraph at a time

Expected effect:

- learner moves from copied or patchwritten material toward controlled synthesis

Reverification target:

- one paraphrase-versus-synthesis writing task

### For W5-FM-04 Question-And-Source Transfer Fragility

Primary interventions:

- rotate to changed research questions with the same synthesis frame
- vary source sets while preserving a stable writing routine
- reduce novelty only enough to observe transfer cleanly

Expected effect:

- learner generalizes research synthesis beyond one question or source set

Reverification target:

- one changed-question or changed-source-set writing task

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of whether `W4`, `R6`, and `L3` are actually secure enough, and whether the research-writing demand exceeds the intended `W5` boundary.
