# A2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `A2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For A2-FM-01 Summary-For-Interpretation Substitution

Primary interventions:

- contrast literary summary with interpretive argument
- require one interpretive claim after one cited textual moment
- keep the interpretive target bounded before expanding into full essay-length response

Expected effect:

- learner builds sustained literary interpretation rather than summary or reaction alone

Reverification target:

- one short advanced literary-analysis prompt

### For A2-FM-02 Evidence-And-Interpretation Link Weakness

Primary interventions:

- pair each interpretive claim with one textual detail and one reasoning step
- model how literary evidence supports a reading before fading support
- keep the interpretive focus limited to one literary problem at a time

Expected effect:

- learner connects literary interpretation to evidence and reasoning more clearly

Reverification target:

- one claim-evidence-reasoning literary task

### For A2-FM-03 Intertextual Comparison Blur

Primary interventions:

- map each text's contribution before combining them into argument
- require the learner to preserve distinctions in theme, voice, or interpretation
- use a two-text comparison frame before broader intertextual synthesis

Expected effect:

- learner compares and synthesizes literary texts without flattening their differences

Reverification target:

- one two-text intertextual argument task

### For A2-FM-04 Literary-Task Transfer Fragility

Primary interventions:

- rotate to changed literary texts or interpretive tasks with the same argument frame
- vary complexity gradually rather than changing all dimensions at once
- reduce novelty only enough to observe transfer cleanly

Expected effect:

- learner generalizes advanced literary interpretation beyond one familiar text or task

Reverification target:

- one changed-text or changed-task literary-analysis prompt

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of whether `W4`, `R4`, and `L3` are actually secure enough, and whether the advanced literary-analysis demand exceeds the intended `A2` boundary.
