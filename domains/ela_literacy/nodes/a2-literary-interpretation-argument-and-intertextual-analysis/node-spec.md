# A2 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `A2`
- name: `literary_interpretation_argument_and_intertextual_analysis`
- domain: `ela_literacy`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `advanced-literary-analysis-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner constructs sustained literary interpretation and intertextual argument well enough to establish the advanced-secondary capstone branch for literary analysis.

## Dependencies

- `W4` multi-paragraph explanation, narrative, and argument
- `R4` literary structure, theme, and character analysis
- `L3` syntax revision and style control

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `A2` only when all of the following are true:

1. develops an interpretive literary claim beyond summary
2. supports literary interpretation with relevant textual evidence and reasoning
3. compares texts, themes, or interpretations where the task requires intertextual argument
4. succeeds across changed literary texts or interpretive tasks
5. remains stable on delayed recheck

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.85`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- develop sustained literary interpretations
- compare texts or interpretations in an intertextual frame
- support advanced literary argument in writing or discussion
- operate at the capstone literary-analysis frontier of the literacy graph

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
