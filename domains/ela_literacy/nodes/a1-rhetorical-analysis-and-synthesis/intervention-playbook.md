# A1 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `A1` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For A1-FM-01 Summary-And-Assertion Substitution

Primary interventions:

- contrast rhetorical analysis with summary or unsupported argument
- require one identified rhetorical move and one explanation of its effect
- keep the analysis target bounded to one source passage before full synthesis

Expected effect:

- learner grounds response in rhetorical analysis rather than summary or assertion alone

Reverification target:

- one short rhetorical-analysis task

### For A1-FM-02 Rhetorical-Choice Interpretation Weakness

Primary interventions:

- isolate one rhetorical choice and ask what it is doing for the audience or argument
- compare two passages with different rhetorical effects
- reduce the number of rhetorical features under discussion at once

Expected effect:

- learner explains the significance of rhetorical choices more convincingly

Reverification target:

- one rhetorical-choice effect task

### For A1-FM-03 Complex-Source Synthesis Drift

Primary interventions:

- map claims, perspectives, and evidence across sources before drafting
- require the learner to preserve differences among sources, not flatten them
- keep the synthesis claim bounded to one analytical focus

Expected effect:

- learner synthesizes complex source material while preserving distinctions meaningfully

Reverification target:

- one bounded complex-source synthesis task

### For A1-FM-04 Situation-And-Source Transfer Fragility

Primary interventions:

- rotate to changed rhetorical situations or source sets with the same analysis frame
- vary complexity gradually rather than changing all dimensions at once
- reduce novelty only enough to observe transfer cleanly

Expected effect:

- learner generalizes advanced rhetorical analysis beyond one familiar source situation

Reverification target:

- one changed-context rhetorical-analysis task

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of whether `W5`, `R6`, and `L3` are actually secure enough, and whether the advanced rhetorical-analysis demand exceeds the intended `A1` boundary.
