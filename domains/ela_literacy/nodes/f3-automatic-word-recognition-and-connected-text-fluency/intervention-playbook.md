# F3 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `F3` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For F3-FM-01 Labored-Word-By-Word Reading

Primary interventions:

- use brief repeated reading on highly controlled connected text
- pre-correct a small number of likely blocking words before the read
- keep the passage short enough that automaticity can build without fatigue collapse

Expected effect:

- learner increases connected-text automaticity while preserving accuracy

Reverification target:

- one short passage reread with meaning check

### For F3-FM-02 Tracking Instability

Primary interventions:

- use line focus or finger tracking temporarily to stabilize eye movement through text
- reduce layout complexity while preserving a true connected-text surface
- separate tracking failure from decoding failure

Expected effect:

- learner maintains place and line progression through the passage

Reverification target:

- one changed-line tracking passage

### For F3-FM-03 Rate-Over-Meaning Tradeoff

Primary interventions:

- slow the reading rate slightly and insert meaning checkpoints
- reinforce accuracy and phrasing over speed alone
- compare two reads to show when pace destroys meaning

Expected effect:

- learner balances automaticity with accuracy and comprehension

Reverification target:

- one fluency read with embedded meaning checks

### For F3-FM-04 Text-Set Fragility

Primary interventions:

- rotate to new but similarly controlled passages
- vary layout or topic while holding decoding demands within the supported band
- prevent overfitting to one memorized passage

Expected effect:

- learner transfers connected-text reading to changed texts and layouts

Reverification target:

- one changed-passage fluency task

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains blocking after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to human review of whether the real bottleneck remains `F2` or `P2`, and whether fluency demands exceed the learner's controlled text band.
