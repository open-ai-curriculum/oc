# FR5 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `FR5` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For FR5-FM-01 Slow Accurate Decoding Without Consolidation

Primary interventions:

- rehearse a bounded set of already-decodable items for faster accurate recognition
- contrast effortful first pass with later recognition
- avoid introducing too many new items at once

Expected effect:

- learner begins consolidating known words and patterns into quicker accurate recognition

Reverification target:

- one repeated mixed-list recognition task

### For FR5-FM-02 Mixed-List Recognition Collapse

Primary interventions:

- mix consolidated and recently learned items in a short list
- use distributed review rather than massed repetition alone
- track which items remain unstable across spacing

Expected effect:

- learner maintains recognition when familiar items appear in mixed conditions

Reverification target:

- one mixed-list transfer task

### For FR5-FM-03 Accuracy-Speed Tradeoff

Primary interventions:

- slow the pace until accurate recognition stabilizes
- emphasize full-word integrity before speed
- compare accurate slower responses with inaccurate faster ones

Expected effect:

- learner improves speed without abandoning accuracy

Reverification target:

- one paced accuracy-first recognition task

### For FR5-FM-04 Fragile Pattern Recognition

Primary interventions:

- group words by shared transferable pattern
- contrast pattern-consistent and pattern-breaking examples
- verify that recognition extends beyond a few memorized words

Expected effect:

- learner consolidates pattern-level recognition, not isolated words only

Reverification target:

- one pattern-generalization recognition task

## Escalation Rule

If automaticity remains narrow or accuracy-speed tradeoffs worsen after two intervention cycles, escalate to human review.
