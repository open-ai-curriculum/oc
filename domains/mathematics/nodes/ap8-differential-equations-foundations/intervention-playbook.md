# Intervention Playbook

## `accumulation_without_rate_link`

- compare rate graphs and accumulation stories so learners must explain what is being accumulated and in what units
- use bounded net-change routines that connect small increments of rate to total change over an interval
- require learners to state what the integral is measuring before computing or estimating it

## `fundamental_theorem_connection_breakdown`

- compare accumulation functions and their rate graphs to show how one encodes change in the other
- use routines that move deliberately among rate, accumulated amount, and antiderivative notation
- require learners to explain why differentiating an accumulation function recovers the original rate in bounded contexts

## `signed_area_orientation_confusion`

- compare above-axis and below-axis regions in the same graph and ask what each contributes to net change versus total amount
- use signed-area and total-amount contrast routines before any formal evaluation
- require learners to justify whether negative contributions should remain negative or be converted to absolute value in context

## `antiderivative_procedure_without_context`

- compare contexts that require exact antiderivative evaluation with contexts that require accumulated meaning from graphs or tables
- use decision routines that ask whether the task is about net change, total accumulation, or recovering a function from a rate
- require learners to name the contextual quantity before selecting an integral procedure
