# B2 Node Specification

- id: `B2`

- domain: `mathematics`

## Purpose

The learner interprets rational, radical, and exponential functions as distinct structured families, connects symbolic form to domain and behavior constraints, and reasons about growth, decay, and asymptotic or restricted behavior rather than treating each family as unrelated procedures.

## Dependencies

- `B1`
- `J1`
- `K1`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. distinguishes rational, radical, and exponential functions by structure, domain, and graphical behavior in bounded cases
2. explains and uses restrictions such as excluded values, nonnegative radicands, and multiplicative growth or decay
3. solves and interprets bounded equations or comparisons involving these function families with coherent structural reasoning
4. connects symbolic form to graphical features such as asymptotes, intercept behavior, end behavior, and domain boundaries
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.82`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- identify the family and key structural constraints of bounded rational, radical, and exponential functions
- solve bounded equations and comparisons while preserving domain restrictions and family meaning
- explain why asymptotes, domain cutoffs, or multiplicative growth arise from the symbolic structure
- compare representations to justify function-family-specific behavior in algebraic and graphical form
