# G5 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `G5`
- name: `symmetry_and_equal_part_visual_reasoning`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner recognizes lines of symmetry and visually reasons about equal parts in shapes.

## Dependencies

- `G1`
- `U4`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `G5` only when all of the following are true:

1. identifies whether a simple figure has a line of symmetry
2. determines whether a partition creates equal visual parts
3. explains why a fold, line, or split does or does not preserve symmetry or equal parts
4. succeeds across paper-folding, drawings, and simple partition tasks
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `14`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- identify a vertical or horizontal line of symmetry in simple shapes
- reject partitions that create unequal parts even if they look superficially balanced
- connect equal-part reasoning to visual shape structure
- explain where a shape would fold to match itself

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
