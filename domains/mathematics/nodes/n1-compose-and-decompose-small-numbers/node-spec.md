# N1 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `N1`
- name: `compose_and_decompose_small_numbers`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner understands that a number can be made of parts and that the same whole can be decomposed in multiple valid ways.

## Dependencies

- `C3`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `N1` only when all of the following are true:

1. composes a whole from smaller parts
2. decomposes a whole into smaller parts in more than one way
3. explains that the total stays the same when the parts are rearranged
4. succeeds across object, drawing, and story-based forms
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `14`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- show that `5` can be made from `2 and 3` or `4 and 1`
- move between a whole and its parts with counters, drawings, or oral descriptions
- explain that the whole remains constant while the grouping changes
- connect number decomposition to simple joining or separating situations

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
