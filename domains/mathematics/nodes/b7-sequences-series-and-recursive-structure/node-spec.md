# B7 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `B7`
- name: `sequences_series_and_recursive_structure`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner reasons about arithmetic, geometric, and recursively defined sequences together with introductory series so repeated change, accumulation, and recurrence become explicit mathematical structures rather than side topics inside algebra.

## Dependencies

- `B1`
- `B5`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. distinguishes arithmetic, geometric, and recursive sequence structure in bounded symbolic, tabular, graphical, and contextual forms
2. writes and interprets explicit and recursive rules for bounded sequences and explains how terms are generated
3. evaluates and interprets finite arithmetic and geometric series in bounded settings, including contextual accumulation cases
4. connects sequence and series structure to related function-family behavior without collapsing recurrence into one-step formulas
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.8`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- classify bounded sequence situations as arithmetic, geometric, recursive, or non-matching and justify the classification
- generate terms and rules for explicit and recursive sequences and translate between those forms when appropriate
- compute and interpret bounded finite arithmetic and geometric series in symbolic and contextual cases
- explain how sequence or recurrence structure connects to broader function behavior and accumulation


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
