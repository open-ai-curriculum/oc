# W4 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `W4`
- name: `probability_models_and_compound_events`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner interprets probability as a model for uncertain events, reasons about compound events and conditional structure, and compares theoretical and experimental outcomes rather than treating chance as guesswork.

## Dependencies

- `D6`
- `F2`
- `Q1`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. explains and computes probabilities of simple and compound events in bounded sample spaces
2. uses lists, tables, tree diagrams, and area or set models to represent compound-event structure
3. distinguishes independent and dependent events and interprets conditional probability in introductory form
4. compares theoretical and experimental probabilities and explains reasonable variation across repeated trials
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.84`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- compute and interpret probabilities for simple and compound events in bounded sample spaces
- represent compound events with organized models such as tables, trees, or set diagrams
- explain independence, dependence, and introductory conditional probability in context
- compare experimental results to theoretical models and explain variation without overreacting to short-run noise


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
