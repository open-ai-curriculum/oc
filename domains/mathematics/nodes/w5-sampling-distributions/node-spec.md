# W5 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `W5`
- name: `sampling_distributions`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner interprets sampling distributions and standard error as probability-based descriptions of statistic variability, so inferential methods rest on a coherent model of repeated sampling rather than on memorized procedures.

## Dependencies

- `W1`
- `W4`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. explains how repeated random sampling produces a distribution of sample statistics and why that distribution matters for inference
2. interprets center, spread, and approximate shape of bounded sampling distributions for proportions or means in standard settings
3. reasons about the effect of sample size on standard error and sampling variability without losing the probabilistic meaning
4. connects observed sample statistics to expected sampling-distribution behavior when evaluating evidence strength
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.83`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- explain why sample statistics vary across repeated samples and how sampling distributions model that variation
- interpret bounded sampling-distribution summaries for proportions or means in words and notation
- compare how larger or smaller sample sizes change inferential stability through standard error
- connect an observed statistic to what would be expected under repeated sampling


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
