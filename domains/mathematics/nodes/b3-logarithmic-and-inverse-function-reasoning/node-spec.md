## Purpose

The learner interprets logarithms as inverse exponential reasoning, connects inverse-function structure to algebraic and graphical behavior, and uses logarithmic representations to solve and interpret bounded advanced-function problems rather than memorizing isolated rules.

## Dependencies

- `B2`
- `K1`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. explains logarithms as inverse relationships to exponentials and interprets inverse-function structure in bounded cases
2. rewrites and solves bounded exponential and logarithmic equations using structurally valid transformations
3. connects logarithmic and exponential graphs through inverse relationships, intercepts, domain/range, and asymptotic behavior
4. interprets logarithmic expressions and scales in bounded mathematical or contextual settings with coherent meaning
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.82`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- explain inverse relationships between exponential and logarithmic forms in bounded cases
- solve bounded exponential and logarithmic equations while preserving inverse-function meaning
- compare logarithmic and exponential graphs and identify reflected or inverse-linked structure
- interpret logarithmic quantities or scales with coherent algebraic and contextual meaning
