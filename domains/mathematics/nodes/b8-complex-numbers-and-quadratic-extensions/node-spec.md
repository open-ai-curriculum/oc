# B8 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `B8`
- name: `complex_numbers_and_quadratic_extensions`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner extends quadratic and polynomial reasoning beyond the real-number system by using imaginary and complex numbers to interpret non-real roots, preserve algebraic structure, and explain why some equations require an expanded number system.

## Dependencies

- `K7`
- `B1`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. explains the role of the imaginary unit and performs bounded operations with complex numbers in standard forms
2. interprets why some quadratic and polynomial equations have non-real roots and represents those solutions with valid notation
3. solves bounded quadratic equations that require complex-number outputs and checks the solutions by substitution or structural reasoning
4. connects factor structure, conjugate structure, and polynomial roots when extending algebra beyond the real numbers
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.82`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- perform bounded addition, subtraction, multiplication, and simple powers with complex numbers
- solve bounded quadratic equations with non-real roots and represent the solutions correctly
- explain why conjugate structures arise in polynomial contexts with real coefficients
- connect quadratic or polynomial structure to the need for complex-number extensions


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
