# Intervention Playbook

## `representation_structure_disconnect`

- translate one quadratic relationship across area model, expression, table, and graph in a fixed sequence
- anchor each translation with matched values and visible symmetry cues
- compare correct and drifted quadratic representations side by side

## `feature_meaning_collapse`

- label vertex, axis of symmetry, and zeros directly on graphs and connect them to contexts
- use teacher think-alouds that explain maximum/minimum and intercept meaning before formula language
- compare quadratics with shared features and one changing feature at a time

## `zero_solution_confusion`

- connect factor form to zero-product meaning with explicit substitution checks
- verify candidate solutions in the original equation after each solve attempt
- compare roots, x-intercepts, and factor pairs in matched examples

## `linear_overgeneralization`

- compare linear and quadratic tables using first-difference and shape checks
- use bounded geometric and contextual examples where quadratic growth is visible
- require learners to state why a relationship is quadratic rather than only naming it
