# M5 Intervention Playbook

### M5-FM-01 Transitive Chain Breakdown

- build three-object comparison chains with visible placeholders
- require restating each relation before drawing the final conclusion

### M5-FM-02 Inference Justification Weakness

- use sentence frames such as "If A is longer than B and B is longer than C, then..."
- contrast correct and incorrect conclusions and ask which follows from the evidence

### M5-FM-03 Shared-Reference Confusion

- practice comparing two objects through the same benchmark object
- use side-by-side shared-reference diagrams before verbal-only tasks

### M5-FM-04 Language-Chain Drift

- reduce linguistic load and rebuild the chain with symbols or arrows
- move gradually from visual chains to spoken-only chains
