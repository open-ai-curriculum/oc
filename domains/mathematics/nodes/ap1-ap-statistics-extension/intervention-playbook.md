# Intervention Playbook

## `evidence_chain_fragmentation`

- map each statistical claim to the design, sample, variability, and probability facts that support it
- compare complete and broken AP-style statistical arguments to identify the missing link
- require learners to restate a conclusion only after naming all evidence components it depends on

## `chance_evidence_opacity`

- compare results that are plausibly due to chance with results that are less plausible under the model
- use teacher think-alouds that distinguish “unlikely under chance” from “certainly true”
- require learners to explain what the probability statement is actually about before concluding

## `inferential_overreach_from_weak_design`

- compare strong experimental designs, random samples, convenience samples, and observational studies on the same question
- require learners to name the strongest claim the design can actually support
- use matched examples where identical numerical evidence leads to different conclusions because the design differs

## `model_claim_misalignment`

- compare two plausible statistical models or summaries and ask which one actually addresses the question
- require learners to state what claim is being evaluated before selecting a model or test logic
- use side-by-side examples where the same data supports one claim but not another
