# AP1 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `AP1`
- name: `ap_statistics_extension`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner synthesizes statistical distributions, association, study design, inference, and probability into a coherent AP Statistics-level reasoning system, using probabilistic and inferential logic to justify claims rather than treating procedures as disconnected units.

## Dependencies

- `W2`
- `W3`
- `W4`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. integrates descriptive statistics, probability, and inference to justify claims about populations and processes in bounded AP-style contexts
2. evaluates sampling, design, variability, and significance logic together rather than as isolated checklist items
3. uses probabilistic models to interpret sampling distributions, chance variation, and evidence strength in bounded cases
4. critiques statistical arguments for design weakness, unsupported causality, weak evidence, or misinterpreted probability
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.79`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- integrate distribution, association, design, and probability evidence in one bounded inferential argument
- explain how chance variation and study design interact when interpreting evidence strength
- compare competing statistical explanations and identify the strongest supported conclusion
- critique AP-style statistical claims for misuse of probability, bias, or inferential overreach


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
