# R2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `R2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For R2-FM-01 Part Selection Drift

Primary interventions:

- trace or shade a single equal part in multiple models
- ask the learner to identify the whole before selecting one part
- use one-part-of-the-whole sentence frames

Expected effect:

- learner stabilizes one-part selection relative to the whole

Reverification target:

- one direct interpretation item and one transfer item

### For R2-FM-02 Whole Count Substitution

Primary interventions:

- contrast counting pieces with naming one equal part
- use "one out of how many equal parts?" prompts
- revisit `R1` equal-part and whole reference tasks

Expected effect:

- learner connects piece count to whole structure rather than just enumerating pieces

Reverification target:

- one direct item and one explanation item

### For R2-FM-03 Denominator Magnitude Reversal

Primary interventions:

- compare the same whole partitioned into different counts
- overlay or stack one-half, one-third, and one-fourth models
- require prediction before observing the visual model

Expected effect:

- learner understands that more equal parts of the same whole produce smaller unit parts

Reverification target:

- one comparison item and one error-diagnosis item

### For R2-FM-04 Fair Share Without Unit Meaning

Primary interventions:

- connect fair-sharing stories back to area or set models
- ask learners to label each share as one equal part of the whole
- use teacher modeling that links fairness language to unit language explicitly

Expected effect:

- learner ties intuitive fairness to formal unit-part meaning

Reverification target:

- one transfer item with explanation

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to dependency review and inspect upstream node stability rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when attention, language, confidence, or context suggests a different explanation of the learner state.
