# M3 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `M3`
- name: `unit_size_matters`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner understands that the number of units used in a measurement depends on how large each unit is, and that more units can mean a smaller unit rather than a greater quantity.

## Dependencies

- `M2`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `M3` only when all of the following are true:

1. compares measurements made with different-sized units correctly
2. explains why smaller units produce larger counts for the same quantity
3. rejects count-only interpretations of measurement results
4. succeeds across length, area, amount, or partition-adjacent contexts
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `14`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- explain why a desk measured with paper clips gives a larger count than with blocks
- compare same-size wholes partitioned into different numbers of parts
- connect smaller unit size to larger unit count
- avoid assuming a larger count always means a larger quantity

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
