# H1 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `H1`
- name: `transformations_and_coordinate_symmetry`
- domain: `mathematics`
- support tier: `Core release`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `upstream-readiness-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner interprets translations, rotations, reflections, and basic coordinate symmetry as rigid motions that preserve shape and size, and uses those motions to reason about congruence and image/preimage relationships.

## Dependencies

- `A4`
- `G5`
- `G6`


## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on this node only when all of the following are true:

1. identifies and performs translations, rotations, and reflections in bounded geometric and coordinate settings
2. explains which properties are preserved under rigid transformations and why congruence is maintained
3. interprets line symmetry and simple coordinate symmetry through image/preimage reasoning
4. represents and verifies image rules, orientation changes, and congruent correspondence in bounded cases
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing


## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.86`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- perform and verify rigid transformations on grids or coordinate planes in bounded cases
- explain which lengths, angles, and orientation properties are preserved or changed by a given transformation
- identify corresponding points and figures under coordinate symmetry or rigid motion
- justify congruence claims through transformation sequences rather than visual resemblance alone


## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
