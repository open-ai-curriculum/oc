# Pre-Fraction Frontier Completeness Review

## Purpose

Assess whether the current mathematics graph represents the comprehensive knowledge required for a learner from pre-kindergarten through the peer level of the repository's fraction spine segment.

This review stays within the leading graph edge defined by:

- all prerequisite mathematics junior to the fraction branch
- all major peer branches that normally coexist with the `F1` through `F6/F5` fraction territory
- no new claims about downstream post-fraction mathematics

## Review Standard

This review separates three questions:

1. Is the territory present?
2. Is the branch internally coherent?
3. Is the dependency structure final enough to trust as executable?

## Overall Judgment

The graph is now comprehensive at the branch-territory level for pre-kindergarten through the peer level of the fraction spine segment.

It is not yet the final executable graph at the structural-quality level.

That distinction matters:

- the graph is no longer missing major junior or peer-level math territory
- the remaining issues are narrower dependency and integration issues, not broad missing-branch failures
