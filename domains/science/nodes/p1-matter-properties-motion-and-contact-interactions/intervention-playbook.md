# P1 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `P1` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For P1-FM-01 Property Observation Instability

Primary interventions:

- contrast two materials with one clear property difference
- use touch and observation routines with property word banks
- sort objects by one property at a time

Expected effect:

- learner identifies observable properties more reliably

Reverification target:

- one property item

### For P1-FM-02 Motion Change Misread

Primary interventions:

- replay the event slowly and name what changed
- compare before-and-after positions or speeds
- use movement arrows or simple diagrams

Expected effect:

- learner notices motion changes more accurately

Reverification target:

- one motion item

### For P1-FM-03 Interaction-Cause Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- pair a visible push or pull with the resulting motion change
- ask what touched what just before the motion changed
- contrast contact and no-contact examples

Expected effect:

- learner connects a contact interaction to the motion outcome

Reverification target:

- one interaction explanation item

### For P1-FM-04 Hidden-Cause Or Fantasy Drift

Primary interventions:

- separate observed physical evidence from intention-based explanations
- ask what was actually seen happen
- compare a science explanation with a pretend explanation

Expected effect:

- learner grounds physical-science claims in observation rather than fantasy

Reverification target:

- one error-diagnosis item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for language load, sensory access, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when motor access, language access, or response-mode constraints suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
