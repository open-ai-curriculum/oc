# T1 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `T1`
- name: `weather_sky_patterns_and_local_earth_materials`
- domain: `science`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `science-entry-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner notices recurring weather and sky observations and identifies observable properties of local Earth materials such as rock, soil, sand, or water in familiar contexts.

## Dependencies

- `Q1` observation, noticing, and descriptive comparison
- `E1` observation-based explanation and evidence talk

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `T1` only when all of the following are true:

1. describes a simple weather or sky observation accurately
2. notices a recurring pattern such as sunny versus rainy days or day-night sky differences
3. identifies observable properties of local Earth materials
4. succeeds across more than one weather, sky, or material context
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `21`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- state what the weather or sky looks like using direct observation
- notice repeated patterns over time
- compare local Earth materials by visible or touchable properties
- avoid unsupported hidden-cause claims when describing early Earth/space phenomena

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
