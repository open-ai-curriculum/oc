# T1 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `T1` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For T1-FM-01 Weather Observation Instability

Primary interventions:

- use daily weather routines with one observation at a time
- separate what is seen from what is predicted
- compare two sky images and name one direct observation for each

Expected effect:

- learner describes weather and sky observations more reliably

Reverification target:

- one weather observation item

### For T1-FM-02 Pattern-Over-Time Breakdown

Primary interventions:

- highlight repeated observations on a class chart
- ask what happened again across several days
- use simple icons to mark sunny, cloudy, rainy, or windy conditions

Expected effect:

- learner notices recurring weather or sky patterns more reliably

Reverification target:

- one pattern item

### For T1-FM-03 Earth-Material Property Confusion

Primary interventions:

- compare two local materials side by side
- use touch and observation routines to name one property at a time
- sort materials by a single observable property

Expected effect:

- learner compares Earth materials more accurately by observable properties

Reverification target:

- one materials item

### For T1-FM-04 Hidden-Cause Or Fantasy Drift

Primary interventions:

- separate observed evidence from imagined weather causes
- ask what the chart image or sky actually shows
- compare a science explanation with a pretend explanation

Expected effect:

- learner grounds Earth/space claims in observation rather than fantasy

Reverification target:

- one error-diagnosis item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for language load, limited observation opportunity, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when local context, weather access, language access, or response-mode constraints suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
