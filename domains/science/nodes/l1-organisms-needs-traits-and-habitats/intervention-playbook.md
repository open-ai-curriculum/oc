# L1 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `L1` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For L1-FM-01 Needs Misclassification

Primary interventions:

- sort living and nonliving needs with picture cards
- match an organism to water food air light or shelter where appropriate
- contrast true needs with wants or decorations

Expected effect:

- learner identifies basic organism needs more reliably

Reverification target:

- one needs item

### For L1-FM-02 Trait Observation Instability

Primary interventions:

- focus attention on one observable body feature at a time
- use labeled images and prompt "what do you notice"
- compare two organisms and name one visible trait for each

Expected effect:

- learner describes observable traits using direct evidence

Reverification target:

- one trait item

### For L1-FM-03 Habitat-Function Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- pair habitat features with the need they support
- ask how this place helps the organism live
- use concrete examples such as nests, ponds, shade, or soil

Expected effect:

- learner connects habitat features to organism needs more reliably

Reverification target:

- one habitat explanation item

### For L1-FM-04 Anthropomorphic Or Fantasy Drift

Primary interventions:

- separate observed evidence from made-up or human-like explanations
- ask what was actually seen in the image or environment
- compare a science explanation with a pretend explanation

Expected effect:

- learner grounds life-science claims in evidence rather than fantasy

Reverification target:

- one error-diagnosis item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for language load, background-knowledge mismatch, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when cultural context, language access, or limited observation opportunities suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
