# I3 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `I3` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For I3-FM-01 Repetition-Value Blindness

Primary interventions:

- compare one-trial and repeated-trial examples side by side
- ask what more trials help us see
- use matched investigations with different numbers of repeats

Expected effect:

- learner values repeated trials more appropriately

Reverification target:

- one repeated-trials item

### For I3-FM-02 Reliability Misjudgment

Primary interventions:

- sort consistent and inconsistent result sets
- highlight the range of outcomes across trials
- ask whether one unusual trial changes the whole pattern or needs checking

Expected effect:

- learner judges reliability more accurately

Reverification target:

- one reliability item

### For I3-FM-03 Pattern-Overstatement

Primary interventions:

- rewrite overly strong pattern claims into bounded summaries
- compare what the data supports versus what it does not yet show
- use sentence frames such as "the trials suggest..."

Expected effect:

- learner summarizes patterns with more appropriate caution

Reverification target:

- one pattern-summary item

### For I3-FM-04 Context Boundary

Primary interventions:

- repeat reliability routines across life, physical, and Earth/space investigations
- preserve the data-summary structure while changing the phenomenon
- fade scaffolds after one successful familiar example

Expected effect:

- learner transfers repeated-trials reasoning across contexts

Reverification target:

- one transfer item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for statistical-language load, table-reading demands, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when response-mode constraints, reading load, or local investigation conditions suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
