# C5 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `C5`
- name: `thermodynamics_electrochemistry_and_chemical_change_systems`
- domain: `science`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `science-secondary-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner models energetic feasibility, redox change, and electrochemical systems quantitatively without treating energy labels or cell diagrams as self-explanatory.

## Dependencies

- `C4`
- `P6`
- `M4`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `C5` only when all of the following are true:

1. uses thermodynamic reasoning to discuss feasibility and system energy change in bounded cases
2. explains oxidation-reduction and electrochemical behavior with particle and system models
3. connects chemical energy change to measurable electrochemical outcomes
4. succeeds across more than one advanced chemistry context
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `42`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- distinguish energy release from spontaneity or usefulness claims
- explain redox changes with electron-transfer reasoning
- connect electrochemical setup, directionality, and measurement meaning
- avoid treating formal notation as complete explanation by itself

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
