# D3 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `D3` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For D3-FM-01 System-Failure Narrowing

Primary interventions:

- map the system into interacting parts before analyzing failure
- ask what else changed when the failure appeared
- compare part-only and system-level failure explanations

Expected effect:

- learner analyzes failure more systemically

Reverification target:

- one failure-analysis item

### For D3-FM-02 Tradeoff Misjudgment

Primary interventions:

- use tradeoff tables with "helps" and "hurts" columns
- compare two competing design improvements explicitly
- ask what got better and what got worse

Expected effect:

- learner judges tradeoffs more accurately

Reverification target:

- one tradeoff item

### For D3-FM-03 Optimization Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- check each optimization against the whole system goals and evidence
- compare evidence-based and evidence-ignoring optimization moves
- require a because-statement tied to system data

Expected effect:

- learner proposes more system-aware optimizations

Reverification target:

- one optimization item

### For D3-FM-04 Context Boundary

Primary interventions:

- repeat systems-design routines across transport, shelter, and filtration contexts
- preserve the systems-optimization structure while changing the problem
- fade scaffolds after one successful familiar example

Expected effect:

- learner transfers systems-design optimization across contexts

Reverification target:

- one transfer item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for abstraction load, reading load, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when an unconventional optimization remains system-defensible, or when design constraints and response-mode demands suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
