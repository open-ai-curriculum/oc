# T2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `T2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For T2-FM-01 Surface-Change Misread

Primary interventions:

- compare before-and-after land or soil images
- ask what moved, changed, or was carried away
- use simple arrows or labels on the changed surface

Expected effect:

- learner identifies visible Earth-surface changes more reliably

Reverification target:

- one surface-change item

### For T2-FM-02 Sky-Pattern Instability

Primary interventions:

- chart sun or moon observations across several days
- ask what repeated and what changed
- use simple sequence images of the same sky event over time

Expected effect:

- learner notices recurring sky patterns more reliably

Reverification target:

- one sky-pattern item

### For T2-FM-03 Water-Movement Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- trace where water starts, moves, and collects in a simple diagram
- ask what direction the water went
- compare flowing, soaking, and collecting examples

Expected effect:

- learner explains simple water movement more reliably

Reverification target:

- one water-movement item

### For T2-FM-04 Hidden-Cause Or Fantasy Drift

Primary interventions:

- separate observed Earth/space evidence from imagined causes
- ask what was actually seen in the chart, image, or local example
- compare a science explanation with a pretend explanation

Expected effect:

- learner grounds Earth/space claims in evidence rather than fantasy

Reverification target:

- one error-diagnosis item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for observation access, language load, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when local environment access, seasonal timing, or response-mode constraints suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
