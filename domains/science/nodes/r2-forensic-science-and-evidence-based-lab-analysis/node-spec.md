# R2 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `R2`
- name: `forensic_science_and_evidence_based_lab_analysis`
- domain: `science`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `science-secondary-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner applies biological, chemical, and investigative reasoning to forensic evidence problems without treating forensic conclusions as certain because they appear technical.

## Dependencies

- `R1`
- `B1`
- `C3`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `R2` only when all of the following are true:

1. uses biological, chemical, and lab evidence to evaluate bounded forensic claims
2. distinguishes evidence interpretation from certainty or narrative pressure
3. connects laboratory method quality to the strength of a forensic conclusion
4. succeeds across more than one advanced forensic-science context
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `42`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- interpret forensic evidence with explicit method and contamination awareness
- separate evidentiary support from case narrative appeal
- use chemistry and biology reasoning to evaluate lab findings appropriately
- avoid overclaiming identification or causation from limited evidence

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
