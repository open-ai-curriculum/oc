# T5 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `T5`
- name: `geologic_time_climate_systems_and_human_impact_models`
- domain: `science`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `science-secondary-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner explains long-term Earth-system change using geologic time, climate-system models, and bounded human-impact reasoning without collapsing timescales or overclaiming causation.

## Dependencies

- `T4` plate processes, water cycle, and space system interactions
- `M4` conservation, cycle, and energy flow models
- `E3` model-based argument, revision, and source comparison

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `T5` only when all of the following are true:

1. uses geologic time appropriately when explaining Earth history or long-duration change
2. explains climate systems as interacting long-term processes rather than isolated weather events
3. critiques or explains human impacts using bounded evidence and system reasoning
4. succeeds across more than one advanced Earth-system context
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `42`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- distinguish geologic and climate timescales from short-term observations
- coordinate atmosphere, ocean, land, and biosphere interactions in climate explanations
- explain how human activities can alter Earth systems without simplifying every change to one cause
- compare Earth-system explanations against models and available evidence

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
