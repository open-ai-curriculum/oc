# P2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `P2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For P2-FM-01 Matter-Change Misread

Primary interventions:

- compare before-and-after material states directly
- ask what stayed the same and what changed
- use images or observations from the same event in sequence

Expected effect:

- learner identifies matter changes more reliably

Reverification target:

- one matter-change item

### For P2-FM-02 Energy-Evidence Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- pair each energy example with an observable effect such as warming, lighting, or sound
- ask what evidence shows energy moved
- compare evidence-based and unsupported energy statements

Expected effect:

- learner connects energy claims to observable evidence more reliably

Reverification target:

- one energy-evidence item

### For P2-FM-03 Force-Effect Disconnect

Primary interventions:

- trace a visible force event and the resulting motion or change
- use arrows or short before/after diagrams
- ask what happened right after the force acted

Expected effect:

- learner connects force effects to observed outcomes more reliably

Reverification target:

- one force-effect item

### For P2-FM-04 Hidden-Cause Or Fantasy Drift

Primary interventions:

- separate observed physical evidence from intention-based explanations
- ask what was actually seen happen in the system
- compare a science explanation with a pretend explanation

Expected effect:

- learner grounds physical-science claims in evidence rather than fantasy

Reverification target:

- one error-diagnosis item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for language load, sensory access, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when observation access, tool use, or response-mode constraints suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
