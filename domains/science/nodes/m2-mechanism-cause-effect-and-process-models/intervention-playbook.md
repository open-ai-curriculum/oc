# M2 Intervention Playbook

## Purpose

Map `M2` failure modes to bounded intervention responses.

## Intervention Rules

### For M2-FM-01 Process-Step Collapse

Primary interventions:

- sequence process cards from first to later steps
- narrate one step at a time with arrows or frames
- compare a complete process with one missing a step

Expected effect:

- learner sustains process order more reliably

Reverification target:

- one process-sequencing item

### For M2-FM-02 Cause-Effect Reversal Or Blur

Primary interventions:

- label cause and effect explicitly in short examples
- ask what happened first and what followed
- compare a correct and reversed cause-effect pair

Expected effect:

- learner distinguishes causes from effects more accurately

Reverification target:

- one cause-effect item

### For M2-FM-03 Mechanism Omission

Primary interventions:

- ask what happened in the middle, not just at the end
- require a because or then statement between steps
- use simple mechanism diagrams with arrows

Expected effect:

- learner includes the working process rather than just the outcome

Reverification target:

- one mechanism explanation item

### For M2-FM-04 Context Boundary

Primary interventions:

- repeat mechanism routines across life, physical, and Earth/space processes
- preserve the step-cause-effect structure while changing the phenomenon
- fade scaffolds after one successful familiar example

Expected effect:

- learner transfers mechanism reasoning across contexts

Reverification target:

- one transfer item

## Escalation Rule

If the same dominant failure mode remains `blocking` after two bounded intervention cycles, escalate to review for language load, sequencing demands, or prompt-design mismatch rather than intensifying the same local practice blindly.

## Human Review Rule

Interventions may be suggested automatically, but the teacher or human reviewer must remain able to override the recommendation when discourse structure, local curriculum framing, or response-mode constraints suggest a different explanation of the learner state.
