# First Science Node Target

## Purpose

Identify the first concrete node-package wave for the `science` domain.

## Recommendation

The first implementation wave should begin at the true entry point of the science system:

1. `Q1` observation, noticing, and descriptive comparison
2. `Q2` question formation, prediction language, and pattern noticing
3. `I1` fair tests, tool use, and measurement routines
4. `E1` observation-based explanation and evidence talk
5. `L1` organisms, needs, traits, and habitats
6. `P1` matter properties, motion, and contact interactions
7. `T1` weather, sky patterns, and local Earth materials

## Why This Wave

This is the highest-value first build because:

- it starts at the earliest K-2 science access layer rather than assuming investigation readiness
- it creates explicit upstream dependencies for later modeling, argument, and engineering work
- it prevents later life, physical, and Earth/space science claims from bypassing practice readiness
- it establishes the earliest governed learner-state surface for science
- it provides the first honest bridge between repository-native capability modeling and NGSS-informed structure

## Immediate Follow-On Wave

The next wave should deepen the first bridge from observation into stable shared sensemaking and design work:

1. `Q3` phenomenon framing and shared sensemaking routines
2. `I2` variable awareness, data recording, and basic comparison
3. `M1` part-whole structure, function, and simple system models
4. `E2` claim-evidence-reasoning and competing explanations
5. `D1` problem definition, criteria, constraints, and solution sketching
6. `D2` prototype testing, redesign, and tradeoff recording
7. `L2` life cycles, inheritance, and ecosystem relationships
8. `P2` matter change, energy transfer, and observable forces
9. `T2` Earth surface change, solar/lunar patterns, and water movement

## Next Frontier

After the elementary bridge is stable, the next implementation wave should move into middle-grade mechanism and systems work:

1. `I3` repeated trials, data reliability, and pattern summary
2. `M2` mechanism, cause-effect, and process models
3. `M3` scale, proportion, and system interaction models
4. `E3` model-based argument, revision, and source comparison
5. `D3` systems design tradeoffs, optimization, and failure analysis
6. `L3` cells, body systems, and interdependent organism functions
7. `L4` populations, ecosystems, and matter/energy in living systems
8. `P3` particle models, waves, and force interactions
9. `P4` chemical reactions, energy conservation, and wave behavior
10. `T3` climate, resources, Earth-Sun-Moon systems, and map scale
11. `T4` plate processes, water cycle, and space system interactions

## Secondary Frontier

The later implementation wave should deepen quantitative explanation and advanced secondary integration:

1. `I4` multivariable investigation, sampling, and error awareness
2. `I5` quantitative investigation design, uncertainty, and method critique
3. `M4` conservation, cycle, and energy-flow models
4. `M5` field, force, and invisible mechanism models
5. `E4` quantitative explanation, data critique, and uncertainty reasoning
6. `E5` cross-domain synthesis using multiple models and evidence streams
7. `D4` evidence-driven optimization under constraints
8. `L5` genetics, evolution, and homeostasis models
9. `L6` molecular biology, complex ecology, and biological systems integration
10. `P5` fields, atomic interactions, and quantitative conservation models
11. `P6` advanced chemistry, physics, and multiscale physical systems
12. `T5` geologic time, climate systems, and human impact models
13. `T6` Earth-space evolution, planetary systems, and global change synthesis
14. `A1` interdisciplinary phenomenon modeling and scientific synthesis
15. `A2` advanced research design, computational analysis, and engineering-science integration

## Next Phase

Once the first wave begins, priority should shift from breadth to package completeness:

1. science-specific learner-state models
2. verification models that distinguish observation, explanation, transfer, and retention
3. failure taxonomies that separate content gaps from investigation error and language-load effects
4. intervention playbooks tied to diagnosed scientific reasoning breakdowns

## Claim Boundary

This target document defines implementation priority only. It does not mean the listed science nodes are already implemented, classroom-ready, or fully reviewed.
