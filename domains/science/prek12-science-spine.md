# K-12 Science Spine

## Purpose

Define the governed developmental spine for the `science` domain from kindergarten entry through Grade 12 advanced science integration and specialization.

This document establishes the minimum family-level architecture required to model science as one dependency-aware system instead of as disconnected biology, chemistry, physics, and Earth-science lists.

## Design Principle

The repository should model science as an integrated learning system in which:

- learners notice and frame phenomena
- learners investigate and measure with increasing rigor
- learners build and revise models
- learners explain and argue from evidence
- learners connect those practices to life, physical, and Earth/space science ideas
- learners extend that work into engineering design and advanced secondary synthesis
- learners can then branch into explicit upper-secondary course and elective pathways without bypassing the shared scientific-practice spine

The spine therefore preserves both disciplinary progressions and cross-domain reasoning practices.

## NGSS Posture

This spine is informed by the repository's NGSS package and is designed to preserve science's three-dimensional structure:

- science and engineering practices
- disciplinary core ideas
- crosscutting concepts

It does not claim that the current domain already implements official NGSS mappings node by node. That work remains downstream.

## Spine Bands

### A. K-2 Observation And Sensemaking Entry

These nodes establish the earliest scientific learning conditions.

#### Q1. Observation, Noticing, And Descriptive Comparison

- learner notices visible properties, events, and differences
- learner describes simple similarities, differences, and changes over time

#### Q2. Question Formation, Prediction Language, And Pattern Noticing

- learner asks bounded questions about observable phenomena
- learner makes simple predictions and notices repeated patterns

#### I1. Fair Tests, Tool Use, And Measurement Routines

- learner participates in simple tests with basic tools and agreed procedures
- learner begins measuring and recording observable outcomes

#### E1. Observation-Based Explanation And Evidence Talk

- learner explains what happened using observations rather than unsupported guesswork
- learner begins linking claims to directly available evidence

#### L1. Organisms, Needs, Traits, And Habitats

- learner reasons about living things, what they need, and how traits and habitats matter

#### P1. Matter Properties, Motion, And Contact Interactions

- learner reasons about material properties, pushes, pulls, and simple motion effects

#### T1. Weather, Sky Patterns, And Local Earth Materials

- learner notices recurring weather and sky patterns and basic properties of local Earth materials

### B. Grades 3-5 Investigation And Model Strengthening

These nodes strengthen stable investigation, content explanation, and model use.

#### Q3. Phenomenon Framing And Shared Sensemaking Routines

- learner frames a phenomenon for investigation and discussion
- learner distinguishes the focal question from surrounding noise

#### I2. Variable Awareness, Data Recording, And Basic Comparison

- learner tracks relevant variables, records data more systematically, and compares outcomes

#### M1. Part-Whole Structure, Function, And Simple System Models

- learner uses simple models to explain parts, functions, and system relationships

#### E2. Claim-Evidence-Reasoning And Competing Explanations

- learner supports a claim with evidence and begins comparing alternative explanations

#### D1. Problem Definition, Criteria, Constraints, And Solution Sketching

- learner frames design problems with criteria and constraints
- learner proposes and communicates initial solutions

#### D2. Prototype Testing, Redesign, And Tradeoff Recording

- learner tests a prototype, records evidence, and revises a design

#### L2. Life Cycles, Inheritance, And Ecosystem Relationships

- learner connects organism growth, inherited traits, and ecosystem interdependence

#### P2. Matter Change, Energy Transfer, And Observable Forces

- learner reasons about changes in matter, energy movement, and force effects

#### T2. Earth Surface Change, Solar/Lunar Patterns, And Water Movement

- learner explains recurring Earth and sky patterns and surface-change processes

### C. Grades 6-8 Mechanism, Systems, And Multivariable Reasoning

These nodes establish middle-grade structural science reasoning.

#### I3. Repeated Trials, Data Reliability, And Pattern Summary

- learner improves method reliability and summarizes patterns across trials

#### M2. Mechanism, Cause-Effect, And Process Models

- learner explains processes through causal and mechanistic models rather than surface description alone

#### M3. Scale, Proportion, And System Interaction Models

- learner reasons across scales and tracks interactions among system components

#### E3. Model-Based Argument, Revision, And Source Comparison

- learner revises explanations using models and compares evidence sources

#### D3. Systems Design Tradeoffs, Optimization, And Failure Analysis

- learner analyzes why designs fail and how system tradeoffs affect performance

#### L3. Cells, Body Systems, And Interdependent Organism Functions

- learner explains life processes through cells, systems, and coordinated functions

#### L4. Populations, Ecosystems, And Matter/Energy In Living Systems

- learner tracks matter and energy through organisms and ecosystems

#### P3. Particle Models, Waves, And Force Interactions

- learner explains physical phenomena using particle and interaction models

#### P4. Chemical Reactions, Energy Conservation, And Wave Behavior

- learner uses conserved quantities and wave behavior to explain middle-grade physical systems

#### T3. Climate, Resources, Earth-Sun-Moon Systems, And Map Scale

- learner reasons about climate patterns, resource distribution, and system scale

#### T4. Plate Processes, Water Cycle, And Space System Interactions

- learner explains Earth and space processes through system interactions and causal mechanisms

### D. Grades 9-12 Quantitative Explanation And Secondary Integration

These nodes define the advanced secondary science frontier.

#### I4. Multivariable Investigation, Sampling, And Error Awareness

- learner designs more rigorous investigations and recognizes measurement and sampling limits

#### I5. Quantitative Investigation Design, Uncertainty, And Method Critique

- learner critiques methods, quantifies uncertainty, and defends design choices

#### M4. Conservation, Cycle, And Energy-Flow Models

- learner uses formal conservation and cycle models across science domains

#### M5. Field, Force, And Invisible Mechanism Models

- learner models phenomena that depend on non-visible interactions and abstract mechanisms

#### E4. Quantitative Explanation, Data Critique, And Uncertainty Reasoning

- learner explains and critiques scientific claims using quantitative evidence and uncertainty posture

#### E5. Cross-Domain Synthesis Using Multiple Models And Evidence Streams

- learner synthesizes across domains, models, and evidence sources

#### D4. Evidence-Driven Optimization Under Constraints

- learner optimizes engineered solutions using evidence, criteria, and real tradeoffs

#### L5. Genetics, Evolution, And Homeostasis Models

- learner reasons across heredity, adaptation, regulation, and biological stability

#### L6. Molecular Biology, Complex Ecology, And Biological Systems Integration

- learner integrates advanced biological models across scales and systems

#### P5. Fields, Atomic Interactions, And Quantitative Conservation Models

- learner explains advanced physical phenomena using formalized interaction models

#### P6. Advanced Chemistry, Physics, And Multiscale Physical Systems

- learner integrates secondary chemistry and physics reasoning across scales and representations

#### T5. Geologic Time, Climate Systems, And Human Impact Models

- learner reasons about large-scale Earth systems, long time scales, and anthropogenic influence

#### T6. Earth-Space Evolution, Planetary Systems, And Global Change Synthesis

- learner synthesizes advanced Earth and space science models into coherent system explanations

#### A1. Interdisciplinary Phenomenon Modeling And Scientific Synthesis

- learner explains complex phenomena that require multiple science domains at once

#### A2. Advanced Research Design, Computational Analysis, And Engineering-Science Integration

- learner integrates research methods, computational analysis, and engineering-science decision making

### E. Grades 9-12 Course, AP, And Elective Specialization Pathways

These nodes make upper-secondary science coverage explicit enough to support Honors, AP, and elective pathways without forcing all advanced territory into a handful of broad terminal nodes.

#### B1. Biomolecules, Cellular Structure, And Metabolism

- learner explains biological function using biomolecules, organelles, membranes, and energy-transformation models

#### B2. Gene Expression, Inheritance, And Biotechnology

- learner reasons from DNA to trait expression and evaluates biotechnology methods and evidence

#### B3. Evolution, Population Genetics, And Biodiversity

- learner explains evolutionary change using inheritance, variation, population change, and biodiversity evidence

#### B4. Anatomy, Physiology, And System Regulation

- learner models integrated body systems, feedback, regulation, and organism stability

#### B5. Ecology, Field Systems, And Environmental Biology

- learner analyzes population, ecosystem, and field-based biological systems under changing conditions

#### B6. Organismal Specializations, Botany, Zoology, And Marine Biology

- learner extends biological reasoning into plant, animal, and marine organism specialization pathways

#### C1. Atomic Structure, Periodicity, And Quantum Models

- learner explains matter using atomic structure, periodicity, and quantized-energy reasoning

#### C2. Bonding, Structure, And Intermolecular Forces

- learner predicts material behavior from bonding, geometry, polarity, and intermolecular interaction models

#### C3. Stoichiometry, Reaction Quantification, And Solution Chemistry

- learner quantifies chemical change and solution behavior using conservation, ratio, and measurement logic

#### C4. Kinetics, Equilibrium, And Acid-Base Systems

- learner explains reversible chemical systems through rate, equilibrium, and proton-transfer reasoning

#### C5. Thermodynamics, Electrochemistry, And Chemical Change Systems

- learner models energetic feasibility, redox change, and electrochemical systems quantitatively

#### F1. Mechanics, Kinematics, Energy, And Momentum

- learner models motion and interaction using force, energy, and momentum relationships

#### F2. Rotation, Oscillation, Gravitation, And Mechanical Systems

- learner extends mechanics into rotational, orbital, oscillatory, and complex mechanical systems

#### F3. Fluids, Thermodynamics, And Thermal Systems

- learner explains pressure, flow, heat, and thermal-system behavior using physical models

#### F4. Electrostatics, Circuits, And Magnetic Fields

- learner explains electric and magnetic phenomena using field, circuit, and interaction models

#### F5. Waves, Optics, Quantum, And Modern Physics

- learner explains optical, wave, and modern-physics phenomena using multiscale models

#### F6. Calculus-Embedded Physics Modeling

- learner integrates calculus-supported modeling into advanced mechanics and electricity-magnetism reasoning

#### G1. Geology, Geophysics, And Surface Process Systems

- learner explains rocks, Earth materials, tectonics, and surface processes through geologic-system models

#### G2. Atmospheric, Weather, And Climate Dynamics

- learner explains atmospheric circulation, weather systems, and climate dynamics using Earth-system evidence

#### G3. Hydrology, Oceanography, And Marine Earth Systems

- learner models water movement, ocean systems, and coastal or marine Earth interactions

#### G4. Astronomy, Astrophysics, And Cosmic Evolution

- learner explains planetary, stellar, and cosmological phenomena using physical and observational models

#### G5. Environmental Systems, Human Impact, And Sustainability

- learner evaluates environmental systems and human impacts using evidence, tradeoff, and sustainability reasoning

#### G6. Land/Water Use, Agriculture, And Resource Management

- learner applies environmental and biological reasoning to agriculture, land use, water use, and resource decisions

#### R1. Independent Research Design And Scientific Inquiry

- learner proposes and executes sustained scientific inquiry with defended methods, evidence, and interpretation

#### R2. Forensic Science And Evidence-Based Lab Analysis

- learner applies biological, chemical, and investigative reasoning to forensic evidence problems

#### R3. Electronics, Instrumentation, And Embedded Measurement

- learner uses circuits, sensors, and instrumentation in measurement and design settings

#### R4. Computational Science, Data Modeling, And Simulation

- learner uses computational tools and simulation to analyze scientific systems and data

## Minimal Dependency Flow

The current minimum developmental logic is:

`Q1 -> Q2 -> Q3`

`Q2 -> I1 -> I2 -> I3 -> I4 -> I5`

`Q3 -> M1 -> M2 -> M3 -> M4 -> M5`

`Q2 -> E1 -> E2 -> E3 -> E4 -> E5`

`Q2 + E1 -> D1 -> D2 -> D3 -> D4`

`L1 -> L2 -> L3 -> L4 -> L5 -> L6`

`P1 -> P2 -> P3 -> P4 -> P5 -> P6`

`T1 -> T2 -> T3 -> T4 -> T5 -> T6`

`L6 + P6 + T6 + E5 -> A1 -> A2`

`L3 + L5 + P5 + M4 -> B1 -> B2 -> B3`

`B1 + L5 + M4 -> B4`

`L4 + L6 + T5 + I4 -> B5 -> B6`

`P5 + M5 -> C1 -> C2 -> C3 -> C4 -> C5`

`P4 + M4 + I4 -> F1 -> F2`

`F1 + P6 + M4 -> F3`

`P5 + M5 + I4 -> F4 -> F5`

`F2 + F4 + I5 + E5 -> F6`

`T4 + M3 + I3 -> G1`

`T5 + M4 + I4 -> G2`

`G1 + G2 + T6 -> G3`

`T6 + M5 + E4 -> G4`

`T5 + L4 + E4 + D4 -> G5 -> G6`

`A2 + I5 + E5 -> R1 -> R2`

`R1 + F4 + D4 -> R3`

`A2 + M5 + I5 -> R4`

The domain also preserves cross-family dependencies so content mastery does not detach from investigation, explanation, and modeling.

## Grade-Band Continuity

The science domain should preserve continuity across the full span:

- early observation and evidence talk must remain explicit rather than assumed
- later content claims must depend on scientific-practice stability, not only topic exposure
- engineering should remain inside the same governed graph rather than a disconnected enrichment layer
- advanced secondary science should integrate life, physical, and Earth/space reasoning rather than fragment into unrelated course silos
- Honors, AP, and elective science pathways should be explicit graph branches rather than informal labels attached to broad terminal nodes
- computational science may appear as a bridge inside science, but full computer-science course ownership should remain separate from the science core graph

## Initial Implementation Strategy

The first implementation wave should start with the earliest science entry nodes:

1. `Q1` observation, noticing, and descriptive comparison
2. `Q2` question formation, prediction language, and pattern noticing
3. `I1` fair tests, tool use, and measurement routines
4. `E1` observation-based explanation and evidence talk
5. `L1` organisms, needs, traits, and habitats
6. `P1` matter properties, motion, and contact interactions
7. `T1` weather, sky patterns, and local Earth materials

Those nodes give the science domain an upstream-first starting point analogous to the earliest readiness layers already modeled in mathematics and ELA/literacy.
