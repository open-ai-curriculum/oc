# I1 Node Specification

## Node Identity

- id: `I1`
- name: `responsible_participation_in_digital_spaces`
- domain: `computer_science`
- support tier: `Incubation`
- current maturity state: `Drafted`
- supported profile: `computer-science-entry-node-package`
- evidence class: `Synthetic`

## Purpose

The learner follows bounded norms for safe, respectful, and responsible technology use without treating digital participation as consequence-free.

## Dependencies

- `D1`

## Exit Conditions

The learner is secure on `I1` only when all of the following are true:

1. follows simple safety and respect norms in digital activities
2. recognizes that digital actions can affect self and others
3. makes a bounded responsible choice in a familiar digital-space scenario
4. succeeds across more than one classroom technology context
5. passes a delayed recheck after spacing

## Mastery Criteria

- direct accuracy threshold: `0.90`
- explanation required: `true`
- transfer required: `true`
- retention check days: `14`

## Observable Evidence

The learner can:

- identify a safe or respectful action in a simple digital scenario
- describe one reason a digital action has consequences
- distinguish private, shared, and public digital behavior in bounded cases
- follow classroom technology routines without avoidable harm or disruption

## Claim Boundary

This node package is an operational draft and not a classroom-release claim.
